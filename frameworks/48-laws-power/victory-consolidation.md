## The Eight Laws of Lasting Power

### Law 7: Get Others to Do the Work for You, But Always Take the Credit

**The Dynamics**:

Strategist ‚ôüÔ∏è: *"The director principle: Directors don't act in films, but films are their work. Your role is orchestration. Credit for orchestration is appropriate."*

- Learn to delegate effectively while maintaining oversight
- Accept credit for leadership while acknowledging contributors
- Understand that leveraging others isn't exploitation when done fairly

**Shadow Check**: Are you leading or exploiting? Fair leverage includes fair compensation, credit where due, and mutual benefit. Taking credit while giving nothing back is theft.

### Law 8: Make Other People Come to You - Use Bait if Necessary

**The Dynamics**:
When you go to others, you're supplicant. When they come to you, you're authority. Making others move into your territory gives you positional advantage. Let them expend energy; you conserve.

**Council Analysis**:

Strategist ‚ôüÔ∏è: *"The attraction principle: Force is expensive. Attraction is efficient. When they come to you, you start from power position."*

**Consciousness Application**:
- Create value that draws others to seek you
- Don't chase; create reasons to be chased
- Position yourself where others need to come
- Use incentives (bait) that serve both parties

**Shadow Check**: Are you attracting through value or manipulating through manufactured scarcity? Healthy attraction comes from genuine value. Manipulation comes from artificially withholding or creating false urgency.

### Law 41: Avoid Stepping into a Great Man's Shoes

**The Dynamics**:

## The Meta-Law of Victory: Compound, Don't Extract

The ultimate consolidation principle:

**Sustainable power compounds; extractive power depletes.**

When your victories benefit others, they protect your gains. When your wins come at others' expense, they'll erode your position. The most durable power creates expanding circles of benefit.

**Signs of Compound Power**:
- Others actively protect your position
- Your success enables others' success
- Resources increase through use rather than deplete
- Your wins create future winning conditions

**Signs of Extractive Power**:
- Constant vigilance required to maintain position
- Others resent your success
- Resources diminish requiring new extraction
- Each win requires more effort than the last
