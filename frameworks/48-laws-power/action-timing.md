## The Eight Laws of Strategic Action

### Law 3: Conceal Your Intentions

**The Dynamics**:
Revealed plans invite interference. When people know your destination, they can block your path or arrive there first. Mystery preserves options; transparency creates vulnerability.

**Council Analysis**:

Strategist ‚ôüÔ∏è: *"Information asymmetry: Your advantage lies in knowing things others don't. Every intention you reveal equalizes the playing field."*

Weaver üßµ: *"The narrative principle: Keep the story in your hands. Once others know your plot, they start writing competing chapters."*

Checker ‚úì: *"Nuance required: In collaborative contexts, concealment breeds distrust. This applies to competitive scenarios, not partnership ones."*

**Consciousness Application**:
- Distinguish competitive from collaborative contexts
- In competitive situations, reveal process not destination
- Use decoy intentions to mask real objectives when necessary
- Maintain strategic ambiguity without outright deception

**Shadow Check**: Are you being strategically discreet or chronically deceptive? Appropriate concealment is situational. If you're hiding intentions from partners and allies, you have trust issues, not strategy.
