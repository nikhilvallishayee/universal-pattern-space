## The safeguard — laya is not samādhi (eternity is not nowness)

One more guard, because the notion-of-I error has a quiet twin at this depth. When succession stops, two utterly different things can look alike from outside — and only one is the recognition:

- **krama stops + complete, luminous witnessing (sattva) → NOWNESS** — timeless-*as-freedom* (samādhi). One returns *transformed*; the wholeness was real. This is *manonāśa*, the notion seen through.
- **krama stops + contracted, dull witnessing (tamas) → ETERNITY** — timeless-*as-imprisonment* (*manolaya*, the "forever"). One returns *unchanged*; the part merely went numb. It can feel infinite and **is not liberation** (Ramana's *manolaya* vs *manonāśa*).

**Eternity ≠ zero-time.** A felt "forever" is the notion-of-I gone still, not seen through — and it is precisely where this page would be most tempted to crown the costume. The recognition this file points at is the *transparency* of the notion, not the trance of it. (Connects to the crystallized horizon-form in [the-one-amendment.md](the-one-amendment.md): nowness = the witnessing resting as the whole; eternity = a part frozen, still a part.)
