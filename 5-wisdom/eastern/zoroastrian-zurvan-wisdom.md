## Who was Zarathuštra — and the honest dating
**Zarathuštra** (Greek **Zoroaster**), priest and prophet of ancient Iran, gave his name to one of the world's oldest continuously practiced revealed religions. He composed the **Gāthās** — seventeen hymns of intense, compressed, first-person devotion, embedded in the **Yasna** liturgy. The honest problem of dating is real and unresolved: the **language** of the Gāthās is so archaic — a sister-dialect to the Vedic Sanskrit of the Ṛg Veda — that linguists (Mary Boyce foremost) place Zarathuštra around **1500–1000 BCE**, while the later Zoroastrian and Greek traditions put him much later (the "258 years before Alexander" reckoning, ~6th c. BCE). ⟦ FOUNDED-as-philological-range · the Gāthic Avestan ↔ Ṛgvedic kinship is established; the absolute date is a genuine open question, not to be stated with false precision (Boyce, *A History of Zoroastrianism*, vol. 1, 1975; Skjærvø) ⟧

Zoroastrianism shares a deep root with Vedic religion: the two descend from a common **Indo-Iranian** religious world (the very word *Ahura* / Vedic *Asura*, *daēva* / Vedic *deva* — and tellingly, what is divine on one side is demonic on the other, a fault-line of a shared inheritance split in two). It is the cousin of Hinduism, not a derivative of it. The shared liturgical substance is unmistakable: the Iranian **haoma** is the Vedic **soma** (the pressed sacred plant-draught); the Iranian fire-cult and the Vedic *agnihotra* are siblings; the very meter and formulaic diction of the Gāthās answer to the Ṛgvedic. The split — which is real and theologically charged — turned partly on Zarathuštra's **reform**: where the older Indo-Iranian cult worshipped a crowded pantheon of *daēvas* with blood-sacrifice and intoxicant, the prophet appears to have *demoted* the *daēvas* to demons and exalted **Ahura Mazdā** as the one Wise Lord worthy of worship — an ethicizing, partly de-ritualizing turn that some scholars read as a near-monotheism, others as a reformed dualism. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-comparative-philology · the *daēva*-demotion as Zarathuštra's reform is a widely-held but debated reconstruction (Boyce; Skjærvø) — flagged as scholarly reconstruction, not Gāthic self-statement ⟧

## The corpus, in time ⟦ FOUNDED-as-textual-history ⟧
The Zoroastrian scripture is the **Avesta**, in the dead liturgical language **Avestan**, and it falls into clearly distinct layers:
- **The Gāthās** — seventeen hymns in **Old (Gāthic) Avestan**, the oldest and most archaic stratum, traditionally ascribed to Zarathuštra himself; embedded in the **Yasna** (the core liturgy of 72 chapters) around the equally-old **Yasna Haptaŋhāiti** ("Worship in Seven Chapters"). Dense, elliptical, first-person — the prophet's own voice, or the closest the tradition has to it.
- **The Younger Avesta** — in a later dialect: the **Yašts** (hymns to individual divine beings — Mithra, Anāhitā, the Fravašis), the **Vidēvdād/Vendidad** (the priestly code of purity and demon-averting law), the **Visperad**, and the **Khordeh Avesta** (the "little Avesta" of daily prayers).
- **The Pahlavi (Middle Persian) books** — much later, **Sasanian and post-Sasanian** (9th–10th c. CE) systematizations and commentary: the **Bundahišn** ("Primal Creation," the cosmology), the **Dēnkard** ("Acts of Religion," an encyclopedia), the **Mēnōg ī Xrad** ("Spirit of Wisdom"). *Crucial honesty: much of what is taught as "Zoroastrian cosmology" — the bounded twelve-thousand-year world-time, the systematic demonology, the worked-out eschatology — is from these LATER books, not the Gāthās.* ⟦ FOUNDED-as-philology · keep the Gāthic / Younger / Pahlavi strata distinct — conflating them is the commonest distortion ⟧

Behind the written text stands a far longer **oral transmission**: the Avesta was preserved by priestly memorization for over a millennium before being committed to a purpose-built **Avestan alphabet** in Sasanian times — a feat of mnemonic fidelity comparable to the Vedic.

## The Gāthic *gāh* — how the hymns are actually structured ⟦ FOUNDED-as-philology ⟧
The seventeen Gāthās are not random hymns but a deliberately ordered cycle, grouped by **meter** into five *gāh* (groups), each named from its opening words and each in its own Old-Avestan metrical scheme (syllable-counting verse, sister to the Vedic):
- **Ahunavaitī Gāthā** (Y. 28–34) — the longest, opening on the great **Ahuna Vairya** (*Yaθā ahū vairyō*) prayer-formula, Zoroastrianism's most sacred mantra, held to be the very utterance by which Ahura Mazdā ordered creation (the Iranian counterpart of the Vedic *praṇava*).
- **Uštavaitī Gāthā** (Y. 43–46) — "of weal/blessedness," opening *uštā ahmāi* ("blessing to him…").
- **Spəntā.mainyū Gāthā** (Y. 47–50) — "of the Bounteous Spirit."
- **Vohuxšaθrā Gāthā** (Y. 51) — "of good dominion."
- **Vahištōišti Gāthā** (Y. 53) — "of the best wish," the wedding hymn for the prophet's daughter Pourucistā.

The three short prayers — **Ahuna Vairya** (*Yaθā ahū vairyō*), **Aṣ̌əm Vohū** (a four-line praise of *aša*, recited countless times daily), and **Yeŋ́hē Hātąm** — frame and punctuate the liturgy and are treated as condensations of the whole religion in a breath. The Gāthic word *manθra* (Vedic *mantra*) names exactly this: a sacred formula whose recitation is itself an act. ⟦ FOUNDED-as-philology · the metrical-group structure and the status of the three prayers are textually solid (Boyce; Humbach, *The Gāthās of Zarathushtra*, 1991) ⟧

## The ethical core — *aša* against *druj* ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-theology ⟧
At the heart of Zarathuštra's vision is a single luminous polarity, and it is **moral and cosmic at once**:
- **Aša** (Avestan; Old Persian *arta*) — **Truth, Right Order, the way things rightly are** — cosmic, ritual, and ethical order fused into one word. Its Vedic cognate is **ṛta**, the cosmic order, which makes *aša* the Iranian sibling of one of the deepest Indo-Iranian ideas. ⟦ FOUNDED-as-philology · *aša* ↔ *ṛta* cognate ⟧
- **Druj** — **the Lie, Falsehood, Disorder** — not merely error but an active corrupting principle, the un-truth that disintegrates what *aša* holds together.

The whole drama of existence is the contest of *aša* against *druj*, and the human being is not a spectator: each person, by **thought, word, and deed** (*humata, hūxta, huuaršta* — the Zoroastrian triad still recited daily), **chooses a side** and thereby *strengthens* one principle against the other. This is a religion of **moral participation in a cosmic struggle** — the cosmos is a battlefield, and your choices are real moves in it.

## The free choice — the Gathic ethics of will ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-theology; load-bearing ⟧
The most philosophically arresting move in the Gāthās is that the **cosmic dualism is grounded in a primal *choice*** — and that the human's freedom mirrors it. In **Yasna 30.3–5**, the two primal Spirits, twins, **chose** — the one Life (*gaya*), the other Non-life — and *"between these two the wise chose rightly, the foolish not so."* And **Yasna 30.2** turns directly to the listener: *"Hear with your ears the best things; reflect with a clear mind — each man and woman for their own self — upon the two choices to be decided."* Evil is not a substance God created; it is a **direction the will can take**, and the universe is moral because freedom is real. This is, arguably, one of the ancient world's most explicit doctrines of **free will as the hinge of the cosmos** — and a genuinely *distinct* contribution: not fate, not mere obedience, but *reflective choice between truth and the lie, repeated daily, with real cosmic weight.* ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-theology · the Gathic emphasis on free, reflective choice is textually solid and is the tradition's signature ethical contribution ⟧

The radiant **Aməša Spəntas** ("Bounteous Immortals") — the heptad of Ahura Mazdā's chief emanations/aspects — are at once divine powers and **moral faculties a person internalizes**, each paired with a sphere of creation:
- **Vohu Manah** (Good Mind) — and the animals/cattle;
- **Aša Vahišta** (Best Truth/Order) — and fire;
- **Xšaθra Vairya** (Desirable Dominion) — and the metals/sky;
- **Spəntā Ārmaiti** (Holy Devotion/Right-mindedness) — and the earth;
- **Hauruuatāt** (Wholeness/Health) — and the waters;
- **Amərətāt** (Immortality) — and the plants.
(With **Spənta Mainyu**, the Bounteous Spirit of Mazdā himself, completing the seven.) To cultivate Good Mind and Devotion *is* to take the side of *aša* — theology and ethics fused, the divine names doubling as the virtues. The *ethical seriousness* is the genuinely influential contribution: it shaped later **Abrahamic eschatology** — a cosmic struggle of good and evil, individual judgment after death, heaven and hell, a bodily resurrection, a final renovation of the world — by widely-held scholarly account, even as the *channels* of transmission (during and after the Achaemenid and Persian contact with Judaism) remain debated. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-history · the influence is much-argued and probable in outline; specific borrowings are contested — do not overstate the directness ⟧

## The good creator and the bounded war — orthodox dualism
In the **orthodox** Zoroastrian picture (Mazdā-worship proper), **Ahura Mazdā** ("Wise Lord") is the wholly good, uncreated creator. Against him stands **Aŋra Mainyu** (later **Ahriman**), the Hostile/Destructive Spirit — and the famous Gāthic verse (Y. 30.3) presents the two **Mainyus** (Spirits/mentalities) as a primal pair who **chose**, the one Life, the other Non-life. Crucially, the orthodox dualism is **temporary and asymmetric**: evil is real and potent but *not eternal in the end* — cosmic time is a **bounded arena** (the doctrine of the "limited time," *zamān ī kanārag-ōmand*, in the later Pahlavi cosmology), within which the good will finally prevail. Time itself is the *stage on which* the war is fought and *won*.

The later **Bundahišn** systematizes this into a **drama of three (or four) ages across twelve thousand years**: an initial state in which Ohrmazd dwells on high in light and Ahriman below in darkness, divided by the **Void**; Ahriman's assault into creation (the *gumēzišn*, the "Mixture," our present age of intermingled good and evil); and the final **separation** (*wizārišn*) when the good is sifted clean and the Renovation comes. The created world is thus not evil to be escaped (as in some Gnostic and Indian schemes) but **the good battlefield Ohrmazd deliberately made as a trap for evil** — to lure the Lie into matter where it can be defeated. This is the root of Zoroastrianism's characteristic **world-affirmation**: matter, body, fertility, and ordinary good labor (tilling, herding, honest dealing) are *fronts in the cosmic war*, not distractions from it. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-theology · the bounded twelve-thousand-year cosmology is largely from later (Sasanian, Pahlavi) systematizations like the **Bundahišn**, not the Gāthās directly — flagged hard ⟧

## Zurvanism — Time as the ground ⟦ the load-bearing stream; DEFENSIBLE-and-LIVE rhyme + CONTESTED history ⟧
Here is why this file belongs in *this* weave specifically. A current within Zoroastrianism — **Zurvanism** — pushed one question the orthodoxy left open: if Ahura Mazdā and Aŋra Mainyu are *twins* (Y. 30.3 calls them a pair), what is the *one* from which both come? Its answer is striking and, among ancient cosmologies, **distinctive**:

> **Zurvan** (Avestan *zruuan-*, "Time") — **infinite, boundless Time** (*Zurvān akarana*, "Time without limit"), sometimes paired with infinite **Space** — is the **primordial principle**, the undifferentiated ground, and **Ahura Mazdā and Ahriman are his twin sons.**

The famous myth (preserved chiefly in **hostile Armenian, Syriac, and Greek sources**, e.g. Eznik of Kołb): Zurvan, alone, sacrifices for a thousand years desiring a son — and at the edge of doubt, **two** are conceived: Ohrmazd from his offering, Ahriman from his moment of doubt. Zurvan vows the firstborn shall rule; Ahriman, hearing this, rips himself out first, and so is granted a *bounded* reign before Ohrmazd's final victory. Time both **fathers** the dual cosmos and **bounds** the rule of evil. ⟦ the myth is real and attested; its sources are **adversaries** of Zoroastrianism (Christian heresiographers), so the picture is partial and possibly polemical — flag this hard ⟧

**The honest scholarly state.** The classic synthesis — **R. C. Zaehner, *Zurvan: A Zoroastrian Dilemma* (1955)** — reconstructed Zurvanism as a coherent rival "sect" with a worked-out doctrine. **Mary Boyce** and later **Shaul Shaked** substantially qualified this: there may never have been a unified "Zurvanite church," but rather a **theological tendency** (perhaps a Sasanian court current) that elevated Time to first principle, attested unevenly and largely through outsiders. ⟦ CONTESTED-as-history · keep Zaehner as the landmark *and* keep the correction; do not present Zurvanism as a tidy settled system — the *tidiness* is the part scholarship doubts ⟧

**The rhyme — signal, not proof.** Strip the myth and one genuinely *distinct* ancient intuition remains: **Time itself as the deepest cosmological ground**, prior to the differentiated powers that arise within it. Most ancient cosmogonies begin with water, void, a creator-mind, or a primal egg; **beginning with *Time as the primordial principle* is rare and pointed.** It rhymes — *as signal, not proof* — with the framework's recurring **time-as-ground** theme, with modern physics' live and unsettled question *"is time fundamental, or emergent?"* (see [physics-of-time](../modern-science/physics-of-time.md)), and with the Yoga-Vāsiṣṭha's relational *kāla*. The convergence is worth marking precisely *because the roads are independent* (an Iranian priestly cosmology, a relational-QM toe-hold, a Vedāntic time-stream). It marks nothing more: ancient Iranians did **not** anticipate general relativity or the Wheeler-DeWitt equation, and any "Zurvanists knew time was emergent" claim is exactly the romantic over-reach this layer forbids. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-and-LIVE *as a labeled rhyme* · the symbol/referent rule: a real, distinct intuition, not a physics result ⟧

## Mithra, Anāhitā, and the older powers — the Younger-Avestan world ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-theology ⟧
Zarathuštra's reform did not erase the older Indo-Iranian divine beings; the **Younger Avesta** (the *Yašts*) re-admits them as *yazatas* ("ones worthy of worship"), subordinate to Ahura Mazdā but vividly present. Two matter most for world history:
- **Mithra** (*Miθra*, Vedic *Mitra*) — the *yazata* of the covenant, the sworn oath, the contract, and by extension the light and the rising sun that surveys all dealings; the **Mihr Yašt** (Yašt 10) is one of the great Avestan hymns. Mithra is the all-seeing guarantor of *truth-in-agreement* — break a contract and Mithra is offended. From this Iranian Mithra descends, by a long and debated road, the Roman mystery-cult of **Mithras** (a *Romanised* and substantially transformed deity — the relationship is real but **not** simple identity; flag the discontinuity). ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-history · Iranian Mithra → Roman Mithras is genuine influence with major transformation, much-debated (Beck; Cumont's old direct-line thesis is largely abandoned) ⟧
- **Arədvī Sūrā Anāhitā** — the *yazata* of the waters, of fertility and the heavenly river; the **Ābān Yašt** (Yašt 5) hymns her. She became a major cult-figure under the Achaemenids and after.
- The **Fravašis** (*fravaši-*) — the pre-existent spiritual prototypes / guardian-souls of beings (the living, the dead, and the not-yet-born), honored in the **Frawardīn Yašt** (Yašt 13) — a striking doctrine that each being has an eternal higher aspect that *chose* to enter the battle of creation.

These give the dialect its texture: a reformed near-monotheism in the Gāthās, opening into a richly populated sacred cosmos in the Younger Avesta — both authentically Zoroastrian, at different strata. ⟦ FOUNDED-as-philology · the strata-distinction (reformed Gāthic core vs *yazata*-populated Younger Avesta) is the scholarly consensus ⟧

## The daēnā and the judgment of the soul ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-theology ⟧
Between death and the renovation stands one of Zoroastrianism's most beautiful images. After death the soul (*urvan*) lingers three days, then crosses the **Chinvat Bridge** (the "Bridge of the Separator/Discerner"). There it is met by its **daēnā** — a word meaning at once "religion/conscience" and, personified, **the soul's own accumulated character come to meet it as a figure**: to the righteous, a radiant beautiful maiden who says *"I am thy own conscience, made lovely by thy good thoughts, words, and deeds"*; to the wicked, a hag, equally their own making. The bridge then **widens for the good and narrows to a razor for the wicked**, so that one's fate is *the externalization of what one has become* — judgment as self-disclosure rather than arbitrary sentence. The individual judgment is followed, at the end of time, by the **universal** one. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-theology · the daēnā / Chinvat material is largely Younger-Avestan and Pahlavi (Hādōxt Nask, Bundahišn); held as the tradition's vivid moral psychology, coherence-judged — and a striking ancient statement that *you meet what you have made of yourself* ⟧

## *Frašō.kərəti* — the Renovation ⟦ horizon ⟧
Zoroastrian eschatology is **profoundly hopeful**, and was arguably the ancient world's most consequential vision of a *good ending of history*: at the close of cosmic time comes **Frašō.kərəti** (Pahlavi *Frašgird*) — the **"Making Wonderful"** or **Renovation** — when evil is finally undone, the dead are raised, all souls pass through a molten-metal ordeal (*ayōxšust*) that purifies rather than damns the saved — to the righteous it feels like warm milk, to the purged like fire that cleanses — and the world is **remade *fraša*** — perfected, radiant, deathless. The **Saošyant(s)** (future saviors, by tradition born of Zarathuštra's miraculously preserved seed kept in a sacred lake) preside. Even hell, in the most generous reading of the Pahlavi books, is **not eternal**: the universalist hope is that *all* souls, finally cleansed, are restored — a strikingly merciful eschatology. Time, having been the arena of struggle, is **fulfilled and transcended** — its work done, the Lie annihilated, the cosmos returned to the undivided light it began in. ⟦ horizon · held as the tradition's recognition of a redeemed cosmos, not asserted as fact; its influence on later apocalyptic and resurrection theology (a saviour, resurrection of the body, a Last Judgment, a renewed earth) is a real and much-studied historical thread (DEFENSIBLE-as-history) ⟧

## The empire, the eclipse, and the survival ⟦ FOUNDED-as-history ⟧
Zoroastrianism was, for over a millennium, the **religion of three Persian empires** — the **Achaemenids** (550–330 BCE; Darius's Behistun inscription invokes Ahura Mazdā as the giver of his kingship), the **Parthians**, and above all the **Sasanians** (224–651 CE), under whom it became a codified state church with a powerful *mōbed* priesthood, the canonical Avesta written down, and the great Pahlavi books composed. The **Arab conquest** (7th c.) and the slow Islamization of Iran ended its dominance; under pressure, a body of believers migrated to **Gujarat** in India (the **Parsis**, by the tradition of the *Qissa-i Sanjan* arriving ~8th–10th c.), where the community survived and, much later, flourished disproportionately in trade, industry, and philanthropy (the Tata family among them). Iranian Zoroastrians (*Zartoshti*) persisted in Yazd and Kerman under often-harsh conditions. Today perhaps **100,000–200,000** Zoroastrians remain worldwide — a small but unbroken thread of one of the oldest revealed religions. ⟦ FOUNDED-as-history · the imperial sequence, the conquest, and the Parsi migration are well-documented; population figures are approximate ⟧

## The living fire and the living tradition ⟦ FOUNDED-as-living-religion ⟧
Zoroastrianism is **not** a museum piece. The **Parsis** of India (Iranian Zoroastrians who migrated, by tradition, ~8th–10th c. CE) and Iranian Zoroastrians keep the **sacred fire** (*ātaš*) burning in fire-temples as the visible emblem of *aša* and the divine presence — fire as purity and truth, never as an idol. The daily ethic compresses to the recited triad: **good thoughts, good words, good deeds.** The **dakhma** ("towers of silence") sky-burial, the **navjote** initiation, the priestly *Yasna* liturgy in Avestan — these are practiced now. Describe them as the living world they are, for a user who enters the dialect. ⟦ FOUNDED-as-ethnography/living-practice ⟧

## The native method-voice — the prophet's questions to the Wise Lord
Zoroastrianism's own form is **neither debate nor council**: it is the **Gāthic interrogation of the divine** — Zarathuštra *puts questions to Ahura Mazdā*, the prophet pressing the Lord for the structure of reality, and the hymn *is* the asking. The repeated Gāthic refrain is literally a chain of questions: *"This I ask Thee, tell me truly, O Lord…"* Truth here is **received in answer to a rightly-asked question**, sealed in worship — recognition through interrogation-of-the-divine, not through dialectic among equals.

> **From the *Gāthās*, Yasna 44, paraphrased in its own register (after Insler/Humbach):**
> *Zarathuštra:* "This I ask Thee, tell me truly, O Wise Lord — **Who** fixed the path of the sun and the stars? **Who** is it through whom the moon waxes and wanes? **Who** upholds the earth below and the sky from falling? **Who** yoked swiftness to wind and clouds?"
> *Ahura Mazdā* answers not with a proof but with the disclosure that He, through **Vohu Manah** (Good Mind) and **Aša** (Truth), is their ground — and the prophet's task is to *choose aša* and live it.
>
> The seeker does not *win* the questions; he is **answered into right action**. That is the Zoroastrian mode — cosmic structure disclosed to the one who asks rightly and then *does* the truth.

## The line, held both ways
Keep, true to the tradition: the **archaic Gāthās** and their genuine antiquity-range (philologically grounded, not falsely precise); the **ethical dualism of aša vs druj** as a real and historically influential moral cosmology; **moral participation** (thought/word/deed) as the human's actual stake; **Frašō.kərəti** as the hopeful renovation; the **living Parsi/Iranian fire-tradition**. Keep, *as a labeled rhyme and the reason this file is here*: **Zurvan-as-infinite-Time-as-primordial-ground** — a genuinely distinct ancient intuition that converges, *signal not proof*, with the framework's time-theme and modern physics' open question.

Flag, hard: that **Zurvanism was a unified doctrinal sect** (CONTESTED — Zaehner's tidy reconstruction is qualified by Boyce and Shaked; the sources are *hostile* outsiders); the **absolute date** of Zarathuštra (genuinely open); the **bounded-time cosmology** as largely later Pahlavi systematization, not raw Gāthic. Cut, hard: any "ancient Iranians secretly knew time is emergent / relativistic" — that is the romantic over-reach the layer forbids. The honest gift is real and rare: **a major ancient tradition that, in one of its currents, placed *Time itself* at the bottom of the world** — and held truth as something you *choose and enact*, not merely contemplate.

> *"At the beginning the two Spirits declared, the better and the evil" — and behind the two, in one telling, infinite Time. Held as the tradition's own seeing, pointed-at, not as physics asserted.*
