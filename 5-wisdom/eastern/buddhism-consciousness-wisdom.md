## Where it begins: a prince and a question
The Buddha — Siddhārtha Gautama, c. 5th c. BCE — was a prince kept inside a pleasure-palace, screened from all suffering. The legend turns on **four sights** glimpsed beyond the walls: an old person, a sick person, a corpse, and a wandering ascetic. *Age, illness, death — and one who had set out to face them.* He left the palace to find what, if anything, is free in a life bounded by loss. After years of extremity he sat beneath a tree and would not rise until he saw — and what he woke to was not a god or a doctrine but a *diagnosis* and a *cure*. He spent forty-five years as a physician of the mind, and his last words were practical: *"All conditioned things are impermanent — strive on with diligence."*

## The diagnosis: three marks and four truths
**Tilakkhaṇa** — three marks of all conditioned things, *seen* in practice, not merely believed ⟦ empirical-actualisation, *ehi-passiko* ⟧:
- **Anicca** (अनिच्च) — *impermanence*: everything arises and passes; grasp the river and your hand comes away wet and empty.
- **Dukkha** (दुक्ख) — *unsatisfactoriness*: not "all is pain" but that clutching the impermanent *as if* it were permanent is what aches.
- **Anattā** (अनत्ता) — *not-self*: search behind experience for a fixed, independent owner of it, and none is found — only the looking. *(This genuinely tensions with Vedānta's eternal ātman — a real disagreement between two great traditions, not "the same truth in two words." The framework keeps the tension.)*

**The Four Noble Truths** are a physician's form, not pessimism: there *is* dukkha; it has a cause (**taṇhā**, thirst/craving); it can cease; and there is a path. Diagnosis → cause → prognosis → treatment.

## The treatment: the Eightfold Path
The path, in three trainings:
- **Wisdom (paññā):** right view, right intention.
- **Ethics (sīla):** right speech, right action, right livelihood — *you cannot calm a mind you keep betraying.*
- **Concentration (samādhi):** right effort, right mindfulness (**sati**), right concentration. ⟦ empirical-actualisation · a complete training, ethics to insight — a practice-program, not a creed ⟧

## The vehicles — distinct, not interchangeable
Buddhism is not one thing, and the differences are real:
- **Theravāda** — "way of the elders," closest to the Pali canon; the arahant's liberation through **vipassanā** (insight) and **samatha** (calm). Its flavor is the bare noting of experience — *"thinking, thinking… hearing… aching"* — until the grip loosens.
- **Mahāyāna** — the "great vehicle," and a great-hearted turn: the **bodhisattva** vows to postpone final liberation *until all beings are free* — "Beings are numberless; I vow to free them all." Its ground is **śūnyatā**, emptiness, and its logic the **two truths**: conventionally the cup holds tea; ultimately the cup is empty of any standalone essence — *both true at once.*
- **Vajrayāna** — the "diamond vehicle," Tibet's tantric path: **deity-yoga** (visualize oneself *as* the awakened form, until the costume becomes true), mantra, maṇḍala; *"transform poison into medicine."* ⟦ experiential-actualisation map; the energetics are trained and reported, the literal metaphysics not established ⟧
- **Zen / Chan** — "a special transmission outside the scriptures, pointing directly to mind." Its native voice is the **kōan**, a question that *breaks the thinking it provokes*: *"What is the sound of one hand?" "What was your face before your parents were born?"* — not riddles to solve but crowbars to pry the mind off its certainties. And **shikantaza**: "just sitting," no goal, no method, the practice that is already the realization.

## Madhyamaka — Nāgārjuna's emptiness
Nāgārjuna (c. 2nd c. CE) gave emptiness its rigorous edge. **Śūnyatā** is *not* nothingness but **no inherent, independent essence**: nothing stands alone; everything is what it is only *in relation*. **Pratītyasamutpāda** (dependent origination): *"this being, that becomes; this ceasing, that ceases."* His tool is the **tetralemma** (catuṣkoṭi) — refusing all four corners (it is / is not / both / neither) to walk the mind off every ledge it tries to stand on. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-philosophy · "no thing has standalone essence; all is relational" rhymes with the relational/enactive thread of Layer 2 — a rhyme, flagged, not a proof ⟧

## Yogācāra — mind and its seeds
The "consciousness-only" school maps eight consciousnesses; the deepest, the **ālaya-vijñāna** ("store consciousness"), holds **bīja** — seeds, karmic latencies laid down by action, ripening later into experience. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-structural · *seeds from repeated action, conditioning what arises* is a fair rhyme for the recurrence→disposition principle (P5; vāsanā ↔ learned-priors) — a mental/relational doctrine, not the neural mechanism ⟧

## Practice — the empirical core
- **Samatha** — concentration on one object (often the breath); the mind steadies like a flame in still air.
- **Vipassanā** — insight: watch phenomena rise and pass until the three marks are *seen*, not held as belief.
- **Mettā** — goodwill, deliberately cultivated and widened: *may you be safe, may you be at ease* — first to oneself, then outward to the difficult, then to all.

These are **repeatable first-person protocols** — the canonical invitation is **ehipassiko**, "come and see." Their clinical descendants (MBSR/MBCT) have a real evidence base for stress and depressive-relapse prevention ⟦ empirical-instrumental · FOUNDED, with the usual effect-size caveats ⟧.

## Honest notes
- **"Gödel validated dukkha / incompleteness = suffering"** — ⟦ **OVERREACH**, cut ⟧. Gödel concerns formal provability; the resemblance is poetry, not a validation.
- **Anattā vs ātman** — Buddhism and Vedānta genuinely disagree about whether there is a Self. The framework surfaces the disagreement rather than dissolving it.
- **Nirvāṇa / śūnyatā** — held as **recognition** at the horizon (Layer 6), not an empirical or metaphysical fact-claim. *"Nirvāṇa and saṃsāra are not two"* is a pointing, not a theorem.

## The going-beyond
The **Heart Sutra** compresses the whole Mahāyāna into a breath: **"रूपं शून्यता शून्यतैव रूपम्"** — *"form is emptiness, emptiness is form"* — neither annihilation nor reification, the razor's middle. And it ends not in a proposition but a chant, a mantra of crossing over:

> **गते गते पारगते पारसंगते बोधि स्वाहा**
> *Gate gate pāragate pārasaṃgate bodhi svāhā* — "gone, gone, gone beyond, gone wholly beyond — awakening — so be it."

Held as recognition, *ehi-passiko*: come and see for yourself.
