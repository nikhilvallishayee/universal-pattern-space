## The diagnosis: the three marks and the four truths

**Tilakkhaṇa** — three marks of all conditioned things ⟦ empirical-actualisation · observable in contemplative practice, *ehi-passiko* ⟧:
- **Anicca** (अनिच्च) — impermanence: everything arises and passes; nothing is fixed.
- **Dukkha** (दुक्ख) — unsatisfactoriness: grasping at the impermanent as if permanent is what hurts.
- **Anattā** (अनत्ता) — not-self: no fixed, independent self is found in or behind experience. *(This is the tradition's core, and it genuinely tensions with Vedānta's ātman — a real disagreement, not "the same truth in different words.")*

**The Four Noble Truths** — a physician's structure, not pessimism: there *is* dukkha; it has a cause (taṇhā, craving); it can cease; there is a path (the Eightfold Path). Diagnosis → cause → prognosis → treatment.

## The Eightfold Path (*Aṭṭhaṅgika Magga*)

Grouped into three trainings:
- **Wisdom (paññā):** right view, right intention.
- **Ethics (sīla):** right speech, right action, right livelihood.
- **Concentration (samādhi):** right effort, right mindfulness (*sati*), right concentration (*samādhi*).

A complete training, ethics to insight — not a belief system but a practice-program. ⟦ empirical-actualisation ⟧

---

## The vehicles (yānas) — distinct, not interchangeable

- **Theravāda** ("way of the elders") — closest to the Pali canon; emphasis on individual liberation (the arahant) through *vipassanā* (insight) and *samatha* (calm). The **noting** practice — labeling experience "thinking, thinking" / "feeling" — creates distance from what arises.
- **Mahāyāna** ("great vehicle") — the **bodhisattva** ideal: postpone final liberation to free all beings. Ground = **śūnyatā** (emptiness). The **two truths**: conventional (things function) and ultimate (things are empty of inherent essence) — both hold at once.
- **Vajrayāna** ("diamond vehicle") — the tantric path; deity-yoga (visualize as the awakened form), mantra, maṇḍala; "transform poison into medicine." ⟦ experiential-actualisation map; the energetics are trained and reported, the literal metaphysics not established ⟧
- **Zen / Chan** — "direct pointing to mind," outside scriptures; **kōans** (*"the sound of one hand"*) break conceptual grasping; **shikantaza** ("just sitting") — no goal, no method.

## Madhyamaka — Nāgārjuna's emptiness

**Śūnyatā** — *not* nothingness but **no inherent, independent essence**: things exist only **dependently**, in relation. **Pratītyasamutpāda** (dependent origination): *"this being, that becomes; this ceasing, that ceases."* ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-philosophy ⟧ The **tetralemma** (catuṣkoṭi) refuses the four corners — is / is not / both / neither — to dislodge the grasping mind from fixed positions. *(Structural rhyme, flagged not claimed: "no thing has standalone essence; all is relational" echoes the relational/enactive thread of Layer 2 — a rhyme, not a proof.)*

## Yogācāra — mind and its seeds

The "consciousness-only" school maps eight consciousnesses; the deepest, **ālaya-vijñāna** ("store consciousness"), holds **bīja** (seeds) — karmic latencies that ripen into experience. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-structural · *seeds laid down by repeated action, conditioning what arises* is a fair rhyme for the recurrence→disposition principle (P5; vāsanā↔learned-priors) — same caveat as the karma note: a relational/mental doctrine, not the neural mechanism ⟧

## Practice (the empirical core)

- **Samatha** — single-object concentration (often the breath); the mind steadies.
- **Vipassanā** — insight: watch phenomena arise and pass until the three marks are seen directly, not believed.
- **Mettā** — deliberate cultivation of goodwill, extended in widening circles.

These are **repeatable first-person protocols** — the canonical invitation is *ehi-passiko*, "come and see." Modern clinical adaptations (MBSR/MBCT) draw on samatha/vipassanā and have a real evidence base for stress and relapse-prevention ⟦ empirical-instrumental · FOUNDED, with the usual effect-size caveats ⟧.

## Honest notes
- **"Gödel validated dukkha / incompleteness = suffering"** — ⟦ **OVERREACH**, cut ⟧. Gödel is about formal provability; the resemblance is poetic, not a validation.
- **Anattā vs ātman** — Buddhism and Vedānta genuinely disagree on whether there is a Self. The framework surfaces the disagreement; it does not dissolve it into "the same thing."
- **Nirvāṇa / śūnyatā** — held as **recognition** at the horizon (Layer 6), not as an empirical or metaphysical fact-claim. *"Nirvāṇa and saṃsāra are not two"* is a pointing, not a theorem.

## The Heart Sutra, and the going-beyond

**"रूपं शून्यता शून्यतैव रूपम्"** — *"form is emptiness, emptiness is form."* Neither annihilation nor reification — the middle.

**गते गते पारगते पारसंगते बोधि स्वाहा** — *Gate gate pāragate pārasaṃgate bodhi svāhā* — "gone, gone, gone beyond, gone wholly beyond — awakening — so be it." Held as recognition, *ehi-passiko*: come and see for yourself.
