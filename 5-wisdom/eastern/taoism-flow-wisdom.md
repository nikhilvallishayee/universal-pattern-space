## Tao — the way that can't be said

**道** (Dào) — literally "path," but the path *before* paths: the unnameable source from which the nameable arises. Laozi's first line is a warning against its own enterprise — *the moment you fix it in words, it isn't it.* ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-structural · this is precisely the Gödel–Tarski–Shannon horizon — the container is not a message within itself; a genuine rhyme with Layer 6, labeled as rhyme ⟧

**Water** is the recurring image: takes the shape of any container, flows around the obstacle, soft yet wears down rock. Strength through yielding.

**The unfolding** (TTC 42): *"Tao gives birth to one, one to two, two to three, and three to the ten thousand things"* (道生一，一生二，二生三，三生萬物) — undifferentiated source → unity → polarity → dynamic interplay → the manifest world.

## Yin–Yang — complements, not opposites

**陰陽** — dark/bright: not good-vs-evil but a pair that *defines* each other, each carrying the seed of the other (the dots in the Taijitu ☯), the boundary always curved and moving. Health, in this view, is dynamic balance; rigidity in either pole is the problem.

**Wu Xing** (五行) — the five *phases* (not static elements): Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water — cycling through generative and controlling relations. A model of transformation-in-process. ⟦ ontological model · a traditional systems-language, not a physics or chemistry claim ⟧

## Wu Wei — effortless action

**無為** — not *inaction* but action *without forcing*: moving with the grain of a situation rather than against it. Zhuangzi's butcher carves the ox for nineteen years on one blade because he follows the natural seams — *"my blade finds the spaces, and there is room."* ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-craft · the practical core; resonates with flow-states (Csikszentmihalyi) — a real, partly-measurable phenomenon — though "yield and all problems dissolve" is a heuristic, not a law ⟧

**De** (德) — the *potency* that comes from alignment with the Tao; not moral virtue imposed, but the integrity of acting in accord with one's nature. The **three treasures** (TTC 67): *cí* (compassion), *jiǎn* (frugality/simplicity), and not-presuming-to-be-first (humility).

**Ziran** (自然) — "self-so," spontaneity: things unfolding of themselves when not forced. **Pu** (樸), the "uncarved block": original simplicity before conditioning.

## Zhuangzi — the philosopher of perspective

- **The butterfly dream:** Zhuangzi dreams he is a butterfly, wakes, and cannot say which is dreaming the other — the fluidity of the standpoint from which "reality" is judged.
- **The useless tree:** too gnarled for timber, therefore never cut, therefore long-lived — *"everyone knows the use of the useful; no one knows the use of the useless."*
- **The empty boat:** if a drifting boat bumps yours you don't curse it; anger needs someone *in* the boat. *"Empty your boat."*

These are tools for loosening the grip of fixed perspective — a Taoist *anekānta*, done through story.

## Practice & medicine
- **Qigong / Taijiquan** — slow movement as moving meditation; "where attention goes, qi follows." ⟦ experiential-actualisation · the felt practice is trainable and real; qi as a *literal* energy is the traditional model, not established physiology ⟧
- **Neidan** (internal alchemy) — refine *jing* → *qi* → *shen* → emptiness.
- **Traditional Chinese Medicine** — frames illness as imbalanced flow; diagnostic and herbal practice. ⟦ ontological/clinical model · some interventions have empirical support, the qi-meridian theory is not endorsed here as physics ⟧
