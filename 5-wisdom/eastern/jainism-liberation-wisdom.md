## The diagnosis: a luminous soul, weighed down by matter
Jain metaphysics is **dualist** and, distinctively among Indian systems, **materialist about karma**:
- **Jīva** (soul) — conscious, blissful, and **intrinsically omniscient** — but in bondage its innate knowing is *obscured*, the way a mirror is dimmed by dust.
- **Ajīva** (the non-living) — and within it the crucial term: **karma is a subtle *physical* substance** (*karma-pudgala*), fine particles that, drawn by passion-driven action (*kaṣāya* — attachment and aversion), literally **stick to the soul** and weigh it down, coloring its rebirths (*leśyā*) and dimming its light.

So bondage is not a moral ledger in the abstract; it is a kind of *accretion*. And liberation is therefore not the **gaining** of anything — not knowledge added, not grace received — but a **shedding**: stop the inflow (*saṃvara*) and burn off the accumulated load (*nirjarā*) through vow, austerity, and equanimity, until the soul, *uncovered*, rises (in Jain cosmology, literally) to the summit of the universe (*Siddhaśilā*).

## The renunciatory engine — liberation as *subtraction*
This is the structural heart worth naming: where many paths *add* (devotion, knowledge, union with a ground), Jainism **removes** — purity is what *remains* when the karmic coverings are stripped, exactly as the soul *already* is beneath them. ⟦ a labeled rhyme — *signal, not proof* — with the framework's own discipline: **counter-bias by subtraction; the incompressible essence is what's left when you take away what isn't it** (MDL/compression). The structures rhyme across ethics and epistemics; this is **not** a claim that Jain metaphysics *is* minimum-description-length, only that both are *subtractive* methods of reaching an irreducible core ⟧ It also touches the **parthood invariant** quietly: the soul is a *part* that cannot, while bound, contain or even clearly see the whole it is in — and the path is the slow un-occluding of that seeing (the cardinality floor FOUNDED; the omniscience-at-the-summit reading held at the horizon — see [docs/incompleteness-conjecture.md](../../docs/incompleteness-conjecture.md)).

## The ladder of embodiment
The soul transmigrates through bodies **graded by sense-capacity** — from **one-sensed** beings (earth-, water-, fire-, air-, and plant-bodies: touch only) up through two-, three-, four-, and **five-sensed** beings (full senses + mind). This grading is *why* ahiṃsā reaches so far down: even one-sensed life is *ensouled* and can suffer bondage — the ethics and the metaphysics are **one piece**, not bolted together. ⟦ ontological · the tradition's frame, not a biology claim ⟧

## The fourteen *guṇasthānas* — rungs of un-covering ⟦ DEFENSIBLE as a developmental map ⟧
A precise fourteen-stage map of *spiritual purification*, from total delusion to total freedom:
- **1. Mithyātva** — deluded worldview, the soul wholly identified with matter.
- **4. Samyaktva** — the first dawning of *right faith / right seeing* (often momentary at first, then lost and regained — the tradition expects oscillation, not a clean climb).
- **5–6.** the taking of partial (*aṇuvrata*) then full (*mahāvrata*) **vows**.
- **8–12.** the progressive **conquest of the passions** (*kaṣāya*) — either *suppressing* them (*upaśama*, reversible) or *annihilating* them (*kṣapaka*, irreversible).
- **13. Sayoga-kevalī** — **kevala jñāna**: omniscience attained *while still embodied* (the *kevalin*, e.g. a Tīrthaṅkara teaching after enlightenment).
- **14. Ayoga-kevalī → siddha** — in the final instant all activity ceases; at death the freed soul becomes a **siddha**, rising to the summit, *mokṣa* complete.

⟦ The ladder is a coherent developmental architecture (DEFENSIBLE as a map of stages); **kevala jñāna as *literal* all-knowing is OVERREACH** — labeled, held as the tradition's claim, not asserted as fact. The framework's own awakening-stages caution applies: read it as a diagnostic lens for *where* a process is, not a metaphysical ranking of persons ⟧

## The Tīrthaṅkaras and the shape of time
The **24 "ford-makers"** of *this* descending half-cycle — those who crossed and built the crossing. **Historically grounded for the last two only:** **Pārśvanātha** (c. 8th c. BCE) and **Mahāvīra** (c. 599–527 BCE); the earlier twenty-two belong to the tradition's cosmic prehistory. Jain cosmic time turns in vast wheels (*kāla-cakra*) of ascending (*utsarpiṇī*) and descending (*avasarpiṇī*) half-cycles, each subdivided into ages of waxing and waning dharma. ⟦ the deep-time spans = **mythic cosmology, not chronometry** — labeled (the same discipline applied to the Vedic yugas: keep the *cyclic intuition* as a worldview, drop the *numbers* as literal dates) ⟧ The kept kernel: a **beginningless, cyclic cosmos with no creator-god** — the universe is *eternal and self-running*, neither made nor ended — which is a genuine and unusual metaphysical position (atheistic eternalism), described as the tradition's own.
