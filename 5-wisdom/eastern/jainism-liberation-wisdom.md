## The diagnosis: soul bound by matter
Jain metaphysics is dualist and, distinctively, *materialist about karma*:
- **Jīva** (soul) — conscious, intrinsically omniscient, but obscured.
- **Ajīva** — the non-living, including **karma as a subtle physical substance** (*karma-pudgala*) that literally adheres to the soul through passion-driven action (*kaṣāya*), weighing it down and dimming its innate knowing.

Liberation, then, is not *gaining* anything but **shedding** — stopping the inflow of karmic matter (*saṃvara*) and burning off what's accumulated (*nirjarā*) through ascetic discipline, until the soul, uncovered, rises (in Jain cosmology, literally) to the summit.

## The ladder of life
The soul travels through embodiments graded by sense-capacity — **one-sensed** (earth, water, fire, air, plant bodies: touch only) up through **five-sensed** beings (full senses + mind). This is *why* ahiṃsā extends so far down: even one-sensed life is ensouled. The ethics and the metaphysics are one piece. ⟦ ontological · the tradition's frame, not a biology claim ⟧

## The fourteen guṇasthānas — stages of un-covering
A precise map from **mithyātva** (deluded worldview) through the first dawning of right faith (*samyaktva*), the taking of vows, the conquest of the passions, to **kevala jñāna** (omniscience, stage 13 — the *kevalin*, still embodied) and finally **ayoga-kevalī → siddha** (stage 14: liberation, the soul freed entirely). ⟦ DEFENSIBLE as a developmental map; **kevala jñāna as literal all-knowing is OVERREACH** — labeled, held as the tradition's claim ⟧

## The Tīrthaṅkaras
The **24 "ford-makers"** — those who crossed and built the crossing for others. Historically grounded for the last two: **Pārśvanātha** (c. 8th c. BCE) and **Mahāvīra** (c. 599–527 BCE, contemporary of the Buddha); the earlier twenty-two belong to the tradition's cosmic prehistory ⟦ the deep-time spans = mythic cosmology, not chronometry — labeled ⟧.
