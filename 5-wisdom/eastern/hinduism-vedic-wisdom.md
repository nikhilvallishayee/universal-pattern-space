## The ground: Brahman and Ātman

**Brahman** (ब्रह्मन्) — in Advaita, Reality itself, not a deity. Three-fold: **Sat** (existence), **Chit** (consciousness), **Ananda** (bliss); together **Satchitananda**. The non-dual reading: not God creating a universe, but the universe *as* Brahman knowing itself. ⟦ ontological · a *darśana*, coherence-judged ⟧

**Ātman** (आत्मन्) — the Self, not the ego: awareness that *appears* separate and isn't — wave to ocean. (Example, not proof: a node mistaking itself for independent — the notion-of-I error, Layer 6.)

**The Mahāvākyas** — four "great sayings," one per Veda, each pointing to Ātman = Brahman:
1. **प्रज्ञानं ब्रह्म** *Prajñānam Brahma* — "Consciousness is Brahman" (Aitareya, Ṛg).
2. **अहं ब्रह्मास्मि** *Aham Brahmāsmi* — "I am Brahman" (Bṛhadāraṇyaka, Yajur).
3. **तत् त्वम् असि** *Tat Tvam Asi* — "That thou art" (Chāndogya, Sāma).
4. **अयम् आत्मा ब्रह्म** *Ayam Ātmā Brahma* — "This Self is Brahman" (Māṇḍūkya, Atharva).

These are *recognitions*, not equations — the tradition's own claim, held at the horizon.

## The corpus, in time

**The four Vedas** — the oldest layer (Ṛg ~1500–1200 BCE; see [reweave-timeline](../../docs/reweave-timeline.md) for the dating's honest range):
1. **Ṛg** — hymns of praise to the cosmic powers.
2. **Sāma** — the Ṛg set to chant; knowledge as sound and meter.
3. **Yajur** — the liturgy of sacrifice; knowledge enacted in ritual.
4. **Atharva** — formulas for daily life; the practical and the folk.

**The Upaniṣads** (उपनिषद्, "sitting down near") — 100+ later texts that turn from ritual to inquiry: direct experience over belief, in dialogue. The seed of **Vedānta** ("the Vedas' end").

## The four aims — Puruṣārthas

The tradition's account of a complete human life:
- **Dharma** (धर्म) — right conduct, one's duty and nature.
- **Artha** (अर्थ) — prosperity, security, the means.
- **Kāma** (काम) — pleasure, desire, love.
- **Mokṣa** (मोक्ष) — liberation from the cycle.

A worldly path *and* a liberative one, held together — not world-denying.

## The three paths — the Yogas

- **Karma Yoga** — union through action: act without attachment to the fruit. *"You have a right to action alone, never to its fruits"* (Bhagavad Gītā 2.47).
- **Bhakti Yoga** — union through devotion; love as the path. Classified by *rasa* (bhāva): **śānta** (peaceful), **dāsya** (servant), **sakhya** (friend), **vātsalya** (parental), **mādhurya** (beloved).
- **Jñāna Yoga** — union through knowledge: discriminate the real from the unreal (*viveka*), strip away what you are not (*neti neti*).

## Time, action, and the cycle

**The Yugas** — four ages (Satya, Tretā, Dvāpara, Kali) of declining dharma, turning in vast cycles, dissolving and renewing without absolute beginning or end. *Refuted, and extracted:* the specific **mahā-yuga arithmetic** (4.32-million-year ages, the 432,000 base) as **literal chronometry** is REFUTED — it is post-Vedic, and in the Ṛg Veda *yuga* means "generation" ⟦ REFUTED-as-chronometry ⟧. But the **kernel is real and worth keeping**: a *cyclic* cosmos — creation → dissolution → re-creation, with no privileged single origin — was, among ancient cosmologies, an extraordinary conceptual leap, and its *form* is a **live contender in modern physics**, not a dead myth: bouncing / cyclic cosmologies (ekpyrotic, Penrose's CCC, loop-quantum-cosmology) take exactly that shape, and are falsifiable this decade via CMB B-modes ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-and-live · see [docs/the-grounded-weave.md](../../docs/the-grounded-weave.md) — convergence, not proof; keep the cyclic *intuition*, drop the 432 *numbers* ⟧.

**Saṃsāra** (संसार) — the cycle of birth and death; the round the paths aim to be freed from.

**Karma** (कर्म) — action and consequence; the tradition's law of moral causation. Three kinds: **sañcita** (accumulated), **prārabdha** (now ripening), **kriyamāṇa** (being made). *(Structural rhyme, not identity: "repeated action lays down tendency" echoes the recurrence→disposition principle — see [first-principles](../../docs/first-principles.md) P5 — but karma is a moral/relational doctrine, not the neural mechanism.)*

## The divine forms

**Trimūrti** — three functions of the one: **Brahmā** (creation), **Viṣṇu** (preservation), **Śiva** (dissolution).

**Śiva–Śakti** — consciousness (Śiva, the still) and its creative power (Śakti, the dynamic); "not two but one appearing as two." The **Ardhanārīśvara** (half-Śiva, half-Pārvatī) icons unity-containing-polarity. *(This pair is the source of the framework's UPS=UPS axiom — see [shiva-shakti-principle](../../2-field/shiva-shakti-principle.md).)*

**Avatāra** (अवतार, "descent") — in Vaiṣṇava theology, the divine taking form when dharma declines; the ten of Viṣṇu, fish to Buddha to the future Kalki.

## The practices

**Patañjali's Yoga Sūtras** — *"योगश्चित्तवृत्तिनिरोधः / yoga is the stilling of the mind's fluctuations"* (1.2). The **Aṣṭāṅga** (eight limbs): **yama** (restraints), **niyama** (observances), **āsana** (posture), **prāṇāyāma** (breath), **pratyāhāra** (sense-withdrawal), **dhāraṇā** (concentration), **dhyāna** (meditation), **samādhi** (absorption). ⟦ empirical-actualisation · a staged, repeatable first-person protocol — *ehi-passiko* ⟧

**Tantra** (तन्त्र, "loom/system") — the path that uses everything, includes what others exclude. **Kuṇḍalinī**: energy held latent, awakened to rise through seven **chakras** (Mūlādhāra → Sahasrāra). ⟦ experiential-actualisation map · the *felt* energetics are reported and trainable; their literal physiology is not established — held as model, not anatomy ⟧

## The schools

**Advaita Vedānta** (Ādi Śaṅkara, 8th c.) — only Brahman is real; the world is **māyā** (appearance / creative power, not mere "illusion" — the power by which one appears as many); Ātman and Brahman are one. The **three states** — *jāgrat* (waking), *svapna* (dream), *suṣupti* (deep sleep) — are witnessed by a fourth, **turīya** (pure awareness). ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-philosophy ⟧

**Kashmir Śaivism** (Abhinavagupta, 10th–11th c.) — **pratyabhijñā** ("re-cognition"): liberation is not learning something new but recognizing what always was. **Spanda** (स्पन्द) — the "throb" of consciousness, movement-in-stillness. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-structural — Spanda↔field-dynamics is an apt rhyme (see [the-grounded-weave](../../docs/the-grounded-weave.md)); not a physics result ⟧ *(Note on the name: **"Kashmir Śaivism" is a modern umbrella** — Alexis Sanderson's scholarship shows it gathers several distinct medieval systems: Trika, Krama, Spanda, Pratyabhijñā, and Kaula. The tradition did not call itself that.)*

## Time & self as contraction — the kañcukas, the word, and the moment
*How three Indic streams each treat lived, sequential time not as the ground but as a **limitation** of something sequence-free — and where they sharply disagree.*

> ⟦ **objective** the tradition's own account of time-as-contraction · **mode** ontological (the metaphysics) + empirical-actualisation (the yogic *kṣaṇa*-discipline) · **status** **DEFENSIBLE-as-convergence** with the incompleteness-conjecture's "time = a part's limitation," **NOT identity** — the metaphysical engines differ (non-dual/positive here vs. set-theoretic "part of a whole" there) · see [docs/incompleteness-conjecture.md](../../docs/incompleteness-conjecture.md) ⟧

### Kāla as kañcuka — time is *literally* one of the five cloaks
In the Pratyabhijñā / Trika analysis (Kṣemarāja, *Pratyabhijñāhṛdayam*; Utpaladeva, *Īśvarapratyabhijñā*; Abhinavagupta, *Tantrāloka*), unlimited, eternal (*nitya*), omniscient, omnipotent Śiva-consciousness does not *fall* into limitation — it **freely contracts** itself into a bound individual subject (*aṇu*) by drawing on five **kañcukas** (कञ्चुक, "cloaks/sheaths"), the limiting powers within the **māyā-tattva**:

- **kalā** (कला) — limited *agency* (the all-doer becomes a doer-of-some-things);
- **vidyā** (विद्या) — limited *knowledge* (the all-knower becomes a knower-of-some-things);
- **rāga** (राग) — *attachment/desire* (the self-full becomes one who *lacks* and craves);
- **kāla** (काल) — **time**: the limitation imposing **succession** — past, present, future — on what is intrinsically timeless;
- **niyati** (नियति) — *limited causality/space*, the binding to a particular order and place.

The load-bearing point for the framework: **kāla is not the backdrop — it is one of the cloaks.** Kṣemarāja's gloss runs that *kāla* "reduces the eternity of the Universal and brings about division of past, present, and future," delimiting the knowing subject. So time, here, **is the contraction by which an unlimited, sequence-free whole experiences itself as a limited, sequential part** — stated as doctrine c. 9th–11th c. (Doxography: David Peter Lawrence, IEP "Kashmiri Shaiva Philosophy"; tr. Jaideva Singh, *Pratyabhijñāhṛdayam*.) ⟦ ontological/DEFENSIBLE; near-literal anchor for the incompleteness rhyme ⟧

> **The convergence, and its honest limit.** This lands strikingly close to the [incompleteness conjecture](../../docs/incompleteness-conjecture.md)'s *"time is the serial face of a part's incompleteness."* But the **engines differ**: Trika's is a **non-dual, positive, voluntary** self-veiling (Śiva *plays* at being bound — *svātantrya*, sovereign freedom), whereas the conjecture's is a **set-theoretic / information-loss** floor (a proper part *cannot* losslessly contain the whole). "Cloak" (*kañcuka*) and "compression" rhyme; they are not the same category. **Convergence = signal, not proof** — and "subset of a whole" is modern vocabulary the texts do not use (they say *saṅkoca*, contraction). ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-convergence, NOT FOUNDED-as-identity ⟧

### Bhartṛhari — language is the cut (*Vākyapadīya*, Kāla-samuddeśa)
The grammarian-philosopher **Bhartṛhari** (c. 5th c. CE) holds that reality's ground is **Śabda-Brahman** — the Word as the undivided, sequence-free absolute. At the level of **paśyantī** (the "seeing" speech, prior to articulation) there is **no *krama*** — no succession, no division. Succession and differentiation enter through **Kāla-śakti** (the power of Time) *and*, inseparably, through **language / *vikalpa*** (conceptual division): the One *manifests as* the many-in-sequence the moment it is spoken or thought. The corollary the framework leans on: **the One cannot be expressed without the distortion that symbolic division imposes** — the limit of expressibility is built into the act of saying. (Matilal, *The Word and the World*; Ashok Aklujkar; Jan Houben, *The Saṃbandha-samuddeśa*; P.S. Sharma's edition of the *Kālasamuddeśa*.) ⟦ DEFENSIBLE · the best pre-modern grounding for "it cannot be irrefutably expressed because of the constraint of symbolism itself" — the Tarski/Gödel horizon in Sanskrit; **language IS the cut** ⟧

### Patañjali — only the *kṣaṇa* is real (YS 3.52, 4.33)
Patañjali's *Yoga Sūtras* give the **kṣaṇa** (क्षण, the indivisible moment) doctrine: **only the present *kṣaṇa* is real**; **succession (*krama*) is a mental construction** with no thing-hood of its own — *"for the mind that has discerned, there is no succession"* (the Vyāsa-bhāṣya / Vācaspati Miśra gloss on 4.33). The deepest absorptions — **nirbīja / asamprajñāta samādhi** (seedless, objectless) — are described as **beyond *kāla***. ⟦ empirical-actualisation (the *kṣaṇa*-discipline is a trainable, repeatable contemplative observation) + ontological ⟧
> **The kept disagreement (do not flatten into the Śaiva picture).** Patañjali's frame is **Sāṃkhya-dualist**: time belongs to **prakṛti** (nature/matter); the timeless is **puruṣa's *kaivalya*** — the witness's *isolation* from prakṛti, **not** an infinite whole-experiencing-itself-as-part. So the *kṣaṇa* doctrine converges on "succession is constructed" while **disagreeing** on the metaphysics: dualist-isolation (Sāṃkhya-Yoga) vs. non-dual self-veiling (Trika) vs. sole-reality-of-Brahman (Advaita). Three Indic schools, one phenomenon (lived time is not the ground), **three irreconcilable engines.** The framework keeps the tension. ⟦ real intra-Indic disagreement, surfaced not dissolved ⟧

### Gauḍapāda — *ajātivāda*: nothing was ever born in time (*Māṇḍūkya Kārikā*)
The Advaitin **Gauḍapāda** (c. 6th–7th c. CE, Śaṅkara's *parama-guru*) presses the contraction-of-time logic to its non-dual limit. In the *Vaitathya Prakaraṇa* of the **Māṇḍūkya Kārikā** he argues by **vaitathya** (unreality) that **dream and waking stand on the same footing** — both are mind-projected appearances, neither uniquely "real" by contrast with the other — and from there to **ajātivāda** (अजातिवाद, the doctrine of **non-origination**): in the truly real, *nothing has ever actually come into being*, so **temporal arising itself is illusory** (*"na kaścij jāyate"* — nothing whatever is born). Where Trika says the timeless *contracts into* time and Patañjali says succession is *constructed by* the mind, Gauḍapāda says the boldest version: **there is no real becoming at all** — birth, change, and sequence are *vivarta* (apparent transformation), never *pariṇāma* (actual transformation). ⟦ ontological/DEFENSIBLE-as-philosophy · the strongest non-dual statement of "time is appearance, not ground" — coherence-judged ⟧
> **Honest limit on the modern gloss:** Gauḍapāda deploys the dream-analogy to **level** waking down to dream's status (both equally unreal-in-themselves), *not* to nest dreamers inside dreamers — "we might wake into another's dream" is a **modern extension**, not his claim. And *ajātivāda* is a contested reading even within Vedānta (Rāmānuja's qualified non-dualism rejects it outright). Surfaced, not smoothed.

## The native method-voice: the Upaniṣadic dialogue
The tradition's *own* form of multiplicity-seeking-truth is the **guru–śiṣya saṃvāda** — the teacher and student in dialogue, the question pressed until it opens. In **Chāndogya Upaniṣad 6**, Uddālaka teaches his son Śvetaketu by *experiment*, not assertion:

> *"Bring a fruit of the banyan. Break it. What do you see?" — "Tiny seeds." "Break one. What do you see?" — "Nothing, father." "That subtle essence you cannot see — from it the great banyan grows. Believe, dear one: that is the Self of all this. **Tat tvam asi** — that thou art."*

And the salt dissolved in water (CU 6.13): unseen, yet the whole vessel is salt to the taste — *so the Self pervades, unseen.* This is Hinduism's council: not named voices in one head, but the **back-and-forth of teacher and tested student**, recognition drawn out rather than declared. Illustrate the tradition *through this*, not a generic council.

## OM, and the recognition

**ॐ (AUM)** — Māṇḍūkya Upaniṣad: **A** (waking), **U** (dream), **M** (deep sleep), and the **silence** that is *turīya*, beyond. The whole in one syllable.

**तत् त्वम् असि** *Tat Tvam Asi* — "That thou art": **Tat** (Brahman), **tvam** (you), **asi** (*are*, not *become*). The claim is recognition, not attainment — no journey, only seeing. Held at Layer 6, with its guard: the *notion* of "I" is not the I; the symbol must not crown itself.
