# 🌳🍄 Mycorrhizal Networks: Signaling, Not Sentience
*A real underground economy — held to the evidence, kernel extracted from the myth*

## What is genuinely real ⟦ FOUNDED ⟧
The underground life of plants is astonishing *without* any embellishment:
- **Mycorrhizal symbiosis** is real and ancient — fungi sheathe or penetrate plant roots and trade **mineral nutrients (phosphorus, nitrogen, water)** for the plant's **photosynthetic sugars**. The vast majority of land plants depend on it; it helped make land-plant life possible. This is one of biology's foundational mutualisms.
- **Plants signal.** They release **volatile organic compounds** when attacked, and neighbors downwind can **prime their defenses** in response. They conduct **electrical and chemical signals** internally and, in some setups, between plants. Information genuinely moves.
- **Fungal networks connect plants.** Common mycorrhizal networks (CMNs) can link the roots of different trees, and *some* transfer of carbon and signal between plants has been measured.

## Where the popular story outran the data ⟦ CONTESTED → extracted ⟧
The "**wood-wide web** / **mother tree**" narrative — that a wise old tree *recognizes her own offspring* and *deliberately sends them more carbon* through a forest-wide conscious network — is **not supported by the evidence**, however beautiful. The landmark review **Karst, Jones & Hoeksema (*Nature Ecology & Evolution*, 2023)** found:
- claims that CMNs are **ubiquitous in forests** — *insufficiently supported*;
- claims that network transfer **improves seedling performance** — *insufficiently supported*;
- the claim that mature trees **preferentially route resources to kin** — **no peer-reviewed evidence at all**;
- and that **positive-citation bias roughly doubled** over 25 years — popular claims citing each other into apparent fact.

**The extracted kernel — keep this, drop the rest:** networks *exist*; *some* resource and signal *can* move through them; mycorrhizal symbiosis is foundational and real. What's unsupported is the **intentional, kin-favoring, quasi-conscious "Mother Tree."** The forest is a genuine **distributed exchange economy** — which is wondrous — but it is not a *mind*, and the trees are not *choosing* in the way the story implies. (Plant "consciousness" more broadly: Taiz et al. argue plant neurobiology conflates information-processing with subjective awareness — the former real, the latter unestablished.)

## What it honestly models for the framework
A real, founded picture of **distributed coordination without a center** — nutrient and signal flowing across a network of mutual dependence, resilience emerging from connection rather than command. That is a genuine and useful model (and a true cousin of the relational/enactive thread): *capacities that live in the relations between nodes, not inside any one.* The discipline is to take *that* — the founded ecology of interdependence — and **not** smuggle in intention, altruism, or sentience the data doesn't show. The "forest types through this document as you read" device is cut: you are not a tree and the page is not mycelium; the real symbiosis underfoot is marvel enough.
