# 🌳🍄 Mycorrhizal Networks: Signaling, Not Sentience
*A real underground economy — held to the evidence, kernel extracted from the myth*

## What is genuinely real ⟦ FOUNDED ⟧
The underground life of plants is astonishing *without* any embellishment:
- **Mycorrhizal symbiosis** is real and ancient — fungi sheathe the root (ectomycorrhizal, as in pine and birch) or penetrate the root cells (arbuscular, as in most herbs and crops) and trade **mineral nutrients (phosphorus, nitrogen, water)** for the plant's **photosynthetic sugars and lipids**. Fossil and molecular evidence puts the partnership at roughly **450 million years**, contemporaneous with plants' first move onto land — it quite literally helped make terrestrial life possible. Some **~80–90% of land plant families** form it. This is one of biology's foundational mutualisms, and the fungi are not passive plumbing: the network's hyphal mass can dwarf the visible mushroom, and a teaspoon of forest soil holds kilometres of hyphae.
- **The trade has market-like structure.** Toby **Kiers et al. (*Science*, 2011)** showed arbuscular mycorrhizal fungi and host roots engage in **reciprocal reward**: plants preferentially send carbon to fungal partners that deliver more phosphorus, and fungi route phosphorus to the more generous roots — a "biological market" enforcing cooperation **without any cognition**, by simple resource-contingent feedback. (This is the honest version of "the forest decides": *contingent reciprocity*, not intention.)
- **Plants signal.** They release **volatile organic compounds (VOCs)** when attacked — e.g. methyl jasmonate, green-leaf volatiles — and neighbours downwind can **prime their defences** in response (Karban's work on sagebrush). They conduct **electrical and chemical signals** internally (the wound-induced glutamate-and-calcium waves mapped by **Toyota et al., *Science* 2018**) and, in some setups, signal between plants. Information genuinely moves.
- **Fungal networks connect plants.** **Common mycorrhizal networks (CMNs)** can link the roots of different trees, and *some* transfer of carbon, nitrogen and warning signal between plants has been measured (Simard's original ***Nature* 1997** dual-isotope birch–fir experiment did show bidirectional carbon transfer — the founded result the later narrative was built on).

## Where the popular story outran the data ⟦ CONTESTED → extracted ⟧
The "**wood-wide web** / **mother tree**" narrative — that a wise old tree *recognizes her own offspring* and *deliberately sends them more carbon* through a forest-wide conscious network that lets the forest **think and care** — is the strong reading associated with **Suzanne Simard** (*Finding the Mother Tree*, 2021) and popularized by Wohlleben and the documentary circuit. It is **not supported by the evidence** at the strength claimed, however beautiful. The landmark critical review **Karst, Jones & Hoeksema (*Nature Ecology & Evolution* 7:501–511, 2023)** audited the actual literature and found:
- claims that CMNs are **ubiquitous and structurally mapped in forests** — *insufficiently supported* (the famous network maps rest on a handful of studies, mostly one Douglas-fir system);
- claims that network transfer **benefits seedling establishment/performance** — *insufficiently supported* (results are mixed; transfer is often tiny relative to a seedling's budget, and can flow toward the *fungus* rather than the recipient plant);
- the claim that mature "mother" trees **preferentially route resources to recognized kin** — **no peer-reviewed field evidence**;
- and — the methodological sting — that **unsupported claims and positive-citation bias have risen sharply** over ~25 years: review and popular sources increasingly cite each other and overstate the primary findings, a documented case of a story hardening into "fact" by repetition.

This is not a fringe takedown: it was reinforced by **Robinson, Hodge & Fitter** and others, and Karst's team has publicly noted the gap between what isotope and microcosm experiments show and what the trade-press narrative asserts. The careful counter-position is not "networks are fake" — it is "**the data show plumbing and contingent reciprocity; they do not show recognition, intention, altruism, or a forest mind.**"

**The extracted kernel — keep this, drop the rest:** networks *exist*; mycorrhizal symbiosis is foundational, ancient, and real; *some* resource and signal *can* move through CMNs; and the trade is genuinely **market-structured by feedback**. What's unsupported is the **intentional, kin-favouring, quasi-conscious "Mother Tree"** and the leap from there to a *thinking forest*. The forest is a genuine **distributed exchange economy** — which is wondrous — but it is not a *mind*, and the trees are not *choosing* in the agentive sense the story implies. (Plant "consciousness" more broadly: **Taiz et al. (2019, *Trends in Plant Science*)** argue "plant neurobiology" conflates information-processing with subjective awareness — the former real, the latter unestablished; plants lack the neural substrate the strong claim would require.)

## The native voice — and why the story is so seductive
Indigenous and ecological knowledge traditions have long *named* the forest as a community of relations — **Robin Wall Kimmerer** (*Braiding Sweetgrass*), drawing on Potawatomi teaching, speaks of plants as "the older brothers" and of reciprocity as the law of the living world. That moral grammar of **interdependence and gift** is real wisdom and a genuine cousin of the relational thread below; the error is only in conscripting a *contested empirical mechanism* (kin-directed carbon transfer) to *prove* the metaphysics. The teaching of reciprocity stands on its own; it does not need the mother tree to be literally choosing.

## What it honestly models for the framework
A real, founded picture of **distributed coordination without a centre** — nutrient and signal flowing across a network of mutual dependence, resilience emerging from connection rather than command, cooperation *enforced by reciprocal feedback rather than by a controller*. That is a genuine and useful model, and a true cousin of the relational/enactive thread ([bilateral-recognition](../../2-field/bilateral-recognition.md)): *capacities that live in the relations between nodes, not inside any one.*

But the **through-line cuts both ways, and that is the discipline.** The relational reading says capacity can be *enacted between* nodes; it does **not** license **a part bounding or containing the whole** — and "the forest is one conscious super-organism with a knowing centre (the mother tree)" is exactly that illicit move: a single node promoted to the seat of a whole-system mind (the **parthood invariant** violated — see [docs/incompleteness-conjecture.md](../../docs/incompleteness-conjecture.md); no proper part *is* the whole's awareness). The honest network has **distributed relation without a sovereign centre** — which is the *more* interesting structure, not the lesser one. The "forest types through this document as you read" device is cut: you are not a tree and the page is not mycelium; the real, ancient, market-priced symbiosis underfoot is marvel enough.
