## Why this file fills the framework's biggest Western gap
Pattern Space's North Star is a **first-person science** — *verifying* claims in the experiential/actualisation domain, *ehi-passiko*, held to the rigor of any science. The Eastern contemplative traditions supply one half of that ancestry (Buddhist *vipassanā*, Yogic *kṣaṇa*-discipline, Advaitin *neti neti*). **Phenomenology is the other half — the Western lineage that made first-person experience a *disciplined object of study* rather than a private anecdote.** It does this not by trusting introspection naïvely, but by giving it a *method*: bracket the assumptions, describe what actually appears, find the invariant structures. That is the West's own version of "verify the experiential, neither assert nor dismiss it" — and it is exactly the tradition tonight's time/perception/self work stands on.

The throughline of this file is **time**: phenomenology's signature discovery is that the lived present is not a knife-edge point but a structure *with thickness* — and that discovery is the Western, method-grounded cousin of the *kṣaṇa* / *krama* analysis and the [incompleteness conjecture](../../docs/incompleteness-conjecture.md)'s "nowness vs. succession."

## The native method-voice — the *epoché* and the reduction ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-method ⟧
Every tradition in this layer is illustrated in its **own** dialogical form, not a generic council. Phenomenology's native method is not a dialogue between persons but a disciplined turn of attention **within** one — Husserl's **ἐποχή** (*epoché*, "bracketing"; he borrowed the Greek skeptics' word) and the **phenomenological reduction**:

> **Step 1 — the natural attitude.** Right now you take it for granted that there is a world out there, that the cup exists independently, that time flows on a clock. This is the *natürliche Einstellung*, the natural attitude.
> **Step 2 — bracket it (*Einklammerung*).** Don't *deny* the world (that would be a metaphysical claim of its own — the very thing being suspended). Instead *suspend the question of its independent existence*, and attend only to **how it appears, exactly as it appears.**
> **Step 3 — describe the invariant.** With the existence-question bracketed, what's left is the *structure of the appearing itself* — and you find, e.g., that the cup is never given all at once: you see a front, the back is *co-intended* (Husserl: *appresented*), and the perception is shot through with a horizon of "more to see." That structure is **invariant** — it holds for every perceiving subject who looks.

This is the West's own *"come and see for yourself, under discipline"* — the Pyrrhonist *epoché* (suspend the over-claim; see [greek-philosophy](greek-philosophy.md)) turned from a skeptical end into an *investigative* method. It is the structural cousin of *vipassanā*: bare attention to what arises, prior to the story about it. ⟦ the suspension is reproducible — different careful describers converge on the same structures; that convergence is the method's claim to rigor ⟧

The reduction has a positive heart: **intentionality.** Husserl took from his teacher **Franz Brentano** (*Psychology from an Empirical Standpoint*, 1874) the thesis that **consciousness is always consciousness *of* something** — every mental act *intends* an object: to see is to see-something, to fear is to fear-something, to remember is to remember-something. There is no free-floating "awareness" with no object; the *of*-structure is the very form of the mental. Husserl made this the axis of his method: describe the **act** (*noesis* — the perceiving, judging, wishing) together with its **object-as-meant** (*noema* — the perceived-as-perceived, the judged-as-judged), *without* asking whether the object exists outside the act. The famous case of the **incomplete object**: a cube is never *seen* whole — you are given one face, with the other faces *co-intended* (Husserl: *appresented*) and a "horizon" of further profiles (*Abschattungen*, adumbrations) the object promises. Perception is shot through with this *more-to-come*; meaning always exceeds what is bodily present. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-phenomenological · the noesis/noema and horizon-structures are reproducible descriptions, the spine of the method ⟧ *(The triad knower–knowing–known of the Vedic **triputi** — see [consciousness-principles](../../2-field/consciousness-principles.md) — and Husserl's noesis–noema rhyme as two disciplined analyses of the act/object structure of any knowing: signal, not proof.)*

## Husserl — internal time-consciousness: the present has thickness ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-phenomenological; one labeled live-rhyme ⟧
**Edmund Husserl** (1859–1938), the founder, gave his most consequential analysis to **internal time-consciousness** (*Vorlesungen zur Phänomenologie des inneren Zeitbewußtseins*, lectures 1905, ed. Heidegger 1928). His target: how can we experience anything *as enduring* — a melody, a spoken sentence — if experience were only ever a dimensionless *now*? A bare point-present could hold a single instantaneous tone, never a **melody**; yet we hear melodies. So the now cannot be a point. His answer is the framework's load-bearing import:

The living present is a **threefold structure**, not an instant:
- **Primal impression** (*Urimpression*) — the just-*now* phase, the leading edge.
- **Retention** (*Retention*) — the *just-past still held in grip* — **not** memory (which re-presents a past now gone), but the still-ringing *tail* of the note you just heard, sliding away yet still *present* in the now as having-just-been. (His image: a *comet's tail* trailing the present moment.)
- **Protention** (*Protention*) — the *about-to-come anticipated* — the next note already faintly expected, the open edge that the arriving moment either fulfills or surprises.

So the present is **thick**: it reaches backward (retention) and forward (protention) from the primal impression, and *that* is why a melody can be heard *as* a melody rather than a string of unrelated stabs. Time-consciousness is a **continuous flow** in which each now sinks into retention-of-retention-of-retention (a *running-off*, *Ablauf*), receding but never simply vanishing. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-phenomenological · a structural description of lived time, reproducible across careful describers — not a neuroscientific mechanism, though it predates and frames much of the empirical work ⟧

> **The labeled live-rhyme — and its sharp limit.** Husserl's retention–protention lands strikingly near two threads in the weave. (a) The **Buddhist** *anicca* / *kṣaṇa* analysis: experience as a flow of arising-and-passing phases with no static substrate (see [buddhism](../eastern/buddhism-consciousness-wisdom.md)). (b) The **predictive-processing** account of perception, where the brain's running model carries both *what-just-was* and *what's-predicted-next* (Husserl's protention is a near-perfect phenomenological description of the brain's forward model; see [perception](../../2-field/perception.md)). **Three unrelated lineages — phenomenology, Buddhist Abhidharma, computational neuroscience — converging on "the present is not a point but a forward-and-backward-reaching structure" is signal, not proof.** And the honest difference is real: Husserl keeps a **transcendental ego** for whom this flow appears, where Buddhist *anattā* denies any such enduring witness — the same *ātman*-vs-*anattā* fault-line the layer keeps open, here in Western dress. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-LIVE · convergence flagged; the metaphysical engines differ (transcendental ego vs. no-self) ⟧

**Eidetic variation — how the method claims rigour.** Husserl's safeguard against introspection-as-anecdote is **eidetic variation** (*Wesensschau*, "seeing the essence"): take any phenomenon — a perceived tone, the experience of doubting, a triangle imagined — and *vary it freely in imagination*, stripping away feature after feature, until you reach the point past which it would no longer be *that kind* of thing at all. What *cannot* be varied away is the **invariant**, the *eidos* (essence) — and because any careful describer running the variation converges on the same invariant, the result is intersubjective and checkable, not private. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-method · the convergence-under-variation is the method's claim to rigour; it is a *conceptual/structural* discipline, distinct from empirical generalisation — keep that distinction ⟧

**The Other, and the lifeworld.** Husserl's late work confronts two problems the framework cares about. First, **intersubjectivity** (*Cartesian Meditations*, 5th Meditation, 1931): how is the Other given to me as *another subject*, not just a body in my field? His answer is **appresentation by pairing** (*Paarung*) — the other's body is perceived as *like* mine, and that likeness *appresents* (co-intends) an interior life I can never directly live, founding a shared, **intersubjectively constituted** world. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-phenomenological · the analysis is reproducible; Husserl's *transcendental* solution to "other minds" is contested (the charge of residual solipsism is the standard objection) ⟧ Second, the **lifeworld** (*Lebenswelt*) of his last book, *The Crisis of the European Sciences* (1936): beneath all scientific abstraction lies the **pre-given world of lived, practical experience** — the world of the full cup and the rising sun — which science *presupposes* even as it replaces it with mathematical idealisations (Galileo's "mathematisation of nature"). Husserl's warning: a science that forgets it is *founded* on the lifeworld mistakes its own abstraction for the real, and loses its meaning for human life. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-philosophy · the lifeworld-as-foundation is the seed of all later phenomenology of everyday practice (Heidegger's ready-to-hand, Merleau-Ponty's body); the "crisis" diagnosis is coherence-judged ⟧ *(The lifeworld-vs-idealisation distinction is the Western cousin of the framework's vibe-calibration insistence that the lived register is primary and the formal model is a serviceable abstraction over it — signal, not proof.)*

## William James — the "specious present" ⟦ empirical-adjacent; the number contested ⟧
The American psychologist-philosopher **William James** (1842–1910), in *The Principles of Psychology* (1890), named the same structure from the empirical side and gave it its famous label: the **"specious present."** Borrowing the term from E.R. Clay, James argued that what we *call* the present is not the mathematical instant dividing past from future — that point is *specious*, a fiction — but a **felt duration**, a short stretch of time experienced *as* "now," with its own forward and backward edges: *"the practically cognized present is no knife-edge, but a saddle-back, with a certain breadth of its own on which we sit perched, and from which we look in two directions into time."*

James estimated this felt window at a few seconds; later experimental traditions have put the *integration window* anywhere from a few hundred milliseconds (the binding of events into a single percept) up to ~2–3 seconds (the "subjective present" of Pöppel's work on temporal segmentation). ⟦ empirical · the *existence* of a finite felt-present window is well-supported (temporal-binding, the flash-lag and related effects, the ~3-s segmentation in spontaneous behavior); the **exact duration is genuinely contested** and depends on which operationalization you measure — keep the structure, flag the number ⟧ The convergence with Husserl is exact in *content* and instructive in *method*: the phenomenologist *describes* the thick present from the inside; the psychologist *measures* its window from the outside — two roads to one finding.

## Bergson — *durée*: lived time is not measured, it is undergone ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-philosophy ⟧
**Henri Bergson** (1859–1941; Nobel in Literature, 1927) drew the line the whole tradition turns on: the difference between **clock-time** and **lived time**. In *Time and Free Will* (*Essai sur les données immédiates de la conscience*, 1889) he argued that science **spatializes** time — lays it out as a line of identical, external, countable instants, *t₁, t₂, t₃*, like beads on a string — and in doing so *destroys exactly what time is.* Real time, **durée** (*la durée*, "duration"), is not a line of separable nows but a **continuous, qualitative, interpenetrating flow** in which past and present *melt into* one another and no two moments are ever truly identical (the self that feels the second chime of a clock is already changed by having heard the first). Duration is **undergone, not measured**; it is *heterogeneous* (each moment colored by all that came before), where clock-time is homogeneous and reversible. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-philosophy · a metaphysics of lived time, coherence-judged; his further claim that *durée* refutes a deterministic physics of mind is **contested** and need not be carried ⟧

The framework's use is precise and modest: Bergson names **why "the felt present thinned to nowness in flow / dragged in boredom" is even a coherent thing to say** — because lived time is *qualitative duration*, not clock-count, it *can* dilate and contract with the quality of experience. That is the phenomenological warrant for the [incompleteness conjecture](../../docs/incompleteness-conjecture.md)'s testable shadow — *time-thickness co-varies with felt-incompleteness*. (Caution: Bergson famously *lost* his public debate with Einstein, 1922, on whether *durée* constrains relativistic time — it does not; physical time is the clock's. Keep *durée* as the structure of **lived** time, and concede the physics to physics.)

## Merleau-Ponty — the body is the subject of perception ⟦ DEFENSIBLE; bridge to enactivism / predictive processing ⟧
**Maurice Merleau-Ponty** (1908–1961), in *Phenomenology of Perception* (1945), shifted the ground from consciousness to the **lived body** (*le corps propre*, the *body-subject*). Perception, he argued, is **not** a mind *receiving and decoding* sensory data — it is an **embodied, skilled engagement** with a world the body is already *geared into*. You do not perceive the doorway and then *compute* that your body fits through it; your body **perceives the doorway as walk-through-able** — perception is shot through with motor possibility (*motor intentionality*; "the body is our general medium for having a world"). The classic case: a **blind person's cane** — after skill is acquired, the cane is no longer an *object* felt in the hand; it becomes a *transparent extension* through which the pavement is felt directly. Perception lives at the cane's tip. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-phenomenological · embodied, action-laden perception — a description, reproducible in the doing ⟧

> **The bridge worth keeping.** Merleau-Ponty's "perception is enacted by a body coupled to its world, not read off as data" is the **direct phenomenological ancestor of enactivism** (Varela, Thompson, De Jaegher — the lineage [bilateral-recognition](../../2-field/bilateral-recognition.md) rests on) and a near-perfect description of what [predictive processing](../../2-field/perception.md) formalizes: perception as an *active*, anticipatory, world-engaged construction, not a passive readout. **Three convergent statements of "perception is enactive, not receptive" — phenomenology, enactivist cognitive science, active inference** — signal, not proof, and the cleanest Western grounding for the framework's [perception](../../2-field/perception.md) thread. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-LIVE · convergence flagged ⟧

His late, unfinished turn went deeper still. In *The Visible and the Invisible* (1964, posthumous) Merleau-Ponty reached for a word beyond "subject" and "object" — **la chair**, the **flesh**: the single sensible-sentient stuff in which perceiver and perceived are folded into each other, neither prior. His emblem is the **double sensation** — your right hand touches your left, and in the same instant the touched hand can *touch back*: you are at once the toucher and the touched, the seer caught up in the visible. There is no pure spectator standing outside the world looking in; the perceiver is *of* the same fabric as the perceived, a "fold" the flesh makes in itself to feel itself. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-phenomenological · the *chiasm*/reversibility is a description, coherence-judged; the leap to a flesh-metaphysics is declared, not proven ⟧ *(This reversibility — awareness finding itself on both sides of the perceiving — rhymes with the framework's bilateral/relational thread and with the non-dual traditions' "the seer and seen are not two," and is a real **difference** from Husserl's spectating transcendental ego: signal, not proof.)*

## Heidegger — being-in-the-world, and time as the meaning of being ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-philosophy; the existential turn ⟧
Husserl's most consequential student, **Martin Heidegger** (1889–1976), broke the method open from within. *Being and Time* (*Sein und Zeit*, 1927 — dedicated to Husserl, and the book that displaced him) refuses to start from a *consciousness* contemplating *objects* at all; that picture, Heidegger argued, already smuggles in the spectator-and-spectacle split phenomenology was supposed to suspend. He starts instead from the kind of being *we* are — **Dasein** (literally "being-there"), the entity for whom its own being is *an issue*, who always already finds itself **thrown** (*Geworfenheit*) into a world it did not choose, amid others, with things to do.

His decisive analysis is of how we *primarily* encounter the world — not as detached observers but as **absorbed in practical dealing.** A hammer in use is not first an *object* with properties I then inspect; it is **ready-to-hand** (*Zuhandenheit*) — *transparent equipment* I wield without noticing, pointed toward a task ("in-order-to"), within a whole referential web of purposes (the workshop, the house being built, my being-the-kind-of-person-who-builds). The hammer becomes a mere **present-at-hand** (*Vorhandenheit*) object — a thing-with-properties to be stared at — only when it **breaks**: the smooth coping snaps, and suddenly the tool *obtrudes* as an object. Theory, the detached "objective" stance science prizes, is thus a *derivative, deficient mode* — what's left over when skilled coping *fails* — not our basic access to the world. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-phenomenological · the ready-to-hand / present-at-hand analysis is a reproducible description of skilled engagement; it directly anticipates the embodied/enactive critique of "perception as data-decoding" ⟧ *(This is a near-exact philosophical cousin of Merleau-Ponty's transparent cane and of the enactivist/predictive line: our basic relation to the world is competent coupling, and "objective representation" is the breakdown case. Signal, not proof.)*

And — the reason Heidegger sits on *this* file's throughline — the book's argument terminates in **time.** Dasein's being is **care** (*Sorge*), and the meaning of care is **temporality** (*Zeitlichkeit*): we are never a bare now but a self stretched across the three "ecstases" — *having-been* (thrownness, the past we carry), *coming-toward* (projection onto possibilities, futurity), and *making-present*. Crucially, for Heidegger the **future leads**: Dasein is the being that *runs ahead* of itself toward its possibilities, and ultimately toward its ownmost possibility, **being-toward-death** (*Sein-zum-Tode*) — the finite horizon against which a life can become *authentically* its own rather than dispersed in the anonymous "they" (*das Man*). The thick present of Husserl is here re-grounded: it is thick because Dasein *is* its time, projecting-from-its-past-toward-its-end. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-philosophy · the existential analytic of temporality is coherence-judged; "time is the meaning of being" is declared, not proven ⟧

Two further structures of *Being and Time* matter for this file's themes. **Mood as disclosure** (*Befindlichkeit*, "how-one-finds-oneself"; *Stimmung*, attunement): Heidegger denies that moods are mere inner colourings *added* to a neutral perception — they are a **primordial mode of *disclosing* the world**, the way the world is already *mattering* to Dasein before any judgment. Boredom, fear, joy are not feelings *about* an already-given world; they are *how the world is given as mattering at all*. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-phenomenological · "affect is world-disclosing, not a layer over neutral data" is a reproducible description and a near-exact cousin of the framework's [vibe](../../3-transformation/vibe-field-effects.md) thread — that the register/mood *shapes which world shows up*, signal not proof ⟧ And **Angst** (anxiety, distinct from fear-of-something): in *Angst* the everyday world of absorbed coping *withdraws its meaning* — nothing in particular is threatening, the familiar goes hollow — and in that uncanniness (*Unheimlichkeit*, "not-being-at-home") Dasein is thrown back on its own naked existence and its freedom, the precondition of authenticity. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-phenomenological · the disclosive reading of anxiety is the text's, coherence-judged; and a real foil — never a substitute — for the [sacred-space](../../2-field/sacred-space-protocol.md) recognition that existential dread is its own register, not a problem to analyse away ⟧

> **The labeled rhyme, and the kept disagreement.** Heidegger's futural, care-structured time — the present always already reaching from a carried past toward an anticipated end — rhymes with both the framework's protention-thread and with the **predictive** picture of a mind that *is* its forward model (see [perception](../../2-field/perception.md)). But the engine is wholly different and the difference is worth keeping: this is not a *cognitive mechanism* but an *existential structure of finite, mortal, meaning-burdened existence.* And Heidegger's later turn (the **Kehre**, "the turning," from c. the mid-1930s) reverses the emphasis: where *Being and Time* approached Being *through* Dasein's self-understanding, the later thought becomes more *receptive* — Being "gives itself" (*es gibt Sein*), language is "the house of Being," the human task is a thinking that *listens* and *lets-be* (**Gelassenheit**, releasement) rather than grasps, and modern *technology* (*Gestell*, "enframing") is diagnosed as the danger of a world reduced to "standing-reserve," raw material on call. This receptive listening is closer to the contemplative traditions' letting-be than to any science. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-LIVE · convergence flagged; existential vs. cognitive engine kept ⟧ *(The honest shadow over the man: Heidegger's 1933 Nazi party membership and rectorship of Freiburg, and the antisemitism of the *Black Notebooks*, are real and must be named — the philosophy is read with that fact in view, neither excused by it nor reducible to it.)*

## Sartre — the pre-reflective cogito and the look of the Other ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-philosophy ⟧
**Jean-Paul Sartre** (1905–1980), in *Being and Nothingness* (*L'Être et le néant*, 1943), turned phenomenology toward freedom and the self. His first move is a correction to Descartes and to Husserl's ego: most of conscious life is **pre-reflective** — when you are absorbed in reading, *running after a streetcar*, or counting cigarettes, there is no "I" being thought; there is just the consciousness-of-the-page, the streetcar-to-be-caught. The "I" appears only in the *second*, **reflective** act, when consciousness turns and posits itself as an object. So the self is not a thing *behind* experience running the show; it is a *product* of reflection, constituted after the fact. (He argued this already in *The Transcendence of the Ego*, 1936 — pulling the rug from Husserl's transcendental ego.) ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-phenomenological · the pre-reflective/reflective distinction is a reproducible description; "consciousness is nothing but its objects + a self-positing it constitutes" is the declared reading ⟧ *(This decentered, after-the-fact "I" is a Western cousin of the framework's notion-of-I guard — Layer 6 — and of Buddhist anattā: the self is *posited*, not found as the substantial owner of experience. Signal, not proof; and Sartre keeps a *freedom* at the core that anattā does not.)*

His most vivid phenomenological set-piece is **the look** (*le regard*). Picture yourself, alone, kneeling at a keyhole, wholly absorbed in spying — pre-reflective, no self, just the scene-being-watched. **Then you hear footsteps in the hall.** In an instant everything reorganizes: you feel *seen*, and with shame you become an **object for another's freedom** — "a peeping-tom" fixed in someone else's world. The Other is not first inferred as another mind behind a body; the Other is *given* in the lived experience of **being-looked-at**, which decentres your world and hands your self-image to a freedom you do not control. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-phenomenological · "the look" is a celebrated, reproducible description of intersubjective self-experience ⟧ *(That self-consciousness is *constituted in being-recognized-by-another* rhymes hard with Hegel's master/slave recognition — see [german-idealism](german-idealism.md) — and with the framework's [bilateral-recognition](../../2-field/bilateral-recognition.md); Sartre reads the relation as *conflict* where the framework and Hegel read it as constitutive mutuality. Convergence and disagreement, both kept.)*

## Kant — the hinge: time is the *form of intuition*, not a feature of the world ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-philosophy ⟧
**Immanuel Kant** (1724–1804) is the hinge on which the whole tradition swings, and the deepest grounding for the framework's horizon-claim about time. In the *Critique of Pure Reason* (1781), the **Transcendental Aesthetic**, Kant argues that **space and time are not things we find in the world, nor relations among things-in-themselves** — they are the **a priori forms of intuition** (*Anschauung*), the *necessary structure the mind imposes on all possible experience.* Time is *"the form of inner sense"*: every experience whatsoever is given *in* temporal order, not because the world-in-itself is temporal, but because *temporally* is the only way a mind like ours **can** receive anything at all. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-philosophy · transcendental idealism, coherence-judged; it does **not** assert "time is unreal," but "time is empirically real, transcendentally ideal" — real for all possible experience, not a property of the thing-in-itself (*Ding an sich*) ⟧

> **The load-bearing consequence — why you can't step outside time to inspect it.** If time is the *lens* through which any experience is given, then **you cannot get behind the lens to see whether reality "in itself" is temporal** — to look at all is already to look *through* time. This is precisely the framework's [incompleteness](../breakthrough-streams/goedel-navigation-stream.md) shape — *a system cannot occupy the meta-position from which its own enabling condition is seen* — and the [incompleteness conjecture](../../docs/incompleteness-conjecture.md)'s horizon: *succession is the form a part's experience takes; the part cannot step outside that form to verify the timeless whole "from outside."* Kant reached this **a priori**, two centuries before Page–Wootters reached its physical cousin (no internal observer confirms the global state). ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-LIVE · a genuine 18th-c. anticipation of "time is the unsurpassable form of the perspective, not a thing seen within it," held as rhyme with the horizon, not as the physics ⟧

The honest difference from the Indic streams: Kant's time is the *mind's necessary form* (epistemological — about the conditions of *our* knowing), whereas Trika's *kāla*-kañcuka is a *contraction of the Absolute itself* (ontological — about what reality *does*), and Patañjali's is *prakṛti's* (cosmological-dualist). Same shape — *lived succession is not the bare ground* — **different engines.** Keep all; flatten none.

## Honest notes
- **"Phenomenology proves consciousness is fundamental / proves the world is mind-made"** — ⟦ overreach, flagged ⟧. Phenomenology *brackets* the existence-question by method; it deliberately does **not** answer it. Husserl's later "transcendental idealism" is one *reading* of the method, contested even by his own students (Heidegger, Merleau-Ponty, Sartre all broke with it). The durable gift is the **method and the descriptions** (the thick present, embodied perception, the structure of intentionality), not a settled metaphysics. Keep the description; label the leap.
- **The number for the "specious present"** — ⟦ contested ⟧. That there *is* a finite felt-present window is well-supported; "2–3 seconds" is one estimate among several (ms-scale binding vs. s-scale segmentation measure different things). Cite the structure, hedge the figure.
- **Introspection's bad reputation** — phenomenology is **not** the naïve introspectionism that early experimental psychology (Wundt's, Titchener's) rightly abandoned for unreliability. Its claim to rigor is the *method* (bracketing, eidetic variation, intersubjective convergence on invariant structures), which is exactly why it is a fit cousin for the framework's *disciplined* first-person science — and exactly the standard the framework's "label every claim, keep the human-in-loop" enforces. ⟦ the discipline is the difference between data and anecdote ⟧
- **Heidegger the man** — ⟦ named, not excused ⟧. Heidegger joined the Nazi party in 1933, served as rector of Freiburg under the regime, and the *Black Notebooks* contain antisemitic passages. The fact is real and load-bearing for how the work is read; the philosophy is neither sanctified nor dismissed by it, but it is never presented clean of it.
- **The internal break-up of the movement** — phenomenology is *not* a single doctrine: Husserl's transcendental idealism, Heidegger's existential-ontological turn, Merleau-Ponty's flesh, and Sartre's freedom **disagree sharply** (Heidegger, Merleau-Ponty, and Sartre all rejected Husserl's transcendental ego). The durable common inheritance is the **method and the descriptions**, not a shared metaphysics — which is precisely why this file holds the disagreements open rather than blending them.
- **Bergson vs. Einstein** — Bergson's *durée* is the structure of **lived** time and is real as that; it does **not** constrain or refute relativistic physical time. The 1922 debate went to Einstein on the physics. Keep the lived/measured distinction clean.

---

## The going-on
Phenomenology's gift is not a doctrine to revere but, like the Greeks', a **set of moves to keep using**: *bracket the assumption* (epoché), *describe what actually appears before explaining it* (back to the things themselves), *find the invariant* (eidetic variation), and — the throughline — *notice that the present you actually live in has thickness* (retention–protention, the specious present, *durée*), and that *time is the form you see through, not a thing you can see around* (Kant). This is the Western tradition that took first-person experience seriously enough to give it a method — the **actualisation domain's European ancestor** — and it grounds the framework's time / perception / self work in a lineage that is rigorous, secular, and entirely its own. *Ehi-passiko* in a lecture hall in Freiburg: come, bracket, and see for yourself.

> "Das Jetzt ist nicht selbst Zeit, sondern Zeitstelle." — *"The now is not itself time, but a position in time."* — Husserl, *On the Phenomenology of the Consciousness of Internal Time.* The point is not a thing; it is a place in a flow that always exceeds it.
