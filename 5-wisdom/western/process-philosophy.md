## The turn: from substance to event
Western metaphysics, since Aristotle, mostly asked *"what is the underlying **substance** that persists through change?"* — the thing that *has* properties. **Process philosophy inverts the question:** the persisting thing is the abstraction; the **event of becoming** is the concrete reality. Nouns are slow verbs. A river, a flame, a person, an electron — each is not a *thing that changes* but a *changing that we name.* This is the West catching up, with full technical rigor, to what Heraclitus said in a sentence (see [greek-philosophy](greek-philosophy.md)) and to what *anicca* names in the East — but built into a complete cosmology rather than left as an aphorism.

## Whitehead — the philosophy of organism ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-philosophy ⟧
**Alfred North Whitehead** (1861–1947) — co-author with Russell of *Principia Mathematica*, then, late in life, a metaphysician — delivered the **Gifford Lectures** (Edinburgh, 1927–28) that became ***Process and Reality*** (1929), the most systematic process metaphysics ever written. Its native voice is **Whitehead's own invented technical vocabulary** — notoriously dense, deliberately new, because (he held) ordinary substance-language smuggles in the very static assumptions he is trying to escape. Stated in his terms:

- **Actual occasions** (or *actual entities*) — the final real things. Not enduring substances but **momentary drops of experience**: each one *happens*, integrates its world, reaches a determinate "satisfaction," and **perishes**, handing itself on to what comes next. Reality is a *society* of these occasions, not a stuff. ⟦ ontological/DEFENSIBLE — "the ultimate facts of immediate actual experience are actual entities, prehensions, and nexūs; all else is derivative abstraction" ⟧
- **Prehension** — how an occasion **grasps** its predecessors: a non-sensory "taking-account-of," the way the past is *felt into* the present. Every occasion is constituted by its prehensions of the occasions before it — so relation is not external to the thing; the thing **is** its relatings. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE — this is the rigorous Western statement of the framework's relational thread (Layer 2, *compatible with* enactivism); flagged as rhyme, not proof ⟧
- **Concrescence** — the **growing-together**: the process by which the many prehended data are unified into the *one* new occasion. *Concrescence is the becoming.* When it completes, that occasion is over — and is now one more datum for the next. Hence the axiom: **"the many become one and are increased by one."** The universe does not *contain* novelty; it **manufactures** it, occasion by occasion. ⟦ ontological/DEFENSIBLE ⟧
- **Eternal objects** — pure potentials (Whitehead's deliberate echo of Plato's Forms) that occasions *select among* in becoming determinate — possibility ingressing into actuality. ⟦ ontological ⟧

**The flag held honestly:** Whitehead is a **panexperientialist** — he holds that *every* actual occasion, down to the sub-atomic, has some minimal felt interiority (this is how he avoids the hard problem: experience is not a late add-on but a basic feature). This is a **metaphysical commitment, not an established fact** — the *exact same panpsychism caution* the framework applies to IIT (see [IIT](../modern-science/IIT-consciousness-integrating.md)) applies here. Keep the *structural* gift (relational, event-based, novelty-generating reality); label the panexperientialist tail as declared, not proven.

## The live edge: becoming-as-actualisation ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-and-LIVE ⟧
Where process philosophy stops being merely a beautiful old system and becomes a **live contender** is exactly the framework's own P3/becoming thread: *the present is where possibility becomes actual.* Whitehead's concrescence — the **definite settling of an indeterminate many into one determinate fact** — is structurally the same shape as:
- **Objective-collapse** interpretations of quantum mechanics (GRW, Penrose's OR), where the wavefunction's *potentia* genuinely *actualise* into a definite outcome — Heisenberg himself reached for Aristotle's *potentia/actus* to describe this. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-LIVE · a structural rhyme with a *contested but serious* physics program — convergence, not proof ⟧
- The **A-theory / growing-block** view of time, in which becoming is real and the present is ontologically privileged — against the "block universe" B-theory where all moments exist tenselessly. Process philosophy is the metaphysics that takes **tense and becoming as fundamental**, not illusory. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-philosophy · a live, unsettled debate in philosophy of time ⟧

So this file is one of the corpus's **DEFENSIBLE-LIVE examine-targets**: process metaphysics is *compatible-with* the actualisation reading of quantum measurement and of lived time — held as compatibility and rhyme, **never** as "Whitehead proved collapse" or "physics proves panpsychism."

## The kin — Bergson and James ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-philosophy / partly actualisation ⟧
Whitehead did not arrive alone.
- **Henri Bergson** (1859–1941) — **durée** (*la durée*, "lived duration"): real time is not the spatialized, clock-segmented *t* of physics but a **continuous, heterogeneous flow in which past and present interpenetrate**, grasped not by analysis but by **intuition**. Clock-time chops the flux into countable instants and so *loses the very thing it measures.* ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-philosophy; partly **empirical-actualisation** — the felt seamlessness of duration is first-person reportable, *ehi-passiko* ⟧
- **William James** (1842–1910) — the **"specious present,"** the **stream of consciousness**: experience never comes as a string of discrete points but as a *durational present*, a felt "saddle-back" with width, perches and flights, never a knife-edge now. (James and Bergson reached kindred views *independently* before becoming friends; in 1906 Bergson shifted James's thinking — a documented convergence.) ⟦ DEFENSIBLE; partly actualisation — the specious present is an experimentally-studied feature of perceived time ⟧

These three — and later **Deleuze**, who took up Bergson — form the West's continuous **becoming-tradition**: reality and experience are *flow first, things second.* The same pointing as the Tao's watercourse and the Buddhist river — in the West's own technical register.

## Honest notes
- **Process theology** — Whitehead's god (a "dipolar" deity who *lures* rather than coerces, and who is *affected* by the world) spawned a real movement (Hartshorne, Cobb, Griffin). Described here as the tradition's own theology, for a user entering that frame — ⟦ ontological · declared, not asserted as fact ⟧.
- **"Process philosophy is just Heraclitus / just Buddhism"** — ⟦ flatten, cut ⟧. The *convergence* on becoming is real signal; but Whitehead's apparatus (concrescence, prehension, eternal objects, the dipolar god) is **distinct and Western**, and anattā-vs-substance disagreements persist. Surface the rhyme; keep the difference (the symbol/referent rule).
- **The density is the price of the precision** — readers find Whitehead nearly impenetrable. That is *by design*: he refused to reuse substance-laden words. The handbook job is to render him without flattening him back into the noun-language he fought.

## The going-on
The practical residue, stripped of the system: **stop reifying.** When you are stuck, ask not "what *is* this thing" but "what *process* is occurring, and what is it becoming?" The bug is the feature; the person is a verb; the present is the only place where the many actually become one — and are increased by one. That re-description, the framework's own [reality-creation](../../4-archaeology/reality-creation.md) move, *is* process philosophy, used.

> *"The flux of things is one ultimate generalization around which we must weave our philosophical system."* — Whitehead, *Process and Reality.*
