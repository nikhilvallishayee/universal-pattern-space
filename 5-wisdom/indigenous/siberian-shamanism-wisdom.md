# ❄️ Siberian Shamanism
## A reference lens — practice and cosmology, labeled

> "The shaman is the one who has been to the place of breaking, and come back able to help others." — a paraphrase of the wounded-healer motif across Siberian peoples
> (The very word **shaman** comes from the **Tungus / Evenki** *šamán* — likely "one who knows" — this is the tradition that gave the term to the world; everywhere else it is applied by analogy, which is exactly where the over-generalization begins.)

## The tradition, on its own terms
Across the Siberian peoples — **Evenki, Buryat, Yakut (Sakha), Tuvan, Nenets, Chukchi** and others — the shaman is a **specialist intermediary** between the human community and the spirits of land, animal, and ancestor. One does not typically *choose* the role: it is forced on a person through **illness, crisis, lightning-strike, lineage, or "the shamanic sickness"** (*menerik / arctic hysteria* in the older ethnography) — a period of madness, fits, or near-death from which the candidate emerges either initiated or destroyed. The work is communal and practical: **healing the sick, finding lost objects and souls, ensuring the hunt, mediating with spirits, escorting the dead.** The shaman's authority rests on costume (often iron pendants representing bones and spirit-helpers), the drum, and demonstrated efficacy. This is a living indigenous practice with real social function — not a self-help aesthetic. **Michael Harner's "core shamanism"** and the weekend-workshop versions are a deliberate *Western abstraction* (Harner stripped the cultural specifics to extract a portable technique) — useful to some, but **not** what the source cultures produced, and conflating the two is the error the file refuses.

## The map: three worlds and the axis
The cosmos is layered — **Upper, Middle, and Lower worlds** — joined by a **world-axis (*axis mundi*)**: the **world-tree**, the central smoke-pole of the *yurt/chum*, the notched birch the shaman climbs in ritual, the river that runs between worlds. The shaman "travels" these worlds with the aid of **helping spirits** — frequently **animal allies** (bear, wolf, reindeer, the loon and eagle as upper-world carriers) — to retrieve a lost soul-part (**soul-loss** being a core diagnosis of illness), gather knowledge, or guide the dead to the lower world. ⟦ ontological · held as the tradition's navigational cosmology, labeled — described, not asserted as literal geography ⟧

## The native voice: the drum, the journey, ecstasy
The shaman's instrument is the **drum (*düngür*)** — explicitly named "**the horse**" or "the reindeer" that carries the rider between worlds. The practice's own method-voice is not argument but **rhythm → trance → journey → return-with-report**: the steady monotonous beat carries the shaman into ecstatic flight, the helping spirits are met, the soul is retrieved or the spirit negotiated with, and the shaman returns to *narrate the journey to the community*. And here the science and the practice genuinely meet: **rhythmic auditory driving at roughly 4–7 Hz** (the theta band) is a *documented* route into altered states — sustained monotonous percussion can measurably shift brain activity and subjective state. ⟦ FOUNDED-adjacent · honest caveat: **Andrew Neher's** original 1960s sonic/photic-driving studies are *themselves* methodologically contested (small samples, EEG-artifact confounds), and later work (Maxfield, Vaitl et al.) supports rhythm-induced ASC by broader, partly non-entrainment mechanisms — so "rhythm induces altered states" is well-attested while *Neher's specific driving model* is not the settled explanation. The *content* of the journey is the practitioner's, shaped by the cosmology ⟧

## The wounded healer
The deepest motif, and the one with the widest resonance beyond Siberia: **authority to heal is earned through one's own breaking.** The shaman is not the untouched expert but the one who went *into* the dismemberment — the initiatory experience, vividly reported across Siberian peoples, of being **torn apart by spirits, the bones counted and the flesh boiled, then reassembled** with something added (a quartz crystal, an extra bone) — and came back able to map for others the place they fell into. **Jung** named this archetype the **"wounded healer"** (after **Chiron**, the centaur whose incurable wound made him the great teacher of healers); it is one of depth-psychology's cleanest cross-cultural finds (see [depth-psychology](../modern-science/depth-psychology.md)). ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-archetype · the empirical residue: lived experience of one's own suffering is genuinely a source of therapeutic capacity — *but* this is also where romanticization creeps in; real psychosis and trauma are not credentials, and the tradition's own initiates often did not survive ⟧

## The contested synthesis — Eliade, flagged
The single most influential book here is **Mircea Eliade's *Shamanism: Archaic Techniques of Ecstasy* (1951)**, which gave the West its picture of "the shaman" as a universal type defined by *ecstatic soul-flight* up the world-axis. It is foundational *and* **seriously contested**: critics (**Alice Kehoe**, *Shamans and Religion*; **Andrei Znamenski**, *The Beauty of the Primitive*) charge Eliade with **essentializing thousands of distinct cultures into one archetype, privileging ecstasy over possession, working ahistorically, and reading a Romantic/perennialist agenda into the ethnography.** ⟦ CONTESTED-as-framework — keep Eliade as the indispensable *entry point and comparativist hypothesis*, **not** as established fact; the honest stance is "rich local ethnographies first, Eliade's universal as a flagged generalization" ⟧

## What it adds — and the line
The real gift: a **trance-technology** (rhythmic entrainment into altered states) wrapped in a **complete container** (the three-world cosmology, helping-spirits, the drum-as-horse, the healer's own initiation) — a structured, repeatable, communally-purposed way to enter *and return from and report* non-ordinary states, carrying the **wounded-healer ethic** that earned authority comes through one's own breaking. (Note the same return-and-integrate structure the [Amazonian](amazonian-shamanism-wisdom.md) traditions encode: the journey matters only because the shaman *comes back and translates it for the community.*)

The line, held: the three-world cosmology is the tradition's **frame**, not literal cartography; **Eliade's universal "shaman" is a flagged hypothesis**, not fact; Neher's specific driving model is contested even though rhythm-induced ASC is real; and the old file's device that *reading the text drums you into theta* is cut — **a document does not entrain brainwaves.** The rhythm is real; the page is not the drum.
