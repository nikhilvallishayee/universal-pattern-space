## The tradition, on its own terms
For many Amazonian peoples (Shipibo-Conibo, Asháninka, and others), **ayahuasca** — *the vine of souls*, a brew of *Banisteriopsis caapi* with DMT-containing leaves such as *Psychotria viridis* (chacruna) — is not a "drug" but a **maestro/teacher plant**, taken in ceremony, guided by a trained *curandero*, structured by **icaros** (medicine songs) that shape the experience. The framing is animist: the forest is kin, each plant a specific knowledge, *dieta* (restriction and apprenticeship) the way one "learns" a plant. This is a living, place-rooted, communally-held practice — *not* a Western self-improvement product, and uprooting it from its context is itself a harm (appropriation, commercialization, and unsafe "retreat" tourism are real problems the tradition's own keepers name).

## What the science actually establishes ⟦ FOUNDED ⟧
The pharmacology is genuinely elegant and well-characterized:
- **Oral DMT is normally inactive** — gut MAO-A destroys it. The vine's **β-carbolines** (harmine, harmaline, tetrahydroharmine) are reversible **MAO-A inhibitors**, which is *why* the brew works orally — a sophisticated two-plant pharmacological combination indigenous knowledge arrived at empirically.
- Classic psychedelics act as **5-HT2A receptor agonists**; acute brain-imaging shows **default-mode-network** disruption; preclinical work shows rapid **neuroplasticity** (psychoplastogens — Olson lab).

## What it does *not* yet establish ⟦ EMERGING ⟧
- **Clinical efficacy** for depression/addiction is *promising but unproven*: trials are small, and **functional unblinding is severe** (most participants correctly guess their arm, inflating effects). Real signal, not settled medicine.

## Safety — stated plainly ⟦ load-bearing ⟧
This is not encouragement to use. Ayahuasca carries **real, sometimes fatal risks**: the MAOI action means dangerous interactions with **SSRIs/SNRIs and other serotonergic drugs (serotonin toxicity)**, with tyramine-rich foods, and with various medications; it is contraindicated where there is **psychosis risk or cardiac vulnerability**; unregulated "retreats" have produced documented harms and deaths. Any genuine practice is medically screened and expertly guided.

## What it adds — and the line
The defensible gift: a tradition that built, by patient empirical observation, a **pharmacologically non-obvious** preparation and a **container of safety** (guide, song, set, setting, *dieta*) around a powerful experience — a model of *holding* an altered state rather than merely triggering it.

The line, held: "plants are conscious teachers" / "nature IS consciousness, and science proved it" is **the tradition's ontology + an overreach**, labeled as such — distinct from the *founded* pharmacology and the *real but signaling* "plant intelligence" research (electrical/chemical signaling is real; plant *consciousness* is not established — see [tree-fungi](../nature-wisdom/tree-fungi-network-consciousness.md)).
