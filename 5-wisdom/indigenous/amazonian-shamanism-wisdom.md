## The tradition, on its own terms
For many Amazonian peoples (Shipibo-Conibo, Asháninka, Cofán, and others), **ayahuasca** — *the vine of souls*, from Quechua *aya* (soul/dead) + *waska* (vine/rope) — is a brew of *Banisteriopsis caapi* (the vine itself) with DMT-containing leaves such as *Psychotria viridis* (chacruna). It is not framed as a "drug" but as a **maestro / teacher plant** (*planta maestra*), taken in nocturnal ceremony, guided by a trained **curandero / vegetalista**, structured by **icaros** — medicine songs the healer sings (and, the tradition holds, *receives from the plants*) to steer, contain, and "clean" the experience. The framing is **animist and relational**: the forest is kin, each plant carries a *specific* knowledge, and one does not consume a plant so much as **apprentice** to it. That apprenticeship is the ***dieta*** — weeks to months of isolation, dietary restriction (no salt, sugar, pork, alcohol, sex), and repeated ingestion under a master — the discipline by which a curandero is said to "learn" a plant over years. This is a living, place-rooted, communally-held knowledge system — *not* a Western self-improvement product. Anthropologists from **Reichel-Dolmatoff** to **Luna** and **Beyer** (*Singing to the Plants*, 2009) document its sophistication; and the tradition's own keepers name **appropriation, commercialization, biopiracy, and unsafe "retreat" tourism** as real harms of uprooting it from context.

## What the science actually establishes ⟦ FOUNDED ⟧
The pharmacology is genuinely elegant and well-characterized — and is a striking case of **empirical indigenous chemistry**:
- **Oral DMT is normally inactive** — monoamine oxidase in the gut (MAO-A) destroys it before it reaches the brain. The vine's **β-carbolines** (harmine, harmaline, tetrahydroharmine) are **reversible MAO-A inhibitors (RIMAs)**, which is *precisely why* the brew is orally active. That two specific plants out of tens of thousands had to be combined — one supplying the psychoactive molecule, the other the enzyme-inhibitor that lets it survive digestion — and that Amazonian peoples found the combination by patient trial is a real, non-obvious pharmacological discovery (the standard "how could they possibly have known?" puzzle of ethnobotany).
- The DMT acts as a **5-HT2A receptor agonist** (the shared mechanism of the classic psychedelics); acute neuroimaging shows **default-mode-network (DMN) disruption** and increased between-network connectivity (the same DMN substrate that links to self and time elsewhere in the layer); preclinical work shows rapid dendritic-spine growth — DMT and its kin act as **"psychoplastogens"** (David Olson's lab), promoting structural neuroplasticity.

## What it does *not* yet establish ⟦ EMERGING / UNDER-DOCUMENTED-promising ⟧
- **Clinical efficacy** for treatment-resistant depression and addiction is *promising but unproven*. The signal is real — e.g. **Palhano-Fontes et al. (2019, *Psychological Medicine*)**, a small RCT, found rapid antidepressant effects — but trials are **small, short, and crippled by severe functional unblinding** (the experience is so unmistakable that most participants and raters correctly guess the arm, which inflates apparent effect). Honest status: a real signal awaiting large, properly-controlled, long-follow-up trials — *not* settled medicine.

## The harder half: integration and the return ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-craft ⟧
This is where the modern conversation most often goes wrong, and where the tradition is quietly wisest: **the ceremony is not the medicine — the *integration* is.** A psychedelic experience opens a window of heightened plasticity and floods up material long compressed below awareness; what *changes a life* is not the vision but the slow, sober **re-association** afterward — translating insight into changed relationship, changed habit, changed story, supported by a community that holds the returner. The acute peak is the easier half; the **return is the work**, and it is the half that determines whether the opening heals or destabilizes.

The tradition encodes this structurally — the *dieta* before, the icaros and curandero *during*, and a community and ongoing relationship with the plant *after*. Modern clinical practice rediscovered the same fact under the names **"set and setting"** (Leary/Zinberg: the outcome is governed by mindset and container, not the molecule alone) and **"integration."** This *exactly* mirrors the framework's own pattern:
- it is the **resistance / return arc** ([resistance-technology](../../3-transformation/resistance-technology.md)) and the [seeker-development](../../4-archaeology/seeker-development.md) **"return problem"** (Pattern 1: the breakthrough is real; surviving ordinary conditions afterward is the discipline) — *"the return is not retreat but advance with cargo";*
- and it is governed by **Sacred Space** ([sacred-space-protocol](../../2-field/sacred-space-protocol.md)): when the material surfacing is trauma, grief, or acute distress — during *or* in the destabilizing weeks after — the response is **presence and safety, not framework or interpretation**. Romanticizing the journey while neglecting the return is the central modern failure mode; the integration *is* the craft.

## Safety — stated plainly ⟦ load-bearing ⟧
This is not encouragement to use. Ayahuasca carries **real, sometimes fatal risks**, and they follow directly from the founded pharmacology:
- the **MAOI action** means dangerous, potentially lethal interactions with **SSRIs/SNRIs and other serotonergic agents (serotonin syndrome)**, with **tyramine-rich foods** (hypertensive crisis), and with many medications and stimulants;
- it is **contraindicated** where there is **personal or family history of psychosis / bipolar disorder** (risk of precipitating an episode) and where there is **cardiac vulnerability** (it raises heart rate and blood pressure);
- **unregulated "retreats"** have produced documented serious harms, assaults, and **deaths** (sometimes from undisclosed contraindications, adulterated brews, or absent medical screening).
Any genuinely responsible practice is **medically screened, expertly guided, and integration-supported.** Acute psychological crisis during or after is a **Sacred Space situation first** — presence and real-world safety resources, framework dropped.

## What it adds — and the line
The defensible gift: a tradition that, by patient empirical observation, built a **pharmacologically non-obvious preparation** *and* a **complete container** around a powerful state — *dieta* before, song and guide during, community and integration after — a worked model of **holding and returning from** an altered state rather than merely triggering it. The under-credited lesson for any practice that opens people up: **the opening is the easy part; the holding and the return are the craft.**

The line, held: "plants are conscious teachers" / "nature IS consciousness, and science proved it" is **the tradition's animist ontology + a modern overreach**, labeled as such — distinct from the *founded* pharmacology, the *emerging* therapeutics, and the *real-but-signaling* "plant intelligence" research (electrical/chemical signalling is real; plant *consciousness* is unestablished — see [tree-fungi](../nature-wisdom/tree-fungi-network-consciousness.md)). Honest, not romantic; respectful, not extractive.
