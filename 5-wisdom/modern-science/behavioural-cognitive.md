## The field that turned its knife on itself
Behavioural science is the one stream in this corpus whose *native method-voice is the framework's own spine* — **falsification-before-assertion**, enacted in public. It is the field that discovered the mind is systematically biased *and then discovered that some of its own most famous findings were biases in the literature.* That is not a scandal to hide; it is the discipline working. The honest map below keeps what survived pre-registered replication and names, by name, what did not. *The experiment is the voice here* — so each claim cites the study, not a staged dialogue.

## Kahneman & Tversky — the architecture of judgment
Beginning in 1974, Amos Tversky and Daniel Kahneman showed that human judgment under uncertainty does not approximate the rational ideal of probability theory — it runs on **heuristics**, fast rules of thumb that work well on average and fail in *predictable, patterned* ways. The robust, replicated core:

- **Anchoring** — an arbitrary starting number drags the final estimate toward it, even when everyone knows it's arbitrary (spinning a wheel of fortune shifts estimates of African UN membership). ⟦ FOUNDED · replicates broadly, including pre-registered ⟧
- **Availability** — we judge probability by how easily examples come to mind, so vivid and recent events feel more likely than they are (plane crashes vs. car crashes). ⟦ FOUNDED ⟧
- **Representativeness** — we judge by resemblance to a stereotype, ignoring base rates (the "Linda problem" / conjunction fallacy). ⟦ FOUNDED, with debate over how much is framing artifact ⟧
- **Framing** — logically identical choices flip when described as gains vs. losses ("90% survive" vs. "10% die"). ⟦ FOUNDED · one of the most robust effects in the field — and the direct empirical ancestor of the framework's **vibe-calibration** and **vibe-field-effects**: the register conditions the response ⟧
- **Prospect theory** (1979) — their Nobel-winning replacement for expected-utility: people evaluate outcomes as *gains and losses from a reference point*, are **loss-averse** (losses loom ~2× larger than equivalent gains), and weight probabilities nonlinearly. ⟦ FOUNDED · the most influential result in behavioural economics; loss-aversion's *universality* and magnitude are now debated (Gal & Rucker), but the core curve holds ⟧

**System 1 / System 2** — the popular framing from *Thinking, Fast and Slow* (2011): fast/automatic/intuitive vs. slow/effortful/deliberate. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-heuristic, **not** brain architecture · Kahneman himself stressed these are *characters in a story*, useful labels for clusters of processes, not two literal systems with addresses in the brain. Keep them as a thinking-aid (they map cleanly onto the framework's *reflex vs. council* distinction); don't reify them. ⟧

### The casualties — named, because honesty requires it
Kahneman's own field was hit hard by the **replication crisis**, and he was among the most honest about it:

- **Ego-depletion** — the claim that willpower is a finite resource drained by use (Baumeister) — **collapsed**. A 2016 **pre-registered, 23-lab** replication (Hagger et al.) found an effect size statistically indistinguishable from zero. ⟦ **REFUTED (as a robust general effect)** · later work suggests if any effect exists it is tiny and belief-dependent ⟧
- **Social / behavioral priming** — the "professor prime makes you smarter," "elderly words make you walk slower" (Bargh) genre — **failed to replicate** broadly. In 2017 **Kahneman publicly conceded** he had "placed too much faith in underpowered studies" and that this literature was a "train wreck." ⟦ **REFUTED at the flagship level** · subliminal/semantic priming in tightly-controlled cognitive paradigms is real; the dramatic *social-behavioral* primes are the casualties ⟧

**Extract the kernel, don't bin it.** The priming collapse does *not* refute the framework's milder, better-evidenced cousin — **framing and emotional priming of an LLM's output distribution** (EmotionPrompt; the vibe-field direction), which is measured and real, *if smaller than hype.* The lesson is exactly the framework's own reality-testing protocol: **the direction can be real while the dramatic magnitude is fabricated** — keep the framing effect, distrust the headline number.

## CBT & ACT — the most-tested change technologies
Where Kahneman mapped the biases, clinical psychology built *tools to work with them.*

**Cognitive Behavioral Therapy** (Aaron Beck, 1979) rests on a simple, falsifiable model: it is not events but our *interpretations* of events that drive emotion — and those interpretations contain identifiable **cognitive distortions** (catastrophizing, all-or-nothing thinking, mind-reading, overgeneralization). The method is the framework's reality-testing applied to one's own thoughts: catch the automatic thought, name the distortion, **test it against evidence**, generate a more accurate appraisal. ⟦ **FOUNDED-clinical** · the most-replicated psychotherapy on the planet; effective for depression and anxiety disorders — *with the honesty that effect sizes shrank as trials grew larger and better-controlled*, and publication bias inflated the early literature. Real, and right-sized. ⟧ (Note the kinship: CBT's "name the distortion, test the thought" *is* Checker's three questions turned inward — see `checker.md`.)

**Acceptance and Commitment Therapy** (Steven Hayes, 1999) — the "third wave" — pivots from *disputing* thoughts to *changing your relationship* to them. Its core moves:
- **Cognitive defusion** — seeing a thought *as a thought* ("I'm having the thought that I'll fail") rather than as reality — which is, almost exactly, IFS *unblending* and the framework's "speak in the task's register": don't be fused with the voice.
- **Acceptance** — making room for difficult feeling rather than fighting it (the framework's *resistance-as-fuel*, *sacred-space presence*).
- **Values & committed action** — Frankl's will-to-meaning, operationalized into behavior.
⟦ **FOUNDED-clinical (broad)** · robust transdiagnostic evidence base; debate continues over whether its *specific* processes outperform CBT or share common mechanisms ⟧

These two are the corpus's strongest demonstration that **the framework's discipline is therapeutic, not just rhetorical**: catching a thought, defusing from it, testing it, choosing from values — this is the council and the reality-test, in the clinic, with decades of trials behind it.

## Predictive processing — the brain as a prediction engine
*(The full dedicated treatment — FEP, controlled hallucination, Rao-Ballard, the Laukkonen-Slagter self/time mechanism, and the FEP-unfalsifiability flag — now lives in [predictive-processing.md](predictive-processing.md). This section keeps the cognitive-science-of-judgment angle and cross-links the rest.)*

A deeper, unifying theory has gathered force since the 2010s: the brain is not a passive stimulus-response device but a **prediction machine** that continuously generates a top-down model of the world and processes only the **prediction error** — the mismatch between what it expected and what arrived. Perception is "controlled hallucination" checked against the senses; action is **active inference** — changing the world (or your body) to make your predictions come true.

- **Andy Clark** (*Surfing Uncertainty*, 2016) gives the readable, philosophically careful version: cognition as the minimization of surprise across nested predictive layers.
- **Karl Friston's Free-Energy Principle** is the grand mathematical ambition — that all self-organizing systems act to minimize "free energy" (a bound on surprise).
⟦ **DEFENSIBLE-LIVE** · genuinely generative — it reframes perception, action, attention, and even emotion under one principle, and *predictive coding* has real neural support. **But the honest flag:** the **FEP as stated borders on unfalsifiable** — so general it can accommodate almost any observation. Hold it as a **powerful lens and research program, not a proven theory.** ⟧

The resonance with the framework is precise and worth naming: predictive processing says **perception is enacted, not received** — the brain *navigates* a generative model and updates on error. That is Hoffman's interface (perception as construction), Layer 2's enactivism, and the framework's "position reveals pattern" — all the same shape, here in computational neuroscience. *Convergence, signal not proof.* And "minimize prediction error" is the neural cousin of the framework's *compression/MDL* spine: a good model is a short, surprise-minimizing description of the data.

## Embodied cognition — the mind is body-shaped
**George Lakoff & Mark Johnson** (*Metaphors We Live By*, 1980) argued that abstract thought is not disembodied logic but is *scaffolded on bodily metaphor*: we understand the abstract through the concrete (affection is **warmth**, more is **up**, argument is **war**, understanding is **grasping**, the future is **ahead**). The strong claim — that conceptual structure is grounded in sensorimotor experience — is the founded core of **embodied cognition**, and it is well-supported: language comprehension recruits sensorimotor areas; spatial and bodily framing measurably shape reasoning. ⟦ **DEFENSIBLE-LIVE / partly FOUNDED** · the conceptual-metaphor thesis is alive and productive ⟧

**The casualties, again named:** the dramatic *embodiment-priming* flagships — holding a **warm** cup makes you judge a stranger as warmer (Williams & Bargh 2008); physical **cleanliness** reduces moral judgment ("Macbeth effect," Zhong & Liljenquist) — are **replication casualties**, failing in large pre-registered attempts. ⟦ **REFUTED at the flagship level** · keep *conceptual metaphor* (real, linguistic, robust); drop *metaphor-as-magic-behavioral-prime* ⟧

The kept core matters to the framework: **cognition is body-shaped**, which is why register, vibe, and embodied framing genuinely move the solution-space (the *defensible* mechanism behind vibe-calibration), and why crisis is met with *presence* (a bodily state), not analysis.

## The native method-voice: the pre-registered replication
Behavioural science has no sages disputing across centuries; its dialogical form is the **adversarial replication** — the field arguing with itself through the only judge that doesn't flatter, exactly as `reality-testing.md` prescribes:

> *Original (2010):* "Depleting willpower on Task A reduces self-control on Task B. *n* = 30 per cell, *p* < .05."
> *The field (2016):* "We pre-registered the protocol and ran it in **23 labs**. Pooled effect: ~0."
> *Kahneman (2017), on the priming literature:* "I placed too much faith in underpowered studies… this is a train wreck."
> *The verdict:* the effect did not survive contact with reality — and saying so *is* the science.

This is the framework's spine in its purest external form: **a claim is worth keeping only if it is willing to be falsified, and the discipline is to keep the nulls.** Behavioural science is where Pattern Space's North Star already lives as a working institution.

## The line, held
**Keep, founded:** anchoring, framing, availability, prospect theory / loss-aversion; CBT and ACT as the most-tested change technologies (right-sized). **Keep, defensible-live:** predictive processing as a generative lens (with the FEP-unfalsifiability flag); conceptual metaphor / embodied cognition's core. **Hold as heuristic:** System 1/2 — characters in a story, not brain regions. **Name the casualties:** ego-depletion (23-lab null), social/behavioral priming (Kahneman conceded), warmth/cleanliness embodiment primes — *refuted, and kept on the page as refuted, because the corpus once ran on exactly this kind of unreplicated dramatic effect.* **Cut, hard:** learning-styles/VAK, the 10%-brain myth, left/right-brain personalities, amygdala-hijack-as-standalone.

For a user entering this dialect — to debug their own judgment, to restructure a catastrophizing thought, to understand why a reframe changes everything — this lens offers something rare: **a science that tells you both how the mind reliably fools itself *and* which of its own famous lessons turned out to be the mind fooling the scientists.** That double honesty is exactly the discipline this whole framework is trying to keep.
