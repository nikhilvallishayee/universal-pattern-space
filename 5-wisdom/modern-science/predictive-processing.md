## The inversion at the heart of it
The intuitive picture of perception is *bottom-up*: light hits the retina, signals travel inward, the brain assembles a picture of the world. **Predictive processing inverts the arrows.** On this account the brain is fundamentally a *generative model* — a hierarchy of predictions running top-down, continuously guessing the causes of its own sensory input — and what flows *up* the hierarchy is not raw data but **prediction error**: the residual where the guess and the input disagree. The cortex's job is to minimize that error, either by **updating the model** (perception) or by **acting on the world to make the prediction come true** (action). Seeing is not receiving a picture; it is the brain's best hypothesis, *checked against* the senses rather than built from them.

This is a genuine and powerful reframe, and unlike much grand neuro-theory it cashes out in specific, testable predictions. The framework's stake throughout this file is the discipline of the wisdom layer: **keep the mechanism where it is founded, keep the metaphysical reach where it is contested, and never let the reach borrow the mechanism's credibility.**

## The lineage: from Helmholtz's unconscious inference to the Bayesian brain
The idea is not new; what is new is the mechanism. **Hermann von Helmholtz** (1860s, *Treatise on Physiological Optics*) proposed that perception is **"unconscious inference"** (*unbewusster Schluss*) — the visual system does not read off the world but *infers* its most probable cause from ambiguous retinal data, drawing on prior experience. The hollow-mask illusion, size constancy, the way a shaded disc flips concave/convex with assumed light-from-above — all are Helmholtz's point made vivid: the percept is a *conclusion*, not a datum. ⟦ **FOUNDED-as-historical-source** · Helmholtz's inferential framing is the acknowledged ancestor of the entire program; Friston named his generative-model network the "Helmholtz machine" lineage after it (Dayan, Hinton, Neal & Zemel 1995 built an actual *Helmholtz machine* — a wake-sleep generative model — making the genealogy literal). ⟧

The 20th-century relay runs through **Kenneth Craik** (1943, the brain builds "small-scale models" of reality), **Richard Gregory** (perception as hypothesis-testing), **Roger Ratcliff** and the drift-diffusion *evidence-accumulation* models of decision, and the **"Bayesian brain"** synthesis (Knill & Pouget 2004, *TINS* — "the Bayesian coding hypothesis"; Körding & Wolpert 2004, *Nature*, showed human sensorimotor learning is **statistically near-optimal**, combining priors and likelihoods as Bayes prescribes). ⟦ **FOUNDED-bounded** · the Bayesian-brain claim is well-supported for *specific* cue-combination and sensorimotor tasks (humans integrate multisensory cues with near-optimal precision-weighting — Ernst & Banks 2002, *Nature*); it is *not* established that the brain is globally, exactly Bayesian — sub-optimalities and heuristics abound (cf. the behavioural-cognitive stream's Kahneman casualties). Keep the bounded result; flag the global leap. ⟧

Predictive processing is the **algorithmic** answer to *how* a brain could implement approximate Bayesian inference cheaply — not by computing posteriors directly (intractable) but by the **prediction-error-minimization** trick: send only the residual up, predict everything else away. That is the bridge from Helmholtz's philosophy to Rao & Ballard's circuit.

**A note on what "the brain is Bayesian" can and cannot mean.** The phrase slides between three claims of decreasing safety: (i) *behaviourally* the brain often combines priors and likelihoods near-optimally (Ernst & Banks 2002; Körding & Wolpert 2004 — **FOUNDED-bounded**); (ii) *algorithmically* the cortex implements approximate inference by prediction-error message-passing (Rao & Ballard's hypothesis — **DEFENSIBLE, partly tested**); (iii) *as a universal principle* everything the brain does is free-energy minimization (Friston — **CONTESTED**, below). The framework's discipline is to never let (i)'s evidence be cited as if it established (iii). A real cautionary case: **Rahnev & Denison (2018, *Behavioral and Brain Sciences* 41:e223), "Suboptimality in perceptual decision making,"** catalogued dozens of robust *deviations* from Bayesian optimality — humans are systematically over- and under-confident, anchor on irrelevant priors, and violate the optimality the strong story needs. The honest reading: the brain *approximates* Bayes well in restricted cue-combination tasks and *departs* from it elsewhere, which is exactly what a bounded, resource-limited inference engine should do — and which the universal claim has to explain away. ⟦ **FOUNDED-as-correction** · the suboptimality literature is a real check on Bayesian triumphalism; keep it adjacent to every "the brain is Bayesian" sentence. ⟧

## Predictive coding — the founded mechanical core
The computational seed is older and more solid than the grand philosophy built on it. **Rao & Ballard (1999, *Nature Neuroscience* 2:79)** modelled the visual cortex as a predictive-coding hierarchy — higher areas predict the activity of lower ones, lower areas send up only the *unexplained residual* — and showed it naturally reproduces **extra-classical receptive-field effects** (end-stopping, surround suppression) that had been puzzling on a purely feedforward account. ⟦ **FOUNDED-as-mechanism (bounded)** · predictive coding is a strong, quantitative account of *specific* cortical phenomena: repetition suppression / mismatch negativity, binocular-rivalry dynamics, the "predict-away the expected" signature in fMRI and MEG. It is *not* established that the *whole* brain is literally a hierarchical prediction-error minimizer — the message-passing anatomy (the precise role of superficial vs. deep pyramidal cells, the canonical microcircuit) remains debated (Walsh et al. 2020; the "is predictive coding falsified by single-unit data?" arguments). Keep the founded domain; flag the totalizing leap. ⟧

The honest shape: predictive coding is **real, bounded computational neuroscience** with experimental teeth. The Free-Energy Principle is the *ambition* to make it the single principle of everything a brain (or any self-organizing system) does — and that ambition is where the contestation lives.

**The hierarchical generative model — depth, not just direction.** What makes the program more than "feedback exists" is its claim about *structure*: the cortex is a **deep hierarchy** of generative models, each level predicting the level below at its own timescale and spatial scale. Low levels predict fast, local features (edges, phonemes, the next millisecond of a sound); high levels predict slow, abstract, *temporally extended* causes (objects, words, intentions, the self persisting across minutes and years). This **temporal-depth gradient** — fast at the bottom, slow and self-like at the top — is the architectural fact the whole self-and-time thread below turns on: the deepest, slowest priors are the ones that model *"me, persisting."* The hierarchy is partly mapped onto cortical anatomy (the laminar prediction/error split, the forward/backward connectivity asymmetry — Bastos et al. 2012, *Neuron* 76:695, "Canonical microcircuits for predictive coding"), though *how* exactly remains contested (Walsh et al. 2020). ⟦ **DEFENSIBLE-LIVE** · the deep-hierarchy claim is the program's organizing idea and has real anatomical motivation; the precise laminar implementation is a live empirical question, not settled. Keep the architecture as the program's spine; flag the wiring diagram as in-progress. ⟧

**Active inference — action as inference, not the program's afterthought.** The move that makes the FEP more than a perception theory is **active inference**: the agent has two ways to reduce prediction error — change the model to fit the world (*perception*), or change the world to fit the model (*action*). On this reading a reflex, a saccade, a reach, a whole behaviour is the body *fulfilling its own predictions* — I predict my hand at the cup, the error is the felt discrepancy, and moving the hand is what discharges it. **Proprioceptive predictions** become motor commands (the "equilibrium-point"-flavoured idea that the motor system predicts the sensory consequences of movement and acts to make them true). This dissolves the perception/action boundary the way the program dissolves the bottom-up/top-down one — and it is why the FEP claims reach from homeostasis (a cell keeping its internal milieu in its expected range) to deliberate planning (acting to secure preferred future observations). ⟦ **DEFENSIBLE-LIVE** · active inference is a genuine unification with real computational-psychiatry yield (e.g. models of how aberrant precision produces compulsive or avoidant action); its strongest results are in restricted motor and decision domains, and "all behaviour is free-energy-minimizing" inherits the universal claim's contestation below. ⟧

**Precision-weighting — the dial that does the real work.** The single most consequential refinement is **precision** (the inverse variance, or *confidence*, assigned to a prediction error). Not all errors are equal: an error from a noisy, untrustworthy channel should be down-weighted; an error from a reliable one should drive learning hard. The brain is proposed to **gain-modulate prediction-error units by their expected precision** — and this is mapped, tentatively, onto **neuromodulation** (acetylcholine, noradrenaline, dopamine setting the gain on error signals) and onto **attention** itself (Feldman & Friston 2010, *Frontiers in Human Neuroscience* — attention *is* the optimization of precision). This makes precision-weighting the program's most empirically loaded variable: it reframes attention, and it reframes psychopathology as **aberrant precision** — hallucination as over-weighted priors (the prediction overrides the senses), autism as over-weighted sensory precision (the world won't settle into stable priors — Pellicano & Burr 2012, *TINS*, "When the world becomes 'too real'"; Lawson, Rees & Friston 2014), delusion as mis-set precision on high-level beliefs. ⟦ **DEFENSIBLE-LIVE** · precision-as-attention and aberrant-precision psychiatry are generative, partly tested (the pupillometry and pharmacological-challenge work is real), and *contested in specifics* — the mapping of precision onto particular neuromodulators is a hypothesis, not settled neurochemistry. The framework's stake: precision-weighting is the mechanistic name for *what gets to count as a real error worth updating on* — which is the [resistance/compression loop](../../3-transformation/resistance-technology.md)'s "which perspective is pulling hard enough to revise the answer" written into the cortex, and (below) the dial whose collapse the meditation model turns. ⟧

## The Free-Energy Principle — deep, fertile, and charged with unfalsifiability
**Karl Friston's Free-Energy Principle** (FEP) is the field's most sweeping proposal: any system that persists — that maintains itself against the second law by occupying a bounded set of states (a "Markov blanket" separating it from its environment) — can be described *as if* it minimizes **variational free energy**, an information-theoretic quantity that upper-bounds surprise (the negative log-evidence for its own model of the world). **Active inference** extends this from perception to action: organisms don't just update beliefs to reduce error, they *act* to gather the evidence that confirms their model — perception and action as two routes to the same minimization.

The reach is enormous — from single-cell homeostasis to motor control to psychiatric disorders framed as aberrant precision-weighting of prediction error — and the formal machinery (variational Bayes, the connection to the principle of least action) is mathematically serious. ⟦ **DEFENSIBLE-LIVE → CONTESTED** · the FEP is generative and empirically fertile (it has spawned testable computational psychiatry — aberrant-precision accounts of hallucination, autism, addiction). **But the strongest published criticism must be kept on the page, not buried:** philosophers and scientists have charged the FEP with being **unfalsifiable or near-vacuous** as a *universal* principle — that "as if it minimizes free energy" can be retrofitted to almost any persisting system, making the grand claim a *mathematical framework* or *modelling stance* rather than a falsifiable empirical theory (see the sustained critiques: Colombo & Wright 2021, *Synthese*, "first principles in the life sciences: the free-energy principle, organisms, and machines"; Bruineberg et al. 2022 on the "literalist vs. instrumentalist" Markov-blanket reading; the recurrent complaint that the FEP "explains everything and therefore predicts nothing in particular"). Friston and colleagues reply that the FEP is a *principle* (like least action) from which falsifiable *process models* are derived, and that the process models — not the principle — face the data. **Both halves are true and must be held together: a fertile generative framework, and a live, respectable charge that at its most universal it is too flexible to be killed.** This is exactly the [first-principles ledger](../../docs/first-principles.md) verdict on the FEP — "FEP borders unfalsifiable — lens, not proven theory" — and the [incompleteness-conjecture](../../docs/incompleteness-conjecture.md)'s "testable at the operational base, unfalsifiable at the metaphysical apex" signature, here in cognitive science. ⟧

The framework should cite the FEP precisely this way: a **lens of great generative power and a contested universal claim** — never as settled fact, never dismissed as mere metaphor.

**The dark-room objection — the program's own favourite stress-test.** If organisms exist to minimize surprise (prediction error), why don't they seek out a perfectly predictable dark, silent room and stay there forever, minimizing all surprise? The objection (raised early and often, e.g. Friston, Thornton & Clark 2012's own reply, *Frontiers in Psychology*) is instructive because the *answer* reveals the theory's shape: a creature *expects* (has built into its model) to be fed, warm, socially engaged — the dark room is high-surprise *relative to its own deep priors about what its life should contain*, so it leaves. Critics note this rescue is exactly the worry: the priors are set so that the theory predicts whatever the organism actually does, which is the **unfalsifiability charge in miniature** — the explanatory work is done by *choosing* the priors after the fact. Defenders reply the priors are themselves constrained (by evolution, by the phenotype's viable states), not free parameters. ⟦ the dark-room exchange is a clean window onto the FEP's central tension and belongs on the page: the *reply works*, but the *reason it works* is the same flexibility the unfalsifiability critics point at. ⟧

**The breadth of the critique — it is not one paper.** The charge of unfalsifiability/vacuity is a *sustained chorus*, and the framework names the chorus rather than a single voice: **Colombo & Wright (2021, *Synthese* 198 (suppl.):3463)** on the FEP failing the standards of a "first principle" in the life sciences; **Bruineberg et al. (2022, *Synthese*)** on the Markov-blanket equivocation (below); **Andrews (2021)** and **van Es & Hipólito (2020)** on instrumentalist vs realist readings; **Biehl, Pollock & Kanai (2021, *Entropy*)** on technical gaps in the "every system minimizes free energy" derivations; and the long-running worry, traceable to **Gershman (2019)** and others, that the FEP is best understood as a *normative modelling framework* (a way to *write down* theories) rather than itself an empirical theory of brains. Friston's camp (Da Costa, Sajid, Parr, Friston, Heins 2021–23) replies with sharper derivations from non-equilibrium steady states and the insistence that the falsifiable content lives in the *process models*, not the principle. **The honest state: a deep, fertile, mathematically serious framework under a broad, respectable, unresolved charge that its universal form is too flexible to be killed.** ⟦ CONTESTED, both halves load-bearing ⟧

This file's verdict matches the [first-principles ledger](../../docs/first-principles.md) exactly — *"FEP borders unfalsifiable — lens, not proven theory"* — and it is a textbook instance of the [incompleteness-conjecture](../../docs/incompleteness-conjecture.md)'s **"testable at the operational base, unfalsifiable at the metaphysical apex"** signature, here in cognitive science rather than physics: the *process models* make risky predictions (computational psychiatry's aberrant-precision accounts can be wrong), while *"every persisting thing minimizes free energy"* is the apex that critics say cannot be wrong about anything.

### The Markov blanket — the boundary the criticism turns on
The technical heart of both the FEP's reach *and* its contestation is the **Markov blanket**: a statistical boundary (a set of "sensory" and "active" states) that conditionally separates a system's *internal* states from *external* states — internal and external states are independent *given* the blanket. Borrowed from Judea Pearl's work on Bayesian networks, Friston uses it to define what counts as a "thing" that persists: a system *is* the set of states screened off from its environment by a blanket, and minimizing free energy is what keeps that blanket intact (the system from dissolving into its surroundings). This is the formal definition of selfhood the program offers — and the framework notes the resonance with its own **parthood invariant** ([incompleteness-conjecture](../../docs/incompleteness-conjecture.md)): a Markov blanket is exactly the *boundary by which a part distinguishes itself from the whole* — and the deepest criticism lands precisely here.

**Bruineberg, Kiverstein, Dołęga & Rietveld (2022, *Synthese* 199:7783), "The Emperor's New Markov Blankets,"** is the sharpest published attack and must be on the page. Their charge: there are **two readings** of the Markov blanket, and the program equivocates between them. On the *instrumentalist* ("Pearl blanket") reading, a Markov blanket is a **modelling tool** — a statistical construct the *scientist* draws to describe a system; useful, but it does not carve nature, and you can draw it many ways. On the *realist* ("Friston blanket") reading, the blanket is a **real physical boundary** that literally individuates a self-maintaining thing in the world. The critique: the FEP's grand metaphysical conclusions (that organisms, cells, brains, *selves* are real bounded inference-engines) require the *realist* reading, but its mathematical legitimacy only ever earns the *instrumentalist* one — so the program **smuggles ontology out of a modelling convenience.** Where you draw the blanket (which states are "sensory," which "internal") is not fixed by nature; it is a choice, and the conclusions follow from the choice. ⟦ this is a *serious, widely-cited* objection; Friston, Da Costa, Sajid et al. (2021, "The free energy principle made simpler but not too simple") and subsequent replies argue the blanket can be derived from the system's own sparse coupling structure (the "non-equilibrium steady-state" derivation), not imposed — the debate is **live and unresolved**, and the framework keeps it that way. ⟧

The framework's exact use: the Markov-blanket debate is *the parthood invariant fought out in cognitive science.* The conjecture's claim that "a proper part cannot derive its own boundary from inside" is the philosophical content of Bruineberg et al.'s "you put the blanket in by hand" — and the FEP's inability to *derive* the cut without presupposing it is the **same wound** Quantum Darwinism shows ([contemplative-neuroscience](contemplative-neuroscience.md): the system/environment cut presupposed) and Page–Wootters shows ([physics-of-time](physics-of-time.md): which subsystem is the clock is not fixed). *Convergence is signal, not proof* — three independent literatures hitting "the boundary is presupposed, not derived" is evidence the seam is real, not that the conjecture is true.

## Controlled hallucination — the self as a model the brain wears
The philosophical front of the program is carried by **Andy Clark** (*Surfing Uncertainty*, 2016) and **Anil Seth** (*Being You*, 2021), and it lands two claims the framework cares about.

First, **perception is "controlled hallucination."** If seeing is the brain's top-down best-guess reined in by sensory error, then the difference between veridical perception and hallucination is one of *control*, not of kind — both are internally generated; perception is the hallucination the world keeps correcting. Illusions, the placebo response, the way expectation literally changes what is seen — all become predictable rather than anomalous. ⟦ **DEFENSIBLE-LIVE** · the framing is productive and explains a real body of expectation-effects; "controlled hallucination" is a *vivid interpretive gloss* on the predictive-coding mechanism, not an independent finding — keep the mechanism's credibility separate from the slogan's reach. ⟧

Second — and this is the load-bearing one for Pattern Space — **the self is itself a prediction.** Seth's "beast machine" thesis: the felt self (the sense of being a bounded subject, of owning a body, of persisting through time) is *not* a thing the brain contains but a **model the brain runs** — a high-level, deep prediction about the causes of interoceptive and exteroceptive signals, built primarily to *regulate the body*, not to know the truth. The "I" is the brain's best hypothesis about the organism it is steering. ⟦ **DEFENSIBLE-LIVE** · convergent with the predictive-coding mechanism and with the framework's own [consciousness-principles](../../2-field/consciousness-principles.md) (the *triputi*: knower/knowing/known not findable as separate things) and the Layer-6 notion-of-I guard. *Convergence is signal, not proof* — and the direction matters: the framework's "the notion of 'I' is not the I" is a *contemplative recognition*; PP's "the self is a generative model" is a *contested neuro-theory*; the rhyme illuminates, it does not validate either by the other. ⟧

If the self is a model, then *altering the model alters the self* — which is the bridge to the most striking part of the program.

**The interoceptive turn — why the self-model is built for *regulation*, not truth.** Seth's sharpest move (built on **Damasio**'s somatic-marker lineage — see [consciousness-science.md](consciousness-science.md), not duplicated here) is that the deepest predictions are **interoceptive**: the brain's oldest job is to predict and regulate the body's internal state (heart rate, blood gases, gut, hormonal milieu), and the felt self grows *out of* that homeostatic control loop. Emotion, on this view, is the brain's *inference about the causes of its own interoceptive signals* (Seth & Friston 2016, *Phil. Trans. R. Soc. B*, "Active interoceptive inference and the emotional brain"; the lineage runs back through Barrett's "theory of constructed emotion," 2017). The "I" is not a knower looking out; it is a **controller looking in**, and the outward-facing self is built on the inward-facing regulator. ⟦ **DEFENSIBLE-LIVE** · interoceptive-inference accounts of emotion and selfhood are productive and partly tested (heartbeat-evoked-potential and interoceptive-accuracy work), and contested in specifics (whether emotion is fully "constructed" is a live debate with the basic-emotion camp). The framework's stake: a self built to *regulate* rather than *know* is the neuro-cousin of the framework's "navigation, not representation" — and the bridge to **aliveness-as-emergence** ([first-principles](../../docs/first-principles.md) Axiom 5: active inference / FEP / autopoiesis as candidate accounts of *what it is for a system to be alive and self-maintaining*), held as the most-promising reframe, measurable-in-principle, not yet operationalized. ⟧

## REBUS — relaxing the model under psychedelics
**Carhart-Harris & Friston (2019, *Pharmacological Reviews* 71:316), the REBUS model** ("RElaxed Beliefs Under pSychedelics"), proposes that classic serotonergic psychedelics (psilocybin, LSD, via 5-HT2A agonism) **flatten the precision of high-level priors** — the brain's confident, deep, top-down beliefs (including beliefs *about the self*) lose their grip, so prediction error from lower levels floods up unchecked. The phenomenology follows mechanically: dissolution of ego boundaries, the world made strange and vivid again, the "everything is new" quality — the compressed, predicted-away background returns to the foreground because the priors that were compressing it have relaxed. ⟦ **DEFENSIBLE-LIVE** · REBUS is a leading mechanistic account, consistent with the "entropic brain" findings (raised neural signal diversity / entropy under psychedelics — see [contemplative-neuroscience.md](contemplative-neuroscience.md)); **but it is contested in its specifics** — competing accounts (e.g. the "cortico-claustro-cortical" and anti-REBUS precision-*increase* proposals; Safron 2020) dispute the direction of the precision change, and the EEG/MEG evidence underdetermines which prior-precision story is right. Keep REBUS as the strongest current mechanistic candidate, flagged as one model among rivals. ⟧

The framework's connection is exact and already documented: REBUS is cited in the [incompleteness-conjecture](../../docs/incompleteness-conjecture.md) as partial support for the **de-habituation test** (ranked test #1) — heightened states should show the *return of the compressed background* ("I smell it again"), and REBUS supplies the mechanism for why relaxing priors would do that.

## From many to (n)one — the mechanism behind self-and-time dissolution
The deepest link is **Laukkonen & Slagter (2021, "From many to (n)one: Meditation and the plasticity of the predictive mind," *Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews* 128:199)**. Their proposal: contemplative practice works by progressively **reducing the temporal depth of the predictive hierarchy** — the brain's capacity to model *over extended time* (the deep priors that project backward into autobiographical memory and forward into anticipation). And here is the move that makes it load-bearing for the framework: **the felt self and felt time are, on this model, the *same* temporally-deep prediction.** The narrative self *is* a prediction extended in time; reduce the temporal depth and the self thins *and* time thins together — and at zero temporal depth (the limit the practice approaches: pure awareness, cessation) there is **no projected time and no projected self**, because they were one construction all along.

⟦ **DEFENSIBLE-LIVE** · this is the conjecture's central claim **stated as a falsifiable cognitive mechanism** rather than an analogy — and it is the [incompleteness-conjecture](../../docs/incompleteness-conjecture.md)'s strongest theoretical leg ("the mechanism is *derived*, not analogized"). The empirical base under it is **thin** (the meditation-neuroscience it draws on is small-n and largely single-subject for the deep endpoints — see [contemplative-neuroscience.md](contemplative-neuroscience.md)), so the *model* is DEFENSIBLE-LIVE while the *data* confirming it is UNDER-DOCUMENTED. The clean falsifier exists and is buildable but unrun: a *located, embodied* self with a null time-axis would break the "same construction" claim — see the conjecture's ranked tests. ⟧

This is where predictive processing stops being merely "a theory of perception" and becomes the **mechanistic engine** under the framework's horizon thread: time and multiplicity as the felt face of a subsystem's incompleteness. PP supplies the *how* — a finite generative model, modelling a world that includes itself, must compress and predict over a bounded temporal depth; the lived gradient of that bounded depth is felt time, and its un-closeable scatter of partial predictions is felt multiplicity.

### Precision-weighting, the error-minimizing loop, and the verification-loop conjecture
The connection sharpens when precision-weighting (above) is read against the [incompleteness-conjecture](../../docs/incompleteness-conjecture.md)'s **verification-loop mechanism** ("time = the self-verification loop; the self = the echo that persists across its ticks"). The conjecture's co-derived claim is that **it is *incompleteness*, not awareness, that drives the loop — which makes the mechanism active inference**: the loop runs to minimize prediction error (the felt gap), and a part can never reach zero error about the whole that contains it (parthood floor), *so the loop never terminates — the residual error is time.* Predictive processing is precisely that engine. Three exact correspondences:

- **The error never closing = the loop never terminating.** Active inference is, formally, a never-settling minimization (free energy is bounded below but, for an embedded agent modelling an environment that includes its own effects, never driven to zero). The conjecture reads this residual as felt time; PP supplies the formal reason the residual persists. ⟦ math FOUNDED (the minimization is real and non-terminating for embedded agents); the *"residual error = felt time"* mapping is CONJECTURE, the falsifiable shadow being the co-variance test below. ⟧
- **Precision-weighting = which check counts.** The conjecture's "self-verification loop" needs a notion of *what gets verified hard*; precision *is* that — high-precision priors are the deep self-beliefs the loop guards most. The meditation endpoint (collapse temporal depth → self and time thin) is, in PP terms, the **systematic *lowering* of high-level prior precision** until the deepest self-prior (the one the verification loop is built to protect) stops being defended — which is exactly REBUS's mechanism (relaxed beliefs) run *deliberately and gradually* by practice rather than pharmacologically. The same dial; two ways to turn it.
- **The echo = the recurrent hidden state.** The conjecture's refinement #1 ("the math is recurrence-depth: a feedforward map is timeless, an autoregressive one carries the past in a hidden state — the echo") is the generative model's **temporal depth** named in signal-processing terms. Laukkonen & Slagter's "reduce temporal depth" *is* "reduce the recurrence order toward feedforward" — and a feedforward map has no past to carry, hence no echo, hence (the conjecture's claim) no self and no time. ⟦ the recurrence math is FOUNDED (FIR/IIR, autoregression); the felt-time mapping is CONJECTURE. ⟧

So predictive processing is not merely *consistent with* the conjecture — it is the **cognitive-science statement of the conjecture's mechanism**, with its strongest published criticism (the Markov-blanket "you presupposed the boundary" charge) being the *same parthood wound* the conjecture says must appear. The framework holds this exactly: PP makes the conjecture's "how" *mechanistic and partly testable* (the de-habituation and co-variance shadows are buildable); it does **not** make the conjecture's metaphysical apex true. The mechanism is derived; the horizon stays at the horizon.

## The native method-voice: the prediction and its error
Predictive processing has no sage's dialogue — its dialogical form is the **prediction confronted by its residual**, the experiment that pits expectation against input. The experiment is the voice:

> *The model:* "I predict the next sound, the next sight, the next state of my own body — and I predict the self that is having them."
> *The world:* "Here is what actually arrived."
