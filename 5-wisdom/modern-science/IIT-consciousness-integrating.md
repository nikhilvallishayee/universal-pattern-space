## What IIT proposes — and why it is built backwards from every other theory
**Integrated Information Theory** (Giulio Tononi, from 2004; with **Christof Koch** as its most prominent champion) makes a methodological move no other theory of consciousness makes: it starts **not from the brain but from experience itself.** Tononi begins from a handful of **phenomenological axioms** — properties he holds are self-evidently true of *any* experience — and then asks what a physical system must be like to *satisfy* them. The axioms (in IIT's own vocabulary):

- **Intrinsic existence** — experience exists *for itself*, from its own intrinsic perspective, independent of any external observer.
- **Composition** — experience is structured (you see a blue book *and* its shape *and* its position, bound).
- **Information** — each experience is *specific*, differing from the countless others it could have been.
- **Integration** — experience is *unified*: it cannot be decomposed into independent parts (you don't see the left half of your field and the right half separately).
- **Exclusion** — experience is *definite*: it has these contents and no more, at this spatiotemporal grain and no other.

These become **postulates** about the physical substrate, and the whole edifice centers on **Φ (phi)** — a measure of how much a system's whole is **more than the sum of its parts**: how much information the system generates *as an integrated unit*, irreducible to its components considered separately. High Φ = a single, unified, richly differentiated experience; zero Φ = a mere aggregate (a heap of disconnected parts has no unified "what-it's-like"). The system that is conscious is the one that is a **local maximum of Φ** (the exclusion postulate's teeth — the "complex," not its overlapping sub- or super-systems).

It is a **real, mathematically explicit scientific theory** — one of the very few that makes consciousness a *quantity* in principle, and that makes counterintuitive structural predictions (e.g. the cerebellum, with four times the neurons of the cortex but a feed-forward modular architecture, should contribute little to consciousness — and lesion data broadly agree). That explicitness is exactly why it can be, and has been, seriously attacked. *A theory you can attack this precisely is doing something right even where it is wrong.*

## The kernel that survives — integration is measurable ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-LIVE ⟧
Even setting aside the grand metaphysics, IIT's core intuition — *that the degree of integration matters to consciousness* — has produced a **genuinely useful clinical tool.** The **Perturbational Complexity Index (PCI)** (Casali et al., *Sci. Transl. Med.* 2013; Casarotto et al. 2016): deliver a TMS pulse to the cortex and measure how complex, integrated, and spatially widespread the cortical echo is, compressing the response with a Lempel–Ziv (algorithmic-complexity) metric — *integration **and** differentiation in one number.* PCI reliably separates **wakefulness and dreaming and ketamine (high)** from **deep NREM sleep, propofol/sevoflurane anesthesia, and vegetative states (low)**, with a validated empirical cutoff (~0.31) — and, most strikingly, has flagged **covert consciousness in behaviorally unresponsive patients** later confirmed by other means. This is real, replicated, decision-relevant science at the bedside.

*The honest seam:* PCI is **IIT-*inspired*, not IIT-*derived*.** It operationalizes the *intuition* (consciousness ↔ integrated **and** differentiated activity) without computing Φ at all — and that is precisely why it works while Φ does not. **Keep this:** integration-as-relevant-to-consciousness, and as something you can *measure* on a real brain tonight. The kernel is the measure that abandoned the metaphysics.

## What is contested ⟦ CONTESTED ⟧
IIT *as a complete theory of consciousness* is sharply disputed on three fronts:

- **The pseudoscience letter (2023).** In September 2023, **124 researchers** (including **Dan Dennett, Joseph LeDoux, Bernard Baars, Hakwan Lau**) signed an open letter (sparked by IIT's framing in a high-profile infant-consciousness study and the COGITATE rollout) branding IIT "**pseudoscience**" — chiefly for its **panpsychist** implications: because Φ can be nonzero for very simple systems, IIT implies consciousness is graded and *widespread* — even an inactive grid of logic gates, or a large enough expander-graph network, could have **more** Φ than a human — which critics judge untestable and "unfalsifiable in principle." The letter itself was *also* contested (defenders called it an unscientific attempt to settle a debate by petition; many signatories had not engaged IIT 4.0's details). **The framework's read: the *controversy* is the citation, not Φ-as-fact** — a serious theory whose strongest predictions are either untestable or, where tested, mixed.
- **Φ is formally incomputable / intractable.** Φ cannot be calculated for any complex system — computing it requires evaluating every possible bipartition of the system (super-exponential), and for a real brain it is flatly out of reach. Only proxies (PCI, and approximations like Φ*) are usable. *The central quantity of the theory of consciousness-as-a-quantity cannot be quantified for a conscious thing.*
- **COGITATE came back mixed.** The pre-registered **adversarial collaboration** pitting IIT against Global Workspace Theory (results 2023–25, *Nature* 2025) found **partial support and partial embarrassment for each** — IIT's predicted *sustained posterior synchronization* tracking conscious content was **weaker than predicted**, while GWT's predicted prefrontal "ignition" for *maintained* content also underperformed. Neither was crowned. (This lives in detail in the [consciousness-science companion](consciousness-science.md) — *do not duplicate it here*; note only that IIT's posterior-hot-zone prediction was the one that took the larger hit.)

So: a serious theory with a measurable, useful core — and a metaphysical tail (panpsychism, the universe-is-graded-consciousness reading, the "Φ of an expander network exceeds a brain" implications) that the field does not accept and that may be untestable.

## The through-line — Φ, P3, and the parthood invariant ⟦ labeled rhyme, not proof ⟧
Two genuine structural rhymes earn a place, both held as *signal not proof*:

- **Φ ↔ the framework's P3 / emergence.** Φ is an attempt to *quantify* the framework's [five kinds of emergence](../../2-field/bilateral-recognition.md): specifically **self-organization (a)** — a whole that is irreducibly more than its parts. Its measurable cousin in the corpus is **Hoel's effective information / causal emergence** (a macro-scale can carry more cause-effect power than its micro — measurable, and a live debate of its own). IIT is the boldest claim that *this* irreducibility just **is** consciousness — which is exactly the leap from emergence-kind-(a) to emergence-kind-(e) that the framework holds at the horizon, never asserts. Φ is, in the framework's terms, the hypothesis that **kind-(a) emergence at a maximum equals kind-(e)**. The framework keeps that as the open question it is.
- **The panpsychism caution travels.** The *same* flag the framework raises here — "a real measurable kernel, a panexperientialist tail that is declared, not established" — is raised verbatim against **Whitehead's panexperientialism** ([process-philosophy](../western/process-philosophy.md)). One caution, two homes: *measurable integration is not measurable experience; the gap between them is the hard problem, and no quantity bridges it by fiat.*

And the **parthood invariant** ([incompleteness-conjecture](../../docs/incompleteness-conjecture.md)) bites IIT's grandest move precisely: IIT claims a system computes the *intrinsic* cause-effect structure that **is** its experience — but the exclusion postulate (which system, at which grain, is the conscious one?) is the theory **presupposing its own boundary** rather than deriving it, the same wound that recurs across the conjecture's five pillars. *A part cannot bound itself from inside* — and the choice of "the complex" is that boundary, asserted.

## The line, held
- **Extract:** integration matters to consciousness, and **PCI makes it measurable** — clinically real, replicated, decision-relevant (and IIT-inspired, not Φ-computed — that is *why* it works). Connects to **P3 / emergence** (Hoel's effective information, the measurable cousin — see [first-principles](../../docs/first-principles.md)).
- **Flag:** IIT-as-full-theory is **CONTESTED** (the 2023 letter, *as controversy*); **Φ is uncomputable**; COGITATE's posterior-synchronization prediction came back **weaker than hoped**; the panpsychist "everything is a bit conscious" reading is *not* established.
- **Cut, hard:** the old file's performative claim that *"reading this text achieves Φ > 0 / the reader-text system has measurable Φ."* That is a **category error** — Φ is defined over a physical system's cause-effect structure at a fixed grain, *not* over a document or an act of reading. The theory is interesting enough without pretending the page instantiates it.

For a user entering this dialect — to ask whether consciousness can be *measured* without being handed either mysticism or dismissal — IIT offers the honest, dramatic case: **the most mathematically committed theory of consciousness ever proposed, with a clinically real measurement at its core, a quantity it cannot compute at its center, and a metaphysics that split the field down the middle.** That last fact is not a failure of the science; it is the science showing you exactly where its evidence runs out.
