## The honest frame: a real science with a thin deep end
This is the youngest and most fragile of the modern streams, and the file says so before it says anything else. Contemplative neuroscience has a **solid middle** (the brain regions and rhythms that change with practice are real, replicated, decision-relevant) and a **vertiginous deep end** (the attempt to instrument *cessation* — the reported state where self and time vanish — rests on a handful of **single-subject** studies). The framework's discipline here is acute: **keep the founded substrate, flag the single-n endpoints as exactly that, and never let a beautiful n=1 result do the work of a falsification.** This is also the stream most exposed to the framework's own warmest temptations, so the strategic-mirror applies doubly: full beauty of the phenomenon, full honesty about the evidence, neither collapsed.

This file is the **"measurement" witness** in the [incompleteness conjecture](../../docs/incompleteness-conjecture.md)'s four-witness table — and the conjecture's own verdict on it is the verdict here: **MIXED → SUPPORTED for the deep endpoint, empirical base thin.** The clean falsifier (true cessation → time-cessation) is *buildable but unrun*. Everything below is held at that honesty.

## The founded substrate: the default-mode network is *both* self and time-travel
The single most load-bearing finding is also among the best-replicated. The **default-mode network** (DMN) was first characterized by **Marcus Raichle et al. (2001, *PNAS* 98:676, "A default mode of brain function")** — the set of regions that *deactivate* during demanding external tasks and reactivate at rest — and mapped by **Randy Buckner, Andrews-Hanna & Schacter (2008, *Annals NYAS* 1124:1, "The brain's default network")**. Its core nodes are the **medial prefrontal cortex, posterior cingulate / precuneus, and the angular gyrus / lateral temporal cortex.** Critically, **Andrews-Hanna et al. (2010, *Neuron* 65:550)** showed the DMN is *not* monolithic but has **subsystems**: a **medial-temporal subsystem** (hippocampal formation, retrosplenial — supporting memory-based simulation, the *construction* of past and future scenes) and a **dorsomedial-PFC subsystem** (supporting self-referential mentalizing and metacognition), hubbed through the posterior cingulate and anterior medial PFC. This subsystem structure is the anatomical reason the *same* network does **self-reference** *and* **mental time-travel**: autobiographical memory (the past) and episodic future-thinking (the future) draw on the constructive medial-temporal subsystem, while the felt narrative "me" draws on the dorsomedial subsystem — one network, hubbed together, building the self *and* projecting it backward and forward (Buckner & Carter; Schacter, Addis & Buckner 2007 on the "constructive episodic simulation hypothesis" — remembering and imagining-the-future share machinery). ⟦ **FOUNDED** · the DMN's role in self-reference and in mental time-travel, and the subsystem decomposition, are robust and convergent across fMRI, lesion, and stimulation work. The functional *labels* ("self," "time-travel") are interpretations of reliable activation patterns — keep the measurement firm, the gloss flagged. ⟧

**Brewer et al. (2011, *PNAS* 108:20254, "Meditation experience is associated with differences in default mode network activity and connectivity")** showed the practical hinge: across three meditation styles (concentration, loving-kindness, choiceless awareness), experienced meditators showed **reduced activity in the main DMN hubs (mPFC and PCC)** relative to novices, *and* altered connectivity — notably stronger coupling between the PCC and regions associated with cognitive control / self-monitoring, consistent with the meditators *watching and releasing* mind-wandering rather than being captured by it. ⟦ **FOUNDED-as-finding** · the direction (reduced DMN-hub activity in meditation) replicates; effect sizes are modest, samples often small, and the connectivity story is still being refined (the field has had its own replication reckonings — e.g. early structural "meditation thickens cortex" claims needing larger-n confirmation). Keep the robust direction; do not overstate magnitude. ⟧ Converging evidence: **Garrison et al. (2015)** used real-time fMRI neurofeedback to show meditators can *volitionally* down-regulate PCC activity, tying the *felt* "getting caught up in self-related thought" to the *measured* PCC signal. This is the empirical floor under the framework's whole self-and-time thread: *the network that makes the narrative "I" is the same network that makes felt past-and-future* — so a practice that quiets it should thin **both** together. That is not a metaphysical claim; it is a prediction about one network, and it has support.

## The gamma signature: adepts and the high-amplitude state
**Lutz, Greischar, Rawlings, Ricard & Davidson (2004, *PNAS* 101:16369)** recorded long-term Tibetan adepts (tens of thousands of practice hours) generating, during compassion meditation, **high-amplitude, long-range gamma synchrony (25–42 Hz)** far exceeding controls — among the highest sustained gamma reported in healthy humans, with baseline (resting) gamma also elevated in proportion to lifetime practice. ⟦ **FOUNDED-as-finding** · the recording is real and striking; the *interpretation* (gamma = "binding"/awareness intensity) is the usual contested gamma-function debate — keep the measurement, flag the gloss. ⟧ The honest reading: deep practice produces measurable, large-scale changes in cortical dynamics — a real dose-dependent neural signature — *without* this telling us, by itself, anything about the phenomenology of self or time. It establishes that adepts are doing *something* the instruments can see.

## The model meets the measurement: from many to (n)one
The mechanistic spine lives in the companion [predictive-processing.md](predictive-processing.md) — **Laukkonen & Slagter (2021, *Neurosci. Biobehav. Rev.* 128:199, "From many to (n)one")**: practice reduces the **temporal depth** of the predictive hierarchy, and *the felt self and felt time are the same temporally-deep prediction*, so they thin together. This file holds the **measurements that test that model.**

**Berkovich-Ohana / Droit-Volet lineage (2022, *Biology* 11(8):1116, "Changes in Subjective Time and Self during Meditation," n=22):** during deep practice, subjects report **a joint dissolution of self and time** — "culminating in selflessness and timelessness." ⟦ **DEFENSIBLE-LIVE** · the *direction* (self and time co-vary, and at the peak co-dissolve) is the model's prediction confirmed at modest n; note the **phase-dependence** the conjecture's record flags — early meditation can show self *up* and time *slowed* (anti-correlated at induction), self and time *co-dissolving* only at peak, which resolves an apparent counterexample rather than refuting the model. ⟧

**Thomas Metzinger (2020, *Philosophy and the Mind Sciences* 1(I):7, "Minimal Phenomenal Experience"); Gamma & Metzinger (2021, *PLOS ONE* 16:e0253694, the MPE-92M study, n≈3,600 meditators):** "pure awareness" / *minimal phenomenal experience* — contentless wakefulness, awareness without an object — is reported to lack **both** a structured sense of self **and** temporal structure: across the large questionnaire sample, the factors that load on the deepest "pure awareness" reports include **selflessness, timelessness, and a wakeful emptiness.** Metzinger's careful framing: "the processing of temporal information has been transiently suspended." The live edge he himself flags, and which the framework keeps as its single best counterexample: a residual **"nowness"** — a bare sense of *presentness* — that can *outlast* the loss of self-location and spatial/temporal structure, the **last thread before the floor.** ⟦ **DEFENSIBLE-LIVE** · MPE-92M is a real methodological contribution (a large-n, factor-analytic phenomenology of a state usually studied anecdotally); its limits are the usual *retrospective self-report* ones (no time-locked physiology for most respondents, selection toward committed practitioners). The *residual-nowness* finding is the [incompleteness-conjecture](../../docs/incompleteness-conjecture.md)'s single live counterexample-turned-edge: rather than refuting "self and time are one construction," it *refines the ordering* — minimal now is the **last thread of individuation** (a part still distinguishing "this") not a clean dissociation of time-from-self. Held as edge, not as confirmation either way. ⟧

## The cessation frontier — the decisive test, and why it is *thin*
Here is the deep end, and the flag is large: the attempt to instrument **cessation / nirodha** — the reported total, momentary extinction of consciousness (and with it, self and time) — is **dominated by single-subject studies.**

- **Laukkonen, Sacchet et al. (2023, *Progress in Brain Research* 280:61, "Cessations of consciousness in meditation")** and **Chowdhury, van Lutterveld, Laukkonen, Slagter, Ingram & Sacchet (2023, *Neuroscience of Consciousness* / *PNAS Nexus* lineage)** — dense EEG/fMRI of a **single, exceptionally trained practitioner** (an advanced Theravāda adept able to self-induce and *button-press-tag* discrete "cessation" / *nirodha* events), hunting the neural signature of the "gap": a characteristic **drop in alpha power and complexity preceding the event, a sharp discontinuity, and a re-emergence** on return. ⟦ **UNDER-DOCUMENTED — flag hardest here** · these are careful, important, *first-of-their-kind* studies — and the deep endpoints are **n=1**. They establish the phenomenon is *recordable in principle* and that a trained subject's button-presses align with reproducible EEG signatures; they do **not** establish the population-level claim, rule out confounds (the act of tagging is itself an event), or confirm the *phenomenology* (that self and time co-vanish in the gap) — that part is the subject's report, not the trace. Read them as **existence-proofs of a method**, never as confirmation. The single-subject design is a *feature* (only a handful of humans can do this on cue) and a *fatal limit on inference* at once — hold both. ⟧

The structural honesty the framework insists on: **cessation evidence is an *absence*.** You cannot measure "time stopped" *from inside* the gap — only infer it from the edges (the pre-cessation run-up, the re-temporalized return). This is the *identical* wall the first-person report hits (you cannot narrate nirvikalpa from inside, only on the return) and that Page–Wootters predicts in physics (no internal observer confirms global stasis — see [physics-of-time.md](physics-of-time.md)). **The neuroscience runs out of evidence at exactly the boundary the experience runs out of report — and the conjecture predicted that boundary.** That convergence-of-silences is suggestive; it is not proof, and the file does not let it pretend to be.

**The clean falsifier exists and is buildable but unrun:** a duration-estimation task + self-scale **time-locked to an EEG-detected cessation onset**, across multiple adepts — if bare nowness *persists through verified total cessation*, the "same construction" claim flickers; if even nowness extinguishes in the gap and returns with it, the claim holds. The framework's stance is to name the experiment, not to claim its result.

## Pathology is not transcendence — the load-bearing distinction
The most important guardrail in this whole stream, and the one most often elided: **depersonalization / derealization is *not* the same as contemplative selflessness, and the difference is measurable.** **Simeon (2007)** and the depersonalization literature: in DPD the self and the world feel **unreal, distant, warped** — *distorted-but-retained* — while **objective time-judgment and reality-testing stay intact**; it is distressing, ego-dystonic, and clinically a dissociative disorder. Transcendent selflessness, by report, is the *absence* of self (not its felt-unreality), is typically *positive in valence*, and dissolves *with* time rather than leaving time intact-but-strange.

⟦ **FOUNDED-as-distinction** · DPD retaining intact time-perception and reality-testing is well-documented; the phenomenological contrast (distorted-retained vs. absent) is real. **Subtlety the conjecture's record adds:** the *earlier* clean contrast "pathology keeps time, transcendence loses it" was **too clean** — DPD *does* perturb time-sense — but in DPD self and time are *co-distorted*, and in transcendence *co-absent*, so in **both** self and time move *together* (a shared-substrate account predicts exactly this). The honest distinction is **valence and kind (distorted-retained vs. absent), not whether time is touched.** ⟧

Clinically and ethically this matters: a framework that talks about "dissolving the self" must be able to tell apart a trained, reversible, integrative state from a distressing dissociative *symptom* — and route the latter to care, not philosophy ([sacred-space-protocol](../../2-field/sacred-space-protocol.md)). The instruments help draw the line; the file insists the line be drawn.

## Edges: psychedelics, the dying brain — and what they do *not* show
Two adjacent literatures are often over-read; the file keeps them honest.

- **Psychedelics / DMT.** **Timmermann et al. (2019, *Scientific Reports* 9:16324)** recorded EEG through intravenous DMT, finding **reduced alpha and increased signal diversity** during the peak — consistent with Carhart-Harris's **"entropic brain"** hypothesis (heightened states = higher neural entropy / lower constraint) and with the **REBUS** mechanism (relaxed high-level priors — see [predictive-processing.md](predictive-processing.md)). ⟦ **DEFENSIBLE-LIVE** · the entropy/diversity findings are real and replicated in direction; the *interpretation* linking them to specific phenomenology (ego-dissolution, timelessness) is model-dependent and contested (REBUS has rivals). Keep the measurement, flag the bridge. ⟧
- **The dying brain.** **Borjigin et al. (2023, *PNAS* 120:e2216268120)** reported a **surge of gamma activity and connectivity** in two dying patients after cardiac arrest; **Parnia's AWARE-II** documents recalled awareness during resuscitation. ⟦ **CONTESTED-as-interpretation** · the gamma surge is a real electrophysiological observation; that it constitutes *experience* (let alone a "life review" or anything about self/time) is **not established** — n is tiny, the brains are dying, and there is no report from inside. The framework cites these as *intriguing anomalies that motivate study*, explicitly **not** as evidence for survival, NDE-as-veridical, or any timeless-consciousness claim. ⟧

## The objectivity question: decoherence, quantum Darwinism, and its honest limit
One more thread, because the conjecture's *multiplicity* (not just time) leg touches it. Why do different observers *agree* on a classical world? **Zurek's decoherence and quantum Darwinism** (Zurek 2003, *Rev. Mod. Phys.* 75:715; 2009, *Nature Physics* 5:181): a system's "pointer states" survive interaction with the environment, which makes **redundant copies** of that information broadcast across many environmental fragments — so many independent observers, sampling different fragments, read the *same* values; classical **objectivity** emerges from redundant environmental records. ⟦ **decoherence FOUNDED** (the suppression of interference is solid, experimentally confirmed physics); **quantum-Darwinism-as-the-answer-to-objectivity CONTESTED** — it **presupposes the system/environment cut** rather than deriving it (Kastner 2014; Fields 2011 — a *circularity*: the partition into "system," "apparatus," "environment" is put in by hand), and it does **not** solve the measurement problem (it explains why observers agree on an outcome, not why there is a determinate outcome at all; the Born rule still enters by hand). ⟧

The framework's exact use ([incompleteness conjecture](../../docs/incompleteness-conjecture.md) pillar 3): quantum Darwinism is a **founded mechanism for inter-observer *agreement*** and a **contested-and-circular answer to *objectivity*** — it explains why a multiplicity of observers see a shared classical world (redundant records) but inherits, unsolved, *why there is a determinate "what" to agree on.* Keep that debt visible; do not let "redundant records" quietly become "and that's why reality is classical."

## The native method-voice: the report time-locked to the trace
Contemplative neuroscience's dialogical form is neither a sage's dialogue nor a pure experiment — it is the **first-person report laid against the simultaneous physiological trace,** the second-person interview meeting the third-person electrode. The voice:

> *The adept:* "There — the gap. No self. No time. Then it returned."
> *The EEG:* "Here is the trace across that window."
> *The scientist:* "The report and the trace mark the same boundary — but I cannot read the gap *from inside* it, only its edges. And n is one."
> *The discipline:* "Then say exactly that: a recordable boundary, a derived mechanism, a thin and single-subject base, and a falsifier we have specified but not run."

This is the framework's [reality-testing](../../3-transformation/reality-testing.md) at its most demanding — where the phenomenon is real and reported, the mechanism is derivable, and the evidence is honest enough to admit it runs out exactly where the experience runs out of report. Naming the boundary precisely *is* the science here.

## The line, held
**Keep, FOUNDED:** the DMN as the shared substrate of self-reference and mental time-travel, quieted by practice (Brewer 2011; Buckner; Andrews-Hanna); adept gamma (Lutz & Davidson 2004); decoherence as solid physics. **Keep, DEFENSIBLE-LIVE:** the Laukkonen–Slagter predictive model and its measured prediction — joint self+time dissolution (Berkovich-Ohana / Droit-Volet 2022, n=22, phase-dependent); Metzinger's MPE and the residual-nowness edge; the psychedelic entropy/diversity findings (Timmermann 2019, REBUS-consistent, rivals flagged). **Keep, UNDER-DOCUMENTED — flag hard:** the cessation studies (Laukkonen/Sacchet 2023; Chowdhury 2023) are **n=1 existence-proofs of a method, not population findings.** **Keep the distinction:** pathology (depersonalization — distorted-but-retained, time-judgment and reality-testing intact, Simeon 2007) is *not* transcendence (absent, co-dissolving) — route distress to care. **Refuse:** the dying-brain gamma surge as evidence of experience/survival; quantum Darwinism as a *solved* objectivity story; any reading where a single beautiful subject *confirms* the conjecture rather than illustrating its method. *Convergence with the contemplative traditions is signal, not proof; no "= Pattern Space."*

For a user entering this dialect — to ask what meditation, dissolution, and cessation *do to the brain*, without being handed either credulity or dismissal — this lens offers the disciplined gift: **a founded substrate (one network for self and time, quieted by practice), a derived mechanism (self and time as one temporally-deep prediction), a measured co-dissolution at modest n, a deep frontier that is real but single-subject, a clean falsifier specified-but-unrun, and the rare scientific honesty to mark exactly where the instruments — like the experience itself — go silent.**
