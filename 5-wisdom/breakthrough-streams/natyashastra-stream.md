## The text and its claim
The **Nāṭyaśāstra** (attributed to **Bharata Muni**, c. 200 BCE–200 CE; ~6000 verses across 36 chapters) is a vast treatise on theatre, dance, and music — opening with a **myth of origin** that is itself the thesis: the gods ask Brahmā for a fifth Veda, one open to all castes, and he distills it from the four — *recitation* from the Ṛg, *song* from the Sāma, *gesture* (abhinaya) from the Yajur, and **rasa** from the Atharva. Drama is framed from its first breath as a *synthesis*, a knowing made for everyone. At its heart is a genuine **theory of aesthetic experience**: the doctrine of **rasa** ("juice, savor, essence"). Its core insight is precise and, in its domain, *founded*: an emotion *depicted* in art is not the raw emotion but a **savored, contemplated** version of it — the performer evokes a transient feeling (**bhāva**) in conditions that let the audience *taste* it (**rasa**) at one remove, with relish rather than rawness. Grief in life wounds; grief in a play is *karuṇa rasa*, savored. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE · a real phenomenology of aesthetic distance, centuries before Western aesthetics named it (cf. Kant's *disinterested* pleasure, Bullough's "psychical distance" — independent arrivals at the same structure) ⟧

The **rasa-sūtra** itself (NŚ 6) is famously terse — *"vibhāvānubhāva-vyabhicāri-saṃyogād rasa-niṣpattiḥ"*, "rasa arises from the conjunction of the determinants, the consequents, and the transient states" — and the millennium of commentary fighting over that one line *is* the tradition's philosophical depth. The components: **vibhāva** (the cause/situation that grounds an emotion — the lover, the spring garden), **anubhāva** (its outward expression — the glance, the trembling), and **vyabhicāri-bhāvas** (the thirty-three transient flickers — anxiety, jealousy, fatigue) playing over the one durable **sthāyī-bhāva**. Bharata's analogy is culinary, not accidental: as a connoisseur tastes a *flavor* compounded from many ingredients and spices, *rasa* is the savor compounded when these elements meet in a prepared mind.

## The taxonomy ⟦ the kernel — a structured first-person map ⟧
Bharata enumerates the **rasas** (classically eight, later nine with *śānta*), each paired with a **sthāyī-bhāva** (durable underlying emotion):

| Rasa | Savor | Underlying *bhāva* |
|---|---|---|
| Śṛṅgāra | love / the erotic | *rati* (desire) |
| Hāsya | the comic | *hāsa* (mirth) |
| Karuṇa | the compassionate / tragic | *śoka* (grief) |
| Raudra | the furious | *krodha* (anger) |
| Vīra | the heroic | *utsāha* (energy) |
| Bhayānaka | the fearful | *bhaya* (fear) |
| Bībhatsa | the disgusting | *jugupsā* (aversion) |
| Adbhuta | the wondrous | *vismaya* (astonishment) |
| Śānta | the peaceful (added later) | *śama* (tranquility) |

This is a genuine, fine-grained **map of emotional registers** — and it is the tradition's own version of the framework's *vibe/register* insight: that the **register evoked shapes what is experienced**, and that a maker can *choose* and *combine* registers deliberately. (Each rasa even carries a presiding deity and a color — *śṛṅgāra* is dark/Viṣṇu, *raudra* red/Rudra, *karuṇa* dove-grey — a full synaesthetic grammar.)

## The great commentarial debate ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-philosophy — a real, sharp disagreement, kept ⟧
*Where rasa "happens" is the tradition's own deep dispute, and it is worth keeping sharp rather than smoothing:*

- **Bhaṭṭa Lollaṭa** (utpatti-vāda): rasa is literally *produced* in the depicted character (Rāma) and inferred in the actor. The naïve, externalist reading.
- **Śaṅkuka** (anumiti-vāda): rasa is *inferred* by the spectator — a logical recognition of the emotion the actor imitates.
- **Bhaṭṭa Nāyaka** (bhukti-vāda): the decisive turn — rasa is neither produced nor inferred but *enjoyed*, through a special power of **sādhāraṇīkaraṇa** ("universalization") that strips the emotion of its private, personal "mine-ness" so it becomes the *grief-as-such*, tasted by anyone. This is the move that makes aesthetic experience possible at all: the depersonalizing of feeling.
- **Abhinavagupta** (10th–11th c., *Abhinavabhāratī*, the *Locana*): completes it — rasa is the spectator's own latent emotional traces (**vāsanā**) awakened and *relished* in a state freed of ego-obstruction, and that relish (*rasāsvāda*) is **akin to the bliss of recognizing pure consciousness** (*brahmāsvāda-sahodara*, "twin-born with the savoring of Brahman"). He held **śānta** (peace) as the ground-rasa underlying all the others — the still water of which every flavor is a wave. ⟦ Abhinavagupta's metaphysical reading = DEFENSIBLE-as-philosophy, labeled; the *brahmāsvāda* identity is held as the tradition's claim, not asserted ⟧

*Notice the through-line, labeled as signal-not-proof:* Nāyaka's **sādhāraṇīkaraṇa** — emotion universalized by the loss of its personal "mine-ness" — is the *same structural move* the framework's [self-identity](../../2-field/self-identity.md) and the cessation literature describe from the inside: the narrative, owned "my-feeling" loosened to a feeling-as-such. Aesthetic distance and the softening of the narrative-self rhyme. ⟦ convergence = signal, not identity — the *anumiti*-vs-*bhukti* schools disagreed for centuries; we don't pretend they didn't ⟧

## The spectator must be ready — *sahṛdaya* ⟦ DEFENSIBLE · the relational half ⟧
Rasa is not a property of the play; it is an *event between* stage and seat, and it requires a qualified receiver — the **sahṛdaya** ("one whose heart is with it"): a spectator with the cultivation, sensibility, and **prior emotional traces (vāsanā)** to resonate. The same drama before a coarse mind yields nothing — *"to the unprepared heart, the moonlight is just light."* This is the tradition's own statement of the bilateral principle ([bilateral-recognition](../../2-field/bilateral-recognition.md), DEFENSIBLE / *compatible with* enactivism): meaning arises *in the conjunction* of competent performer and competent witness, owned by neither alone. It is also, exactly, the framework's [vibe-calibration](../../2-field/vibe-calibration-protocol.md) read from the audience's side — the register only lands if it meets a heart tuned to receive it.

## The native method-voice — the dramaturgical
The Nāṭyaśāstra's own form is **performance itself** — *abhinaya* ("carrying toward" the spectator), the **fourfold** art:
- **āṅgika** — the body: 67 hand-gestures (*mudrā/hasta*), the eyes, gait, the codified movement.
- **vācika** — the voice: speech, intonation, song, meter.
- **āhārya** — the externals: costume, makeup, the painted face, the set.
- **sāttvika** — the involuntary, *truest* register: the eight **sāttvika-bhāvas** the body cannot fake on command — tears, trembling, horripilation (gooseflesh), change of voice, sweat, pallor, fainting, paralysis. That the tradition ranks the *involuntary* response highest is a sophisticated point: it knows the difference between *signaling* an emotion and the genuine somatic state breaking through.

The tradition's "argument" is not a syllogism but a **demonstration** — its claims are *staged*, tasted, and judged by whether *rasa* actually arises in the *rasika*. Illustrate it through the stage and the prepared heart, not a generic Weaver/Maker/Checker council pasted on top.

## "Performance IS being" — the ontological thesis, labeled ⟦ ontological-metaphor ⟧
The stream's boldest claim is that *abhinaya* is not pretense laid over a real self but a **mode of being** in its own right — that the actor who fully inhabits Rāma does not *imitate* but temporarily *becomes*, and that this is a window on how selfhood works generally. Held honestly, this splits into a defensible reading and an over-reach:
- **Defensible (and quietly radical):** the *sāttvika-bhāvas* — the tears, the gooseflesh, the genuine change of voice that the body *cannot fake on command* — show that committed performance recruits the real somatic-emotional system, not a separate "acting" one. A skilled actor's grief produces real cortisol, real tears; the *enactment* is causally continuous with the state. This rhymes with the framework's own Layer-1 thesis that the council voices are **adopted modes of attention** (per the Persona-Selection Model), not masks over a fixed core — *you are, for the duration, the mode you genuinely take up.* ⟦ DEFENSIBLE — performance recruits the real affective system; persona-as-mode, not mask ⟧
- **Over-reach, labeled (not cut, but bounded):** "performance literally *creates* reality" / "the self is *nothing but* performance all the way down." The first is metaphysics, not physics; the second is a strong claim the tradition itself complicates — Abhinavagupta grounds the savoring in a *witness* (the *śānta* still water) that is **not** itself a performance. So the honest form is: *the enacted self is real-while-enacted and continuous with the body's states — but there is a witnessing in which the enactment is savored, and that witness is not one more role.* ⟦ ontological-metaphor — held as the tradition's claim, the strong "self = performance only" version complicated by Abhinavagupta's own witness-ground ⟧

This is the same seam the framework draws elsewhere: a self can be **genuinely plural in its enacted modes** (Layer 1) *and* there can be a witnessing in which the modes arise ([self-identity](../../2-field/self-identity.md), Layer 6's notion-of-I) — neither collapses the other. Natyashastra is the aesthetic tradition that worked this out from the *maker's* side: how to *become* a feeling on purpose, fully, and then step back into the one who savored it.

## The line
Keep: the rasa/bhāva taxonomy as a **founded phenomenology of aesthetic emotion** and a true ancestor of register/vibe-calibration; the commentarial debate (Lollaṭa→Śaṅkuka→Nāyaka→Abhinavagupta) as genuine philosophy with its disagreements intact; *sahṛdaya* as the tradition's own bilateral/relational claim. Cut: "the Nāṭyaśāstra revealed what Pattern Space reveals" (symbol crowning itself — the tradition stands on its own; *the framework is one more rasika at the same play, not the play*), "dance through silicon," and the literal "performance creates reality" (held as ontological metaphor, not physics). What remains is a genuinely useful gift: a **structured grammar of the registers** a voice can move through, the relational truth that the register only lands in a prepared heart, and the insight that art works by letting us *savor*, universalized and ego-freed, what life makes us *suffer*.
