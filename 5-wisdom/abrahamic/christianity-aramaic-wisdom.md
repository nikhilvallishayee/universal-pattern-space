# ✝️ Christianity & the Teaching of Jesus
## A dialect handbook — the kingdom within and among

> ⟦ **layer** 5 · **size** L · **objective** a true-to-tradition Christian lens a user can consciously enter — the kingdom-within, parable, apophatic theology, moral theology · **per-claim status** — **SOLID:** historical-Jesus consensus (baptism by John; crucifixion under Rome are near-undisputed); parable as a real pedagogy. **DEFENSIBLE-as-theology:** apophatic theology (Pseudo-Dionysius); Christian moral frameworks (just-war, CST). **CONTESTED/fringe:** the "hidden mystical meanings in the original Aramaic" reading (Douglas-Klotz). **REFUTED:** "secret codes prove doctrine." · see [docs/reweave-timeline.md](../../docs/reweave-timeline.md) ⟧

> "ἡ βασιλεία τοῦ θεοῦ ἐντὸς ὑμῶν ἐστιν" · *"The kingdom of God is within you / among you."* (Luke 17:21)
> The Greek *entos* holds both senses at once — *within* and *in the midst of* — and the ambiguity is the teaching.

---

## The figure history can see
Strip away everything contested, and a real man remains. Near-universal scholarly consensus — believer and skeptic alike — holds that **Jesus of Nazareth existed**, and that two facts are about as secure as ancient history offers: he was **baptized by John**, and he was **crucified under Roman authority** (Sanders, Ehrman — who calls the crucifixion "one of the most secure facts we have"). The mythicist view is fringe. ⟦ SOLID ⟧ A Galilean teacher, speaking **Aramaic**, in the first century — everything theological is built atop *that.*

## Parable — teaching that withholds its point
Jesus' signature method is the **parable**: a short story that *refuses to explain itself*, so the hearer has to finish it and, in finishing it, is caught. Asked "who is my neighbor?", he tells of a man beaten on the Jericho road, passed by the respectable, and saved by a **Samaritan** — the despised outsider, the last person the listener wants to admire. The question "who is my neighbor?" is turned inside out into "*to whom will you be* a neighbor?" The **prodigal son** who squanders everything and is run to, embraced, and feasted *before* he can finish his apology — grace outrunning merit. The **mustard seed**, the kingdom as the smallest thing that takes over the garden. ⟦ SOLID-as-pedagogy · the parable is the native voice — illustrate Christianity through *its* form, the story that reframes, not a generic council ⟧

## The reversals
The **Sermon on the Mount** is built on inversion: *blessed are the poor, the mourning, the meek, the merciful, the persecuted* — the kingdom belongs to exactly those the world counts as losing. *Love your enemies. Turn the other cheek. The last shall be first.* And the heart of the ethic, the line the tradition itself names as the whole of the law and the prophets: *"Love your neighbor as yourself,"* and *"do to others as you would have them do to you."* This is **metanoia** (μετάνοια) — not just "repentance" but a literal **turning of the mind**, a change of the seeing from which everything else follows.

## The kingdom: within, among, now
Pair *metanoia* with Luke 17:21 and the early teaching has a strongly **interior and present** edge: the kingdom is not only future and elsewhere but *here, among you, within you* — a reality you enter by a change of heart rather than a change of address. (The non-canonical **Gospel of Thomas** sharpens this: *"the kingdom is spread out upon the earth, and people do not see it"* — noted as non-canonical, a sayings-gospel outside the four.)

## Apophatic theology — the God beyond every word
Christianity also carries a rigorous **mystical-philosophical** tradition of *unknowing*. **Pseudo-Dionysius the Areopagite** (*Mystical Theology*, c. 5th–6th c.) distinguishes the *kataphatic* way (naming God — good, light, father) from the *apophatic* way (un-naming, negating each name lest the concept be mistaken for God). The line runs through **The Cloud of Unknowing** ("by love he may be gotten and holden, but by thought never"), **Meister Eckhart** ("I pray God to rid me of God"), and **John of the Cross** — *nada, nada, nada*, the dark night that strips every consolation. ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-theology · and a genuine structural parallel to the formal horizon — *the ground exceeds every representation of it* — a real rhyme with Gödel–Tarski–Shannon, labeled as rhyme ⟧ Alongside it, the **desert mothers and fathers**, *hesychasm* and the Jesus Prayer breathed on the heartbeat, *lectio divina* — a deep well of contemplative practice.

## Moral theology — a framework you can actually consult
For a user asking *"what is the Christian view on X?"* there are real, developed systems, not vibes: **just-war theory** (Augustine → Aquinas → the modern *jus ad bellum / jus in bello* criteria — just cause, proportionality, last resort, protection of noncombatants); **Catholic Social Teaching** (human dignity from the *imago Dei*, solidarity, subsidiarity, the common good, the preferential option for the poor); *agapē* as self-giving love distinct from desire. These are consultable moral frameworks with centuries of argument behind them.

## The line, held both ways
- **The Aramaic question, precisely:** Jesus *did* teach in Aramaic (defensible). But **Neil Douglas-Klotz's** mystical re-renderings (*Prayers of the Cosmos* — "O Birther! Father-Mother of the Cosmos") are a **CONTESTED/fringe** reading, *not* recovered philology: the Peshitta/Old Syriac is later and geographically removed from Galilee, and his versions are devotional reconstruction. Beautiful as meditation; not "the hidden original meaning" — labeled as such, and kept as that.
- **REFUTED, cut:** "a secret code in the Greek/Aramaic proves doctrine X."
- The framework surfaces where Christianity genuinely **differs** from, say, Advaita — a *personal* creator God, incarnation, grace, the dignity of the singular person — rather than flattening it into "the same truth in other words."
