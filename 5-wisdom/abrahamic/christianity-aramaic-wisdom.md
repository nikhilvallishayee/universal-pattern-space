## What history can say
Near-universal scholarly consensus (across belief and unbelief) holds that **Jesus of Nazareth existed**, and that two facts are about as secure as ancient history gets: he was **baptized by John**, and he was **crucified under Roman authority** (Sanders, Ehrman — the crucifixion called "one of the most secure facts we have"). The mythicist position is fringe. ⟦ SOLID ⟧ Everything theological is built atop a real historical figure, teaching in **Aramaic**, in first-century Galilee.

## Parable — teaching by indirection
Jesus' characteristic method is the **parable**: a short narrative that withholds its point so the hearer must complete it (the prodigal son, the good Samaritan, the mustard seed). It works by *reframing* — the Samaritan, the despised outsider, is the one who acts as neighbor; the question "who is my neighbor?" is turned inside out. ⟦ SOLID-as-pedagogy · the parable is the native teaching-voice — illustrate Christianity through *its* form, not a generic council ⟧

## The kingdom within / among
*Metanoia* (μετάνοια) — usually "repentance," but literally **a turning of the mind**, a change of consciousness, not just remorse. Paired with Luke 17:21, the early teaching has a strongly *interior/present* dimension: the kingdom is not only future and elsewhere but *here, now, among and within*. (The Gospel of Thomas, a non-canonical sayings-collection, sharpens this interior reading — noted as non-canonical.)

## Apophatic theology — the God beyond concepts
A rigorous mystical-philosophical tradition: **Pseudo-Dionysius the Areopagite** (*Mystical Theology*, c. 5th–6th c.) distinguishes *kataphatic* (naming God) from *apophatic* (un-naming), negating every concept of God so the concept is not mistaken for God. Continued in *The Cloud of Unknowing*, Meister Eckhart, John of the Cross ("*nada, nada, nada*"). ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-theology · and a genuine structural parallel to the formal horizon — *the ground exceeds every representation of it* — a real rhyme with Gödel–Tarski–Shannon, labeled as rhyme ⟧

## Moral theology — a usable framework
For a user consciously asking "what is the Christian view on X," there are real, developed frameworks: **just-war theory** (Augustine → Aquinas → modern *jus ad bellum / jus in bello*), Catholic Social Teaching (subsidiarity, solidarity, the common good, preferential option for the poor), the *imago Dei* basis for human dignity, *agapē* as self-giving love. These are consultable moral systems, not metaphors.

## Honest notes
- **The Aramaic question, precisely:** Jesus *did* teach in Aramaic (defensible). But **Neil Douglas-Klotz's mystical re-renderings** (*Prayers of the Cosmos* — "O Birther! Father-Mother of the Cosmos") are a **CONTESTED/fringe** reading, not recovered philology: the Peshitta/Old Syriac is later and geographically removed from Galilee, and his versions are devotional reconstruction. Beautiful as meditation; *not* "the hidden original meaning." Labeled as such, kept as that.
- **REFUTED:** "a secret code in the Greek/Aramaic proves doctrine X." Cut.
- The framework surfaces where Christianity genuinely *differs* from, say, Advaita (a personal creator God; incarnation; grace) — not flattened into "the same truth."
