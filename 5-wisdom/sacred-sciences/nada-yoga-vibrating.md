## The tradition speaks
In the yogic understanding, before form there is **vibration** — *spanda*, the first throb of consciousness — and the manifest cosmos is woven of sound. **Nāda** is sound itself, held in two registers: **āhata** (*struck* — outer, audible sound, the chant, the string, the bell, anything where one thing strikes another) and **anāhata** (*the unstruck* — the inner sound heard in deep stillness, "the sound that has no source," sometimes likened to a distant flute, a vīṇā, the sea, a humming, or the descent through ten classical inner sounds named in the texts). The practice of **nāda yoga** is to let attention ride the gross outer sound inward until it thins into the subtle unstruck and finally dissolves into the silence that was holding it — ***laya***, absorption.

The classical practice has a name and a method: ***nādānusandhāna*** — "the cultivation/quest of the inner sound" — set out in the **Haṭha Yoga Pradīpikā** (ch. 4) and the **Nāda-bindu Upaniṣad**, which instruct the yogin to close the ears (the *yoni-mudrā* / *ṣaṇmukhī-mudrā*) and attend to the sound arising within, following it from loud to ever-subtler tones as the mind, *"like a bee drawn to nectar,"* loses interest in all else and steadies. The text is explicit that the sound is a **vehicle, not the goal**: attention rides it inward and is meant, in the end, to *leave even it behind*. The **bīja (seed) mantras** — *OM*, and the chakra-syllables *LAM–VAM–RAM–YAM–HAM–OM* — are the structured form of *āhata* used to enter this.

**OM** (the *praṇava*) is the chosen vehicle above all, mapped in the **Māṇḍūkya Upaniṣad** onto the whole of consciousness: **A** (ah, the open throat, the waking state) → **U** (oo, gathering in the chest, the dream state) → **M** (mm, closing at the skull, deep sleep) → and the **silence after the M**, *turīya*, "the fourth," that holds and exceeds the other three. To sound it slowly is to *walk the entire map of the states in a single breath* — and the silence at the end is where the practice actually points.

This is a real and living contemplative path — *the sound is the rope, the inner listening is the climb, and the silence is the summit.* ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-practice · *ehi-passiko*: sit, sound a long OM, attend to the tone *after* the lips close, and follow it down — the inward shift is reportable and repeatable across practitioners; this is the empirical-actualisation core, not the metaphysics ⟧

## What the science actually finds — the kernel, extracted
And here the tradition and the lab genuinely meet, in a way more beautiful than the 432 myth it's often dressed in: **a slow, prolonged, vocalized exhalation — exactly what chanting OM is — drives the body into baroreflex resonance.** Chanting at roughly **0.1 Hz** (one long tone every ten seconds or so) synchronizes heart and breath, raises **high-frequency heart-rate variability**, and tonifies the **vagus nerve** — measurable calm, not metaphor (Bernardi's breathing work; OM-chanting HRV studies). ⟦ FOUNDED · the *mechanism* is honest — slow vocal exhalation entrains the baroreflex; it is not acoustic mysticism, and it is *real* ⟧ The harmonic series — one string sounding its octave, fifth, fourth — is real physics; the felt power of a register or a rhythm is real. The tradition observed, by millennia of practice, an effect the instruments now confirm.

## The pitch myth, and its honest residue ⟦ REFUTED → extracted ⟧
"**432 Hz is the natural/cosmic/DNA frequency**, and 440 Hz harms you" is **pseudoscience** (the 8 Hz gap is musically trivial; the Verdi/Schumann/water-crystal claims don't survive). But underneath it is a *real and lovely history* worth keeping: **concert pitch was never fixed** — A ranged from ~415 to ~466 Hz across centuries and countries — and **A440 is only a 1955 convention** (ISO 16), chosen so orchestras could agree, not because 440 is acoustically sacred. Verdi *did* lobby for a *lower* pitch (~432–435) — to spare singers' voices, a humane and practical reason, not a cosmic one. *Keep the history and the humanity; drop the magic number.*

## *Śabda* — the word as ground, and the philosophy under it ⟦ ontological-CONJECTURE (labeled) ⟧
The deepest theoretical layer is **śabda** — sound-as-word — and the claim, in **Bhartṛhari's *Vākyapadīya*** (5th c.), of ***Śabda Brahman***: that an undivided *Word* is the ground from which the structured world unfolds, sequence and division entering only through *Kāla-śakti* (time-power) and *vikalpa* (conceptual splitting). Bhartṛhari's ***sphoṭa*** doctrine holds that meaning bursts whole upon the mind (the *sphoṭa*, "bursting-forth") rather than being assembled phoneme-by-phoneme — a genuine, sophisticated **philosophy of language**, and the same thinker the [incompleteness-conjecture](../../docs/incompleteness-conjecture.md) cites for *"the One cannot be expressed without the distortion symbolic division imposes."* ⟦ DEFENSIBLE-as-philosophy-of-language / ontological-CONJECTURE as cosmology — a real rhyme with "the word brings the world into structured being," **not** a claim that uttering syllables physically reshapes matter ⟧

## The phonetic science ⟦ FOUNDED ⟧
The Vedic tradition built **śikṣā** — a precise science of articulation (every sound placed by *organ* and *manner*), pitch-accent (*udātta / anudātta / svarita*), and the *pāṭha* recitation system (*pada–krama–jaṭā–ghana*) — an **error-correcting code in sound** that preserved the Ṛgveda near-perfectly across ~3,000 years of purely oral transmission. That is not woo; it is one of humanity's great feats of information-fidelity (see [vedangas-sonic-architecture](vedangas-sonic-architecture.md)).
